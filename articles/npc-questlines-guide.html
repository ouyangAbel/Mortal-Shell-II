NPC Questlines Overview — Why They Matter

Mortal Shell II's NPC questlines are easy to overlook but carry enormous rewards. Unlike the first game's minimal NPC interaction, the sequel weaves eight distinct characters through interconnected story threads, each gated behind specific progress triggers, dialogue choices, and item turn-ins. Miss a single step and you lose access to unique Shells, weapon variants, consumable recipes, lore-critical cutscenes — and one of the game's two possible endings.

This guide covers every NPC in the order they naturally appear during a standard playthrough, with explicit trigger conditions, missable windows, and all possible outcomes. If you are pursuing the trophy for all NPC questlines, this is your definitive roadmap. For a broader overview of game mechanics, see our Beginner's Guide.

⚠️ Critical Warning: Two NPCs — Vlas and Baghead — become permanently unavailable if you defeat Imrod the Unrepentant before completing their quest steps. If you want all NPC rewards in a single playthrough, complete Vlas and Baghead's questlines before entering the Seat of Infinity.

The Old Prisoner — Hub NPC & Cycle Trigger

Location: Fallgrim Tower (central hub, ground floor)

First Encounter: Immediately after acquiring your first Shell from the tutorial area. The Old Prisoner is chained to the central pillar of Fallgrim Tower — impossible to miss.

Quest Steps

Step 1 — First Conversation: Exhaust all dialogue. The Old Prisoner explains the nature of Shells, the cycle of death, and hints at the Dark Father. He gifts you the Faded Talisman (key item — tracks boss kills).

Step 2 — After Defeating Your First Major Boss: Return to the Old Prisoner after killing any one of the three major bosses (Crucix, Tarsus, or Imrod). He now speaks of the "Shattered Covenant" and rewards you with 1 Glimpse of Annihilation — a rare currency used for Shell ability unlocks.

Step 3 — After Two Bosses Defeated: He reveals fragments of his past. A new dialogue option appears: "Show me the truth." Selecting it triggers a brief vision cutscene. He gifts Echoes of the Fallen (consumable — reveals hidden wall locations for 60 seconds).

Step 4 — After All Three Major Bosses: The Old Prisoner's chains break. He stands for the first time. Dialogue now includes the option to "begin anew," which transitions you into New Game Plus. Do not select this unless you are ready — the transition is irreversible. If you choose "not yet," you can return later. See our NG+ Guide for what carries over.

Thestus the Merchant — Upgrade Path & Inventory

Location: Fallgrim Tower (upper floor, adjacent to the weapon upgrade anvil)

First Encounter: Available from the start, behind a wooden counter on the upper floor of Fallgrim Tower.

Quest Steps

Step 1 — Basic Inventory: Initially sells Quenching Acid (weapon upgrade material), Roasted Rat (basic healing), and Simple Tools (upgrade the anvil). Purchase at least one of each to advance his dialogue.

Step 2 — Spend 5,000+ Tar: After cumulative spending exceeds 5,000 Tar, Thestus offers a "special arrangement." He unlocks the Intermediate Inventory tier, which includes Ballistazooka Bolts, Enhanced Quenching Acid (+2 upgrade tier), and Mortal Tokens (temporary damage buff).

Step 3 — Bring a Giant Kin Flesh: Dropped by the Giant Kin enemy in Fallgrim Outskirts (the massive figure wielding a tree trunk). This is a guaranteed drop on first kill. Deliver it to Thestus and he unlocks his Advanced Inventory: Superior Quenching Acid (+4), Ornate Mask (respec item — redistributes Shell abilities), and the Merchant's Pendant (passive — 15% discount on all future purchases).

Pro Tip: The Ornate Mask is one of the most valuable items in the game. It lets you reclaim all spent Tar and Glimpses from a Shell and redistribute them — perfect for respecing before a tough boss. Always keep at least one in your inventory. It costs 5,000 Tar each, but with the Merchant's Pendant discount, that drops to 4,250 Tar.

Vlas the Lutenist — Consumable Recipes Quest

Location: Fallgrim Outskirts (small campfire northwest of the first Grisha encounter)

First Encounter: You hear lute music before seeing him. Follow the sound to a small campfire off the main path. Vlas is sitting on a log, strumming.

⚠️ Missable Cutoff: Defeating Imrod the Unrepentant despawns Vlas permanently. Complete his questline before entering the Seat of Infinity.

Quest Steps

Step 1 — Listen to His Song: Stand near Vlas for 30 seconds without interacting. He finishes his song and remarks that you are "a patient one." He gifts the Roasted Rat Recipe — crafting your own Roasted Rats now costs half the Tar of purchasing them.

Step 2 — Bring Him a Tarnished Lute String: Found in the Crypt of Martyrs, second level, inside a sarcophagus in the eastern alcove (past the Wraith hallway). When delivered, Vlas tunes his lute and rewards you with the Bolstering Brew Recipe (temporary max HP increase) and Spiteful Shard Recipe (throwable — deals 80 damage, scales with Resolve).

Step 3 — Bring Him the Song of the Depths: An item dropped by The Unchained (guaranteed drop). Vlas plays a haunting melody, reveals his true identity as a former member of the Fallgrim Chorus, and gifts his final reward: the Echoing Lute (consumable tool — when played near hidden walls, they shimmer visible for 10 seconds. Unlimited uses, 60-second cooldown). This item is essential for finding all Secret Areas & Hidden Bosses.

Baghead the Brigand — Shell Evolution Quest

Location: Fallgrim Marsh (southern path, near the poison pool with two Leapers)

First Encounter: A friendly Brigand wearing a burlap sack over his head, standing apart from hostile Brigands. He does not attack. Speak to him and he offers to "trade favors."

⚠️ Missable Cutoff: Like Vlas, Baghead despawns after defeating Imrod. Complete his quest before Seat of Infinity.

Quest Steps

Step 1 — Accept His Offer: He asks you to bring the Head of a Halberdier Brigand (drops from the armored Brigand variant patrolling the road east of Fallgrim Tower). In exchange, he teaches you the Brigand's Resilience ability — a Shell ability that reduces stagger by 25% when below 50% HP.

Step 2 — Bring Him 3 Tar Sprites: Small glowing creatures found throughout Fallgrim at night. Collect three by approaching and pressing interact (they flee if you attack). Baghead consumes them and evolves — his sack tears slightly, revealing one glowing eye. He now sells Brigand's Tar (500 Tar each — when consumed, grants +30% damage for 60 seconds) and gifts the Torn Sack (helmet item — +5% item discovery).

Step 3 — Bring the Obsidian Shard: Found in the Shifting Archives, hidden behind a breakable wall on the upper floor (use Echoing Lute from Vlas's quest to locate it). Baghead removes his sack entirely, revealing a twisted but recognizable human face. He thanks you, calls you "kin," and permanently moves to Fallgrim Tower where he offers Shell Fusion: combine two Shell abilities from different Shells into a single custom ability slot. This is one of the most powerful systems in the game. See our Best Builds guide for optimal Shell Fusion combinations.

Corrupted Sester — Hidden NPC & Secret Ending

Location: Deep within the Crypt of Martyrs, behind an illusory wall in the room with four sarcophagi (post-Tarsus area).

First Encounter: After defeating Tarsus the First Martyr, return to the room with four sarcophagi. The wall on the far right is illusory — roll through it. Inside, a Sester Genessa stands with blackened veins and glowing red eyes. She does not offer the usual level-up menu.

Prerequisite: You must have at least 50 Glimpses in your inventory. She will not speak to you otherwise.

Quest Steps

Step 1 — Offer 30 Glimpses: Select "Offer Glimpses" from her dialogue menu. She consumes 30 Glimpses and bestows the Mark of the Corrupted (permanent status — all enemies deal 15% more damage to you, but you deal 25% more damage to them. Stackable with enchantments). This cannot be removed for the remainder of the playthrough.

Step 2 — Return After Defeating Two More Bosses: After defeating any two additional bosses (Hadern, Corvid, or The Unchained all count), return to her. She offers a second transaction: 50 Glimpses in exchange for the Corrupted Seed (key item).

Step 3 — Confront the Dark Father with the Corrupted Seed: During the final boss fight against The Dark Father, if the Corrupted Seed is in your inventory, a new interaction prompt appears during his Phase 2 transition. Use the seed to trigger the Corrupted Ending — an alternate final cutscene and a unique boss soul reward: Father's Remnant (used to craft the Abyssal Siphon weapon variant). For boss strategies, see our Dark Father guide.

⚠️ Warning: The Mark of the Corrupted's +15% incoming damage penalty is significant. In NG+, this can make certain boss attacks one-shot kills. If you are struggling, complete the Corrupted Sester questline on a fresh NG cycle instead. The Corrupted Seed and Mark of the Corrupted do not carry into NG+.

Twin-Faced Merchant — Rare Inventory & Currency

Location: Shifting Archives, central atrium (appears after you have collected at least one Archive Key)

First Encounter: A hunched figure wearing a two-faced mask — one side smiling, one side weeping. She appears in the central atrium of the Shifting Archives only after you pick up your first Archive Key. Her location shifts between visits.

Quest Steps

Step 1 — Purchase Using Glimpses: Her currency is exclusively Glimpses, not Tar. Buy the Mask Fragment (Smile) for 15 Glimpses. This unlocks her "Smile" inventory: consumables that buff you (Golden Sap — temporary invincibility on next hit, 8 Glimpses).

Step 2 — Purchase the Mask Fragment (Weep): Spend 25 Glimpses. Her "Weep" inventory unlocks: consumables that debuff enemies (Withering Incense — reduces boss damage output by 20% for 90 seconds, 10 Glimpses).

Step 3 — Purchase Both Fragments and Return After Dark Father: If you own both Mask Fragments and return after defeating The Dark Father (but before starting NG+), she fuses them into the Complete Twin Mask (equippable accessory — grants +10% Glimpse acquisition and reveals all enemy HP bars at all times). The Complete Twin Mask is one of only three accessories that carry into NG+.

Gorf the Toad — Musical Offering Quest

Location: Fallgrim (large swamp pond east of Fallgrim Tower, near the fallen tree bridge)

First Encounter: A massive toad with a distinctly patterned back sits on a rock in the middle of a pond. He is a returning character from the original Mortal Shell, and fans will recognize his musical affinity immediately.

Quest Steps

Step 1 — Play Any Instrument Near Him: Equip the Lute (found in Fallgrim Tower, on a crate near Thestus) or the Echoing Lute (from Vlas's questline) and play it while standing near Gorf. He croaks in rhythm. After a full cycle of the song, he spits out an item: Gorf's Mucus (consumable — when applied to a weapon, adds poison buildup for 120 seconds).

Step 2 — Feed Him a Roasted Rat: Drop a Roasted Rat in front of him (use the "Leave Item" function from inventory). He eats it and belches out 1 Glimpse of Wisdom (worth 2,500 Tar at any Sester). Repeatable up to 5 times per playthrough.

Step 3 — After Vlas's Questline Completion: If Vlas has been fully completed (Echoing Lute acquired), play the Echoing Lute near Gorf. A unique duet cutscene plays — Gorf and the lute harmonize. Afterward, Gorf permanently spawns in Fallgrim Tower's ground floor near the Old Prisoner. He now produces 1 Gorf's Mucus every time you rest at a Sester (up to a maximum of 3 in inventory).

Pro Tip: Gorf's Mucus poison buildup stacks with the Corvid boss weapon's innate poison effect. Combined, you can trigger poison on bosses in 3-4 hits instead of the usual 8-10. Against Corvid herself, this is particularly effective since poison negates her HP regeneration mechanic.

Gressil the Scholar — Lore Fragments Quest

Location: Temple Grounds (library annex, accessible after defeating Hadern)

First Encounter: An elderly man hunched over a desk covered in scrolls, in a small room off the Temple Grounds main hall. He only appears after Hadern is defeated.

Quest Steps

Step 1 — Bring Him a Lore Fragment: Lore Fragments are glowing parchment pieces scattered across all zones. The easiest is in Fallgrim Tower: check behind the weapon upgrade anvil for Fragment I (The Founding). Gressil translates it and rewards you with 500 Tar and a lore entry in your journal.

Step 2 — Bring 5 Lore Fragments: After collecting and delivering five fragments, Gressil reveals the "unified narrative" — all delivered fragments weave into a single cohesive backstory. He gifts Scholar's Spectacles (accessory — highlights interactable objects within 10 meters with a faint gold glow).

Step 3 — Bring All 12 Lore Fragments: All fragments must be collected and delivered. Their locations:

  • Fragments I-IV: Fallgrim (Tower, Outskirts, Marsh, Cave)
  • Fragments V-VII: Temple Grounds (Annex, Sanctum, Catacombs)
  • Fragments VIII-IX: Crypt of Martyrs (Lower level, Ossuary)
  • Fragments X-XI: Seat of Infinity (Throne Room, Observatory)
  • Fragment XII: Shifting Archives (behind breakable wall, revealed by Echoing Lute)

Upon delivering all 12, Gressil reveals the true nature of the Dark Father in a 3-minute cutscene. He then collapses — his body dissolves into light, leaving behind the Chronometer of Regret (accessory — allows one free death per boss fight; when you die, time rewinds 5 seconds and you revive with 30% HP. 10-minute cooldown). This accessory carries into NG+ and is arguably the single strongest item in the game for boss progression.

Missable Quest Triggers — What to Watch For

Mortal Shell II is unforgiving with NPC questline fail states. Here are the exact conditions that lock you out of content:

  • Imrod Defeated: Vlas and Baghead permanently despawn. Complete both questlines fully before entering Seat of Infinity.
  • Dark Father Defeated (without Corrupted Seed): Locks out the Corrupted Ending. You must do the Corrupted Sester questline before the final boss.
  • Starting NG+ Before Completing Twin-Faced Merchant: The Complete Twin Mask fusion can only happen post-Dark Father but pre-NG+. If you transition to NG+ immediately, you lose the mask permanently for that cycle.
  • Killing Gorf: Yes, you can attack Gorf. He has 500 HP and drops nothing of value. This permanently removes him from all future playthroughs across all save files — a cruel, permanent consequence. Don't do it.
  • Not Having 50 Glimpses Before Finding Corrupted Sester: She will not speak to you. You can return later, but if you defeat The Dark Father first, she despawns.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I complete all NPC questlines in a single playthrough?

Yes, absolutely. The critical ordering is: complete Vlas and Baghead before Imrod, complete Corrupted Sester before The Dark Father, and do not transition to NG+ until you fuse the Complete Twin Mask. Following the walkthrough order in this guide ensures nothing is missed.

Do NPC questlines carry into New Game Plus?

No. All NPC questlines reset completely on NG+. Your rewards — items, abilities, and accessories — carry over (with the exception of the Mark of the Corrupted and the Corrupted Seed, which are cycle-specific). You can replay questlines in NG+ for duplicate rewards, but some items (like the Chronometer of Regret) are limited to one per character regardless of cycle.

What happens if Vlas despawns before I finish his quest?

He is gone for that playthrough. You lose access to the Echoing Lute (and by extension, 3 hidden walls and 2 secret boss encounters). The Tarnished Lute String and Song of the Depths remain in your inventory and serve no purpose until your next NG cycle. Always prioritize Vlas before entering Seat of Infinity.

Is the Corrupted Ending required for any trophies?

Yes. The "Twice-Fallen" trophy requires witnessing both the standard ending and the Corrupted Ending. This means a minimum of two playthroughs (or one playthrough plus reloading a backup save before The Dark Father). The Abyssal Siphon weapon acquired from the Corrupted Ending also counts toward the "Armory Complete" trophy for collecting all weapons.

Can I respec Shell Fusion choices from Baghead?

Yes, but it is expensive. You can fuse new ability combinations at any time by speaking to Baghead in Fallgrim Tower (after completing his quest). Each fusion costs 5 Glimpses, and the previous fusion is overwritten — you do not get a refund. Plan your Shell Fusion combinations carefully, and consult our Best Builds guide before committing.

Do I need to complete Gressil's quest for the Chronometer of Regret before NG+?

No, but it is highly recommended. Finding all 12 Lore Fragments is time-consuming, and the Chronometer of Regret is arguably the most powerful accessory in the game — especially for NG+ cycles where boss damage scaling is brutal. If you collect all fragments in NG and carry the Chronometer into NG+, you have a massive survivability advantage from the start. Fragments respawn in every cycle, so you cannot collect them in NG and "save" them for NG+ — you must find them again.
