Mortal Shell II Speedrun Route Guide: Any% Glitchless & Boss Rush Strategies

Optimal Route · Split Breakdown · Shell & Weapon Picks · Boss Skips · Sub-2-Hour Path

Whether you are chasing a personal best or aiming for the top of the Mortal Shell II speedrun leaderboard, having a clean route is everything. This guide breaks down the optimal any% glitchless path from character select to credits, covering every zone transition, boss encounter, and time-saving trick that separates a 3-hour casual clear from a sub-2-hour speedrun. We also cover the Boss Rush category and explain when to use it instead. If you are new to the game entirely, start with our Beginner's Guide first; if you have beaten it once and want to go fast, read on.

Optimal Shell & Weapon Choice

Your Shell and weapon choice in a speedrun is not about personal preference — it is about DPS-per-second-of-animation and movement speed. Two loadouts dominate the leaderboard.

Loadout A: Tiel Shell + Hallowed Sword (Meta Pick)

Tiel is the fastest Shell with the longest sprint and shortest Harden cooldown (~4.5 seconds). The Hallowed Sword upgrades along the natural route and reaches +3 by mid-game without detours. Tiel's low HP is irrelevant because speedrunners rarely get hit, and its high stamina pool supports extended punish windows. ~78% of top-10 leaderboard runs use this combination.

Why Hallowed Sword over other weapons: It requires zero detours to acquire (you start with it), upgrade materials are on-route, and its light attack combo is the fastest 3-hit string in the game (0.9 seconds total). The heavy attack's forward lunge also covers distance, which saves seconds per fight.

Loadout B: Solomon Shell + Martyr's Blade (Consistency Pick)

Solomon offers more HP than Tiel while retaining decent speed and stamina. The Martyr's Blade provides Frost buildup, which trivializes several bosses by slowing their attack tempo. Solomon's Resolve generation is the fastest among all Shells, meaning more parry-ripostes and fewer dodge cycles. Use this if you are learning the route and need a safety net.

Loadouts to Avoid

  • Eredrim: Too slow. Movement speed penalty costs 2–4 minutes over a full run. The extra HP does not compensate.
  • Harrow: Decent survivability but mediocre damage output. Harden cooldown is the longest (~7.0 seconds), which forces more dodge cycles per boss.
  • Smoldering Mace: High damage but painfully slow attack animations. Each combo takes 1.8 seconds vs. Hallowed Sword's 0.9 seconds.

Split-by-Split Breakdown

Split 1: Prologue & Fallgrim (Target 0:06:00)

After the opening cutscene (unskippable, ~45 seconds), grab the Hallowed Sword from the weapon rack. Sprint northeast to the crypt entrance — there is a +1 upgrade material on a corpse beside the first gate. Pick it up without fighting anything. The Fallgrim Brigands are slow and easy to outrun. Upgrade at the nearest workbench before entering the boss arena.

Time save: Skip the tutorial cave's optional loot chest (adds 30 seconds for a Minor Quenching Acid you will never use). Go straight to Hadern's arena.

Split 2: Hadern (Target +2:30)

Hadern has only three attacks: a 3-hit sword combo, a delayed overhead slam, and a grab. Use the Harden-bait loop: walk forward, Harden as he begins his 3-hit combo, let all three strikes bounce off your stone body, then punish with a full light combo during his 1.5-second recovery. Repeat 3 times. The fight should last 45–60 seconds.

Warning: Hadern's grab attack has a deceptively long reach. If he raises both arms, dodge sideways — do not Harden, because the grab bypasses Harden entirely.

Split 3: Temple Grounds Traversal (Target +9:30)

This is the first long traversal section. The optimal path hugs the left wall from the entrance, loops around the central courtyard, and reaches Crucix's arena with minimal combat. You only need to kill two gatekeeper enemies that block mandatory doors. Use Harden to absorb their attacks and punish — do not engage in extended fights.

Pick up the +2 upgrade material from the chest in the small side room (10-second detour). This pushes your Hallowed Sword to +2 before Crucix, which is a massive power spike.

Split 4: Crucix the Twiceborn (Target +10:00)

Apply Fire enchantment before entering the arena. Phase 1 (human form): parry his sword thrust for a riposte, then light combo. Two parry-ripostes end Phase 1. Phase 2 (spider form): stay directly underneath Crucix. His Leaping Crush cannot target beneath him, Venom Spray shoots forward, and Spider Rush needs distance. Punish with 3-hit combos between his attacks. See the full Crucix Boss Guide for attack patterns.

Split 5: Crypt of Martyrs Traversal (Target +8:00)

The Crypt is dark and maze-like, but the speedrun path is linear: follow the left wall, drop down at the first chasm, and sprint through the Wraith hallway. Wraiths are slow — they will not catch you if you keep sprinting. Pick up the Hallowed Sword +3 material from the sarcophagus room (mandatory 15-second stop). Upgrade at the workbench before the Tarsus arena.

Split 6: Tarsus the First Martyr (Target +10:00)

Tarsus is the first significant skill check. Apply Fire enchantment (Tarsus takes ~25% bonus Fire damage). His Frost Greatsword is slow — Harden through his overhead slam, then position behind him for back-strikes. Tarsus's back takes +20% bonus damage (hidden mechanic). His Ice Burst AoE has a long recovery (~2.5 seconds); use this window for a charged heavy attack. Two to three Ice Burst punish cycles end the fight.

Split 7: Seat of Infinity Traversal (Target +12:00)

This is the longest traversal and the biggest time-loss area for new runners. The optimal path avoids the upper platforms entirely — drop into the lower chasm immediately after the entrance, hug the right wall, and take the hidden shortcut tunnel that connects directly to the Imrod arena approach. This skips approximately 80% of the zone's enemies and saves 4–5 minutes versus the intended path.

Warning: The shortcut tunnel has two lava geysers with precise timing. Run past the first during its 3-second cooldown, pause 1 second, then sprint past the second. Getting hit by lava deals massive damage and will likely end the run on Tiel Shell.

Split 8: Imrod the Unrepentant (Target +8:00)

Imrod is the fastest boss in the route for skilled runners. Apply Frost enchantment — it slows his already-slow attack tempo further, making every punish window 30% longer. The key trick: Imrod takes +30% damage from back-attacks (undocumented but confirmed). Dodge through his Flame Hammer swing, position behind him, and unload a full combo. His Spinning Inferno has a 3-second recovery — enough for a charged heavy. Three cycles kill him.

Split 9: Shifting Archives Traversal (Target +10:00)

The Archives contain a warp shortcut that skips the puzzle entirely. From the entrance, take the second left, activate the warp pedestal, and teleport directly to the Corvid arena approach. This saves 6+ minutes compared to solving the Archive puzzle. The warp is a one-way teleport, so you cannot use it to return — commit to the path.

Split 10: Corvid the Watcher (Target +8:00)

Apply Poison enchantment to negate Corvid's HP regeneration. Phase 1 is straightforward — dodge her dive attacks and punish during recovery. Phase 2 (Shroud of Darkness, below 60% HP): the arena goes dark and Corvid dives from random directions. Listen for the wind-rush audio cue (~0.5 seconds before impact) and Harden on cue. She bounces off and crash-lands, giving a 3.5-second punish window. See the full Corvid Boss Guide.

Split 11: The Unchained (Target +14:00)

The Unchained is the hardest boss in the speedrun due to unpredictable attack patterns and high damage. Apply Holy enchantment (~20% bonus damage). The key pattern: his Dark Detonation AoE has a 4-second recovery window — the longest in the game. Stay at medium range during the charge-up, dodge backward, then sprint in and punish. His Chaos Flurry (multi-hit combo) should be Hardened through entirely; do not try to dodge individual hits.

Pro tip: If you die to The Unchained, you respawn at the arena entrance with full heals. This is the one boss where dying and retrying is faster than playing safe. Go aggressive on every attempt.

Split 12: The Dark Father (Target +14:00)

The final boss has three phases but relatively telegraphed attacks. Any enchantment works (~15% bonus). Phase 1: standard punish-after-combo pattern. Phase 2: he gains AoE ground slams — dodge backward, then sprint in. Phase 3 (below 30% HP): he becomes aggressive with long combo strings. Harden through the first 2 hits of any combo, then dodge the final hit and punish. His recovery windows in Phase 3 are shorter (~1.5 seconds) but still enough for a 3-hit light combo.

Advanced Time Saves & Tricks

Cutscene Skipping

All boss intro and death cutscenes are skippable via the pause menu. The only unskippable cutscene is the game's opening (~45 seconds). Boss death animations vary: Hadern's is 3 seconds (fastest), The Dark Father's is 8 seconds (slowest). Factor these into your split targets.

Upgrade Material Route

The optimal upgrade path requires exactly three material pickups — all on the main route:

  • +1 material: Corpse beside the crypt entrance in Fallgrim (Split 1)
  • +2 material: Chest in the Temple Grounds side room (Split 3, 10-second detour)
  • +3 material: Sarcophagus in the Crypt of Martyrs (Split 5, on-route)

These three upgrades push the Hallowed Sword from base to +3, which is enough damage for the entire run. Do not waste time seeking +4 or +5 materials — the detour costs 2+ minutes for a marginal DPS increase.

Healing Optimization

Carry exactly 3 heals. You start with 2; the third comes from a corpse in the Temple Grounds (on-route). Do not farm additional heals — each farm detour costs 30–60 seconds. If you need more than 3 heals in a run, the issue is boss execution, not supply.

Sprint Management

Sprint stamina regenerates after 0.8 seconds of walking. In long corridors, use a sprint-walk-sprint rhythm: sprint until stamina hits the one-bar reserve, walk for exactly 1 second, resume sprinting. This maintains near-constant sprint speed without fully depleting stamina.

Enemy Aggro Resets

If you accidentally aggro enemies during traversal, sprint 50+ meters away and they will de-aggro and return to their patrol. Do not fight non-boss enemies in a speedrun — the kill animation and stamina recovery costs more time than running past them.

Practice Routine & Tools

Boss Practice (Individual)

Use the Boss Rush mode to practice individual boss fights in isolation. Boss Rush lets you fight any boss you have defeated in the main game with pre-set loadouts. This is the fastest way to drill punish windows and Harden timings without running the full route each time. Focus on The Unchained and The Dark Father — these two bosses account for the most time variance in runs.

Traversal Practice (Segment Runs)

Practice each traversal section individually by loading a save at the start of each zone. Time yourself with a split timer (LiveSplit recommended). Target each zone's split time 3 times consecutively before moving to the next zone. Consistency matters more than a single fast time.

Full Run Attempts

Once you can hit all individual splits consistently, attempt full runs. Do not reset after a single bad split — play through the entire run to build mental endurance. Reset only if the cumulative time loss exceeds 5 minutes by the Seat of Infinity (Split 7). A good rule of thumb: if you are more than 3 minutes behind world record pace at any split, reset.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the easiest Shell to speedrun with?

Tiel Shell is the easiest in terms of route execution because its high stamina and fast movement speed forgive timing mistakes. However, Solomon Shell is more forgiving for boss fights due to higher HP. If you are new to speedrunning Mortal Shell II, start with Solomon until you can consistently kill every boss without dying, then switch to Tiel for the faster splits.

Can I skip any bosses in Any% Glitchless?

No. The Any% Glitchless category requires defeating all 7 mandatory bosses: Hadern, Crucix, Tarsus, Imrod, Corvid, The Unchained, and The Dark Father. The only bosses you can skip are optional encounters like mini-bosses in side areas. In the glitchless category, you must follow the intended zone progression — you cannot sequence-break to later zones early.

What enchantment should I use for each boss?

Fire for Crucix and Tarsus (they are both weak to fire, taking ~20–25% bonus damage), Frost for Imrod (slows his already-slow attacks further), Poison for Corvid (negates her HP regeneration), and Holy for The Unchained (~20% bonus). Hadern needs no enchantment because he dies in 3 cycles regardless. The Dark Father takes ~15% bonus from any element equally, so use whichever enchantment you have available.

How important is weapon upgrade level?

Critical. A +0 Hallowed Sword vs. a +3 Hallowed Sword is roughly a 40% damage difference. This translates to needing 5 boss cycles instead of 3, adding 2+ minutes per boss. Always pick up the three on-route upgrade materials — never skip them. The +3 material in the Crypt of Martyrs is the most important single time-save in the entire route.

Should I practice in Boss Rush or the main game?

Use Boss Rush for individual boss practice and the main game for traversal practice. Boss Rush is ideal for drilling specific fight mechanics (parry timings, Harden windows, punish routes) because you can restart instantly without replaying a zone. However, traversal sections have unique enemy placements and shortcut routes that only exist in the main game — you cannot practice those in Boss Rush.

What is a realistic first speedrun goal time?

For a first completed speedrun, aim for under 3 hours. This assumes you know the route, can kill every boss without dying (maybe 1–2 deaths), and do not get lost in any zone. Once you break 2:30, start optimizing individual splits. Sub-2:00 requires near-perfect boss execution and zero deaths. The world record of 1:34:12 requires frame-perfect play across every split.
