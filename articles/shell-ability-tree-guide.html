How Ability Trees Work — Branches, Costs & Prerequisites

Each Shell in Mortal Shell II has a three-branch ability tree: a primary combat branch (the Shell's core identity), a secondary branch (support abilities), and a survivability branch (HP, stamina, defense). Abilities cost 1-3 points depending on tier. Each Shell can reach Level 10 (approximately 17-20 total points depending on branch choices), gained through combat experience and Tar expenditure at Sester Genessa shrines.

Key rules:

  • Lower-tier abilities unlock higher tiers in the same branch. You cannot skip to Tier 3 without buying Tier 1 and 2.
  • Cross-branch abilities have no prerequisites — you can invest in any branch at any time.
  • Ability costs scale: Tier 1 costs 1 point, Tier 2 costs 2 points, Tier 3 costs 3 points. Capstone abilities (Level 10) cost 2-3 points.
  • Points are Shell-specific. Leveling Harrow to 10 does nothing for your Tiel Shell. Each Shell must be leveled independently.
  • Total Tar cost to fully max a Shell: approximately 45,000 Tar (Level 1→10). For farming routes, see the Tar Farming Guide.
Pro Insight: The most efficient strategy across all Shells is to rush the Tier 1 ability in your primary branch (1 point, immediate power spike), then fill the secondary branch's Tier 1, then return to max the primary branch. Spreading points evenly across all three branches is the slowest way to gain power.

If you are leveling multiple Shells, prioritize Eredrim and Harrow first for early-game power, then invest in Solomon for late-game and NG+.

FAQ

Should I max one Shell first or spread points across Shells?

Max one Shell first. A Level 10 Shell with a complete ability tree is dramatically stronger than two Level 5 Shells with half-complete trees. The capstone abilities (Level 8-10) are game-changers — Vengeance, Annihilator, Grand Arcanum, Fatal Precision — and you want at least one of them online before NG+.

Which Shell has the highest Tar cost to max?

Eredrim (19 points, ~52,000 Tar) is the most expensive. Solomon (20 points, ~54,000 Tar) is close. Harrow (18 points, ~48,000 Tar) is cheapest among the four combat Shells. See the Tar Farming Guide for efficient farming locations.

Can I respec ability points?

No. Mortal Shell II does not have a respec mechanic. Every point is permanent. This is why following an optimal route matters — a poorly spent point cannot be recovered. Plan your build before spending.

Do I need to level all 6 Shells for achievements?

Yes. The "Master of All" achievement requires reaching Level 10 on all 6 Shells. This is one of the grindiest achievements in the game. Start with your preferred Shell for the main story, then farm Tar to level the remaining Shells in NG+ where Tar drops are increased.

What is the single most impactful ability across all Shells?

Shadow Step III (Tiel) for mobility, Vengeance (Harrow) for survivability, Grand Arcanum (Solomon) for damage multiplier, Annihilator (Eredrim) for boss speedruns. If forced to pick one: Vengeance. A second life in boss fights is universally useful regardless of playstyle.

Are there hidden abilities not shown in the tree?

No hidden abilities, but some abilities have undocumented synergies. Spell Echo (25% free cast) can trigger on Grand Arcanum-boosted spells for massive free damage. Rampage stacks persist through zone transitions on foot. Ghost Walk decoys work on bosses. These interactions are not described in tooltips but are essential for maximizing each Shell's potential.
