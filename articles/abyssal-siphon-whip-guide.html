Abyssal Siphon Overview — The Void Whip

The Abyssal Siphon is Mortal Shell II's only mid-range melee weapon — a segmented chain whip infused with void energy, found in the Shifting Archives behind a puzzle-locked vault (requires the Void Key from a hidden chest in the Archives' lower level). At Tier 5, it deals 210 base charged heavy damage — the lowest of any non-dagger weapon. But damage is not why you use the Abyssal Siphon.

Its signature ability is Abyssal Pull: hold the heavy attack button to lash the whip forward up to 12 meters (at Tier 5), grab an enemy, and yank them to your feet — staggered and vulnerable for 1.2 seconds. This is the only weapon in the game that lets you reposition enemies rather than repositioning yourself. Against mob packs, it allows you to isolate and eliminate enemies one at a time. Against bosses, certain pulls can interrupt specific attack animations and create punish windows that no other weapon can access.

The Abyssal Siphon is a utility weapon, not a primary DPS weapon. Most players equip it in their second weapon slot, pull dangerous enemies out of packs, then swap to a higher-damage weapon for the kill. It pairs exceptionally well with builds that benefit from isolated targets: Tiel backstab builds and Solomon Resolve-farming setups.

Pro Insight: The Abyssal Pull can yank enemies off ledges. In the Shifting Archives and Seat of Infinity (both have abundant bottomless pits), pulling enemies into the void is an instant kill regardless of their HP. This includes elite Wraiths and Flame Knights that normally take 30+ seconds to kill conventionally.

This is the Abyssal Siphon's sweet spot; upgrading beyond Tier 3 provides marginal range benefit and significant material cost. Most players stop at Tier 3.

Pull Mechanics — Range, Stagger & Combo Setup

The Abyssal Pull operates on a simple but powerful rule set:

  • Stagger on arrival: Pulled enemies are always staggered for 1.2 seconds upon landing. This is a guaranteed punish window — no RNG, no poise check.
  • Pull cancel: If you are hit during the pull windup (0.4s), the pull cancels. Harden before pulling against aggressive enemies.
  • Boss immunity: Most bosses cannot be pulled. Exceptions: Hadern (pullable, short stagger), Crucix human form (pullable, 0.8s stagger). Spider form, Tarsus, Imrod, Corvid, Unchained, Dark Father — immune.
  • Ledge kills: If the pull path crosses a ledge, the enemy falls and dies instantly. This works on ALL pullable enemies including elite variants.
  • Pull → weapon swap: During the 1.2s stagger window, you can swap weapons and begin a combo with your primary weapon. The enemy remains staggered through the swap.

Optimal pull combo: Pull enemy → swap to Martyr's Blade/Smoldering Mace → charged heavy (1.8s/1.5s windup during 1.2s stagger — enemy begins recovering as you release, guaranteeing the hit connects during their recovery frames).

FAQ

Where is the Void Key?

Shifting Archives lower level, in a hidden room behind a breakable bookshelf (east wall of the main library). The bookshelf has a faint purple glow. Hit it with any weapon to reveal the chest containing the Void Key. The vault itself is on the Archives' upper level, marked by a chain-whip symbol on the door.

Can the Abyssal Siphon be a primary weapon?

No. Its 210 base damage at Tier 5 is too low for boss fights. It excels as a utility weapon in the second slot. Pull dangerous enemies, swap to primary, kill. This is its intended role and it performs it brilliantly.

Does the pull work through walls?

No. The pull requires line of sight. However, it can pull through doorways and over railings — perfect for the Shifting Archives' multi-level design.

What is the pull-stagger guaranteed punish window?

1.2 seconds for all pullable enemies. This is enough time for: one charged heavy from any weapon except Martyr's Blade (1.8s), one full light combo from Hallowed Daggers (1.8s), or one spell cast from any catalyst (0.8-1.2s).

Can I pull multiple enemies at once?

No. The Abyssal Pull targets a single enemy. Against packs, pull the most dangerous enemy first (Flame Knight, Wraith Mage), kill it during the stagger window, then pull the next. This "chain-pull" technique is the safest way to clear elite mob rooms.

Should I upgrade the Abyssal Siphon past Tier 3?

Only if you have excess materials after maxing your primary weapon. The 10m range at Tier 3 is sufficient for most engagements. Tier 5's 12m range is a luxury — nice to have, but the materials (5× Tempered Alloy, 1× Pure Titanite Slab) are better spent on Martyr's Blade or Smoldering Mace first.
