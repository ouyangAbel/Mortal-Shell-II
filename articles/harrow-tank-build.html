Why Harrow? The Immovable Object Philosophy

Harrow is Mortal Shell II's dedicated tank Shell — built to absorb punishment that would kill any other Shell twice over. With the highest base HP (~180 at max), the longest Harden duration (1.8 seconds), and an ability tree revolving entirely around damage reduction and counter-attack bonuses, Harrow turns boss fights into wars of attrition that you will always win.

The Harrow tank build operates on a simple principle: you take less damage per hit than any enemy can deal before you kill them. Where Tiel players must dodge every attack or die, Harrow players can eat 4-5 boss hits, pop a heal, and keep trading. Your damage output is lower than Eredrim's and your speed is slower than Tiel's — but your effective HP pool, once you factor in fortification and damage reduction stacking, exceeds 400% of your base HP.

This is the ideal Shell for players new to soulslikes, for anyone struggling with a specific boss, or for NG+ cycles where enemy damage scaling makes lighter Shells untenable. Harrow is not flashy — but Harrow never dies.

Pro Insight: Harrow's strength is not in avoiding damage — it is in making damage irrelevant. In NG+7, The Dark Father's Phase 3 combo deals ~900 damage. A fully built Harrow with fortification active takes ~315 damage. That is the difference between a one-shot and a manageable hit you can heal through.

Harrow Ability Tree — Optimal Path & Point Allocation

Harrow's tree branches into Fortification (passive defense), Retribution (counter-attack bonuses), and Endurance (HP and stamina recovery). The tank build prioritizes Fortification and Retribution in that order. Here is the optimal Level 10 unlock path:

Total: 18 points. Vengeance at Level 10 is expensive but game-changing — it is effectively a second life in boss fights. Combined with Iron Resolve's +25% HP, a Level 10 Harrow at 225 HP can absorb what would be a fatal hit, trigger Vengeance, and immediately heal back to full.

Damage Reduction Stacking — How to Reach 65% DR

The Harrow tank build's core strength is multiplicative damage reduction from multiple sources. Here is the complete DR calculation at max build:

SourceDR%TypeCondition
Fortification III20%PassiveWhile Harden available
Iron Bark (consumable)20%Buff120s duration
Guardian's Ring (accessory)10%EquipmentPermanent passive
Active Harden100%ActiveDuring Harden window only
Total passive DR~42%CombinedFort III + Iron Bark + Guardian's Ring (multiplicative)

The math works multiplicatively, not additively: an incoming 100-damage hit with Fortification III (20%) and Iron Bark (20%) becomes 100 × 0.8 × 0.8 = 64 damage. Add Guardian's Ring (10%) and it becomes 100 × 0.8 × 0.8 × 0.9 = 57.6 damage — an effective 42.4% DR. This stacks with Harden's 100% DR while active, meaning you can cycle between "invulnerable" and "barely scratched."

Important: Fortification is only active while Harden is not on cooldown. If you waste Harden at the wrong time, your DR drops to Iron Bark + Guardian's Ring only. Always Harden reactively against incoming attacks, not proactively.

Harden Synergy — The Longest Harden Window in the Game

With Adamant Shell, Harrow's Harden lasts 2.2 seconds — the longest in the game. This has three major implications for the tank build:

1. Double-Hit Absorption

Most boss combos hit twice in quick succession (Hadern's 3-hit combo, Tarsus's Frost Greatsword → Ice Slash, Imrod's Hammer → Shockwave). Other Shells Harden the first hit, break, and eat the second. Harrow can Harden both hits in a single window, then break with full Counter Strike bonus active for the punish phase.

2. Deliberate Multi-Hit Tanking

The Unchained's Chaos Flurry hits 5 times in 1.8 seconds. Harrow's 2.2s Harden absorbs the entire flurry. When it ends, you are at full HP, Counter Strike is active at +35%, and The Unchained is stuck in a 4-second recovery. This turns the game's most feared attack into your biggest DPS window.

3. Harden During Healing

The healing animation takes 1.8 seconds. With Harrow's 2.2s Harden window, you can start healing, Harden mid-animation, and the heal completes while you remain invulnerable. No other Shell can Harden through the full heal animation — they must choose between healing and defending. Harrow does both simultaneously.

Pro Insight: The Harrow healing trick is strongest against Corvid's Shroud phase — you can heal during her Dive Bomb and take zero damage. It also trivializes Tarsus's Blizzard phase, where chip damage would otherwise wear you down.

FAQ

Is Harrow good for a first playthrough?

Absolutely. Harrow is arguably the best Shell for new players. The combination of high HP, long Harden duration, and Unbreakable (no stagger) means you survive mistakes that would kill any other Shell. Start with Harrow, learn boss patterns safely, then experiment with lighter Shells in NG+.

How do I deal with Harrow's low stamina?

Harrow's base stamina (~110) is the second lowest in the game. You get 2-3 heavy swings before exhaustion. The solution: attack during Counter Strike windows only. The +35% damage bonus means each swing counts 35% more, so you need fewer swings overall. Also, use Harden mid-combo to let stamina regenerate while invulnerable — this is the same technique from the combat tips guide but Harrow benefits most because you have more Harden duration to regen.

What consumables should Harrow carry?

Iron Bark (20% DR) is mandatory. Healing Salve ×3 — Harrow's large HP pool means a single Salve heals less proportionally, so you need more. Stamina Tonic for tough fights offsets Harrow's low stamina. If using Martyr's Blade, skip Frost Vials since the weapon applies frost naturally.

Does Harrow work against The Dark Father Phase 3?

Yes, and better than most Shells. Vengeance (survive at 1 HP) is designed for this fight — Phase 3's clone barrage can delete smaller Shells instantly, but Harrow survives on 1 HP, pops invulnerability, and has 3 seconds to heal. Counter Strike +35% also lets you cleave multiple clones simultaneously with a single charged heavy.

Can Harrow use light weapons effectively?

Yes, but you are wasting the Shell's potential. Light weapons deal less damage per swing, which means you need more swings — and Harrow's low stamina cannot sustain long combo chains. If you prefer light weapons, use Tiel or Solomon. Harrow rewards heavy weapon mastery.

How does Harrow compare to Eredrim for tanking?

Eredrim has higher raw HP (~200 max) but lower active DR. Harrow with Fortification III + Iron Bark takes 42% less damage constantly — effectively giving Harrow a larger EHP pool than Eredrim. Eredrim wins on damage output; Harrow wins on survivability. For pure tanking, Harrow is the definitive choice.
