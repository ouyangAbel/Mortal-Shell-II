How to Unlock Boss Rush Mode

Boss Rush mode is Mortal Shell II's ultimate endgame challenge — a gauntlet of all eight major bosses fought back-to-back with limited resources between encounters. It is available only after completing the main game at least once. Here is the exact unlock process:

  1. Complete the main story — defeat The Dark Father and watch the credits roll.
  2. Return to Fallgrim — the Sester Genessa near the hub area will have a new dialogue option.
  3. Speak to the Sester — she mentions "the echoes that linger" and offers to send you to the Crucible of Echoes.
  4. Accept the challenge — you will be teleported to a special arena where Boss Rush begins.

Boss Rush can be accessed from any Sester Genessa after unlocking it. You do not need to be in NG+ — the mode is available on any completed save file, including the base game difficulty. NG+ completions unlock additional difficulty tiers (more on this below).

Pro Tip: Boss Rush uses your current character build. Make sure you have upgraded your preferred Shell abilities and weapons before starting a run. Any consumables in your inventory carry over, so stock up on Roasted Rats, Quenching Acid, and Moonshine before entering.

Boss Rush Rules & Scoring System

Boss Rush is not a simple boss marathon — it has a dedicated scoring system that ranks your performance. Understanding how scoring works is essential for earning S-rank rewards.

Core Rules

  • 8 consecutive bosses — the order is fixed (see boss order section below).
  • Health carries over — whatever HP you finish one boss with is your starting HP for the next. There is no full heal between fights.
  • Limited healing — you begin with 3 Roasted Rats (or equivalent healing items). You earn 1 additional heal after every boss defeat.
  • Stamina recovery — stamina fully restores between each boss fight.
  • Harden cooldown resets — your Harden is fully available at the start of each encounter.
  • No consumable drops — bosses do not drop items during Boss Rush.
  • Death ends the run — dying to any boss sends you back to the start of the entire gauntlet. There are no checkpoints.

Scoring Breakdown

Your final score determines your rank. Points are awarded as follows:

Maximum possible score: 8,000 (boss kills) + 4,000 (no-hit all) + 6,400 (max speed) + 1,200 (Harden) + 1,600 (parry) + 3,600 (12 heals) + 1,000 (Shell) = 25,800 points.

Optimal Shell & Weapon Loadout

Choosing the right Shell and weapon combination is the single biggest factor in a successful Boss Rush run. You need a balance of survivability, damage output, and flexibility across eight different bosses with different weaknesses.

Shell Rankings for Boss Rush

ShellTierWhy
SolomonSBest all-around: balanced HP, stamina, Harden cooldown. Spell access lets you burst bosses during safe windows. Ideal for the mixed demands of 8 different fights.
TielAFastest Harden cycle (~4.5s) and best dodge recovery. Low HP is a liability across 8 fights, but a skilled player can avoid all damage for S-rank. Best for speed runs.
HarrowAHighest HP pool and longest Harden duration. Slow but forgiving — if you make a mistake, Harrow survives hits that kill other Shells. Good for first-time Boss Rush clears.
EredrimBMassive counter-damage via Brutal Strikes. Can kill bosses faster, but the slow Harden cooldown (~6.5s) makes consecutive bosses punishing. Best paired with weapons that stagger.

Weapon Recommendations

The best Boss Rush weapon depends on your Shell choice and playstyle. Here are the top picks:

  • Hallowed Sword +10 — the most versatile weapon. Fast swings, good range, and a wide parry window make it safe against every boss. Pair with Solomon for the ultimate all-rounder build. See the Hallowed Sword Guide for upgrade details.
  • Martyr's Blade +8 — frost buildup staggers most bosses, and the heavy attack's wide arc punishes recovery windows hard. Best paired with Harrow (high HP compensates for the blade's slow speed). See the Martyr's Blade Guide.
  • Dual Wield (any) — highest DPS but lowest stagger. You need excellent dodging to use dual weapons in Boss Rush. Best paired with Tiel for speed runs. See the Dual Wield Build Guide.
  • Smoldering Mace +7 — fire DoT shreds bosses with large HP pools (Imrod, The Unchained, The Dark Father). The AoE shockwave staggers adds in Crucix's spider phase. See the Smoldering Mace Guide.
Warning: Do not enter Boss Rush with an unupgraded weapon. The difference between a +3 and +10 weapon across 8 bosses is enormous — a +3 run takes roughly 50% longer, which tanks your speed bonus. Farm upgrade materials before attempting S-rank.

Boss Order & Per-Boss Strategies

The boss order in Boss Rush is fixed. Learning the sequence lets you plan heal usage, Harden reserves, and positioning mentally before each fight.

Per-Boss Quick Tips

Hadern (Boss 1): The warmup. Harden his 3-hit combo, punish with a full light combo during his 1.5s recovery. Use this fight to build parry score — his sword swings are the most telegraphed in the game. Goal: defeat in under 45 seconds, zero heals used.

Crucix (Boss 2): Phase 1 is straightforward — dodge the sword swings, punish after his thrust. Phase 2 (spider form): hug his underside. His Leaping Crush cannot target directly below, and Venom Spray fires forward. See the full Crucix guide for detailed patterns.

Tarsus (Boss 3): Fire enchantment deals +25% damage. When he charges Ice Burst, Harden through it at point-blank range and punish during the 2.5s recovery. His clone in the Blizzard phase can be identified by the slightly brighter ice particles — attack the real one. See the Tarsus guide.

Corvid (Boss 4): Her aerial attacks have huge telegraph windows. During Shroud of Darkness (below 60% HP), listen for the wind-rush sound ~0.5s before impact and Harden. She crash-lands with a 3.5s punish window. Poison enchantment negates her HP regeneration. See the Corvid guide.

Imrod (Boss 5): The hardest mid-run boss. He takes +30% damage from behind — use mobility to circle him. Frost enchantment is his weakness. When he does Spinning Inferno, Harden at point-blank and punish the 3.0s recovery. Avoid the lava pools he creates — they persist and chip away at your HP. See the Imrod guide.

The Unchained (Boss 6): His Chaos Flurry is a 5-hit combo — Harden the first hit, let the rest bounce off your shell. After Dark Detonation, you have a massive 4.0s punish window. Holy enchantment deals +20%. See the Unchained guide.

The Dark Father Phase 1 (Boss 7): Deceptively simple attacks with deliberately misleading recovery animations. Only punish for 1.5s maximum — his "exhaustion" animations are bait. Fire enchantment works well.

The Dark Father Phase 2+3 (Boss 8): Shadow Form tracks aggressively — sprint perpendicular to avoid. In Ultimate Form (below 25% HP), he enters desperation mode with faster attacks and AoE slams. This is the most heal-intensive part of the entire run. Save at least 2-3 heals for this phase. See the Dark Father guide.

Pro Tip: Apply elemental enchantments before entering each boss arena. Enchantments last 60 seconds and do not tick down during cutscenes. If you enchant right as the fog gate opens, you get nearly full uptime.

S-Rank Requirements & Tips

S-rank requires 23,000+ points — an extremely demanding threshold. You cannot achieve S-rank by simply clearing all 8 bosses. You need multiple bonus categories stacking. Here is the realistic path to S-rank:

S-Rank Budget Breakdown

CategoryRealistic TargetHow to Achieve
Boss Defeated8,000Beat all 8 bosses (mandatory)
No-Hit Bonuses3,000 (6/8)No-hit the easy 6; accept hits on Imrod & Dark Father
Speed Bonus4,800 (avg 600)Kill each boss within ~2 minutes average
Harden Perfect900 (6/8)Master Harden-timing on bosses 1-6
Parry Riposte1,200 (6/8)Parry at least once per boss (bosses 1-6)
Heals Remaining1,500 (5 left)Use only 3-4 heals total across all 8 bosses
Shell Survived1,000Never swap Shells
Total20,400Close but not S-rank yet

The gap: You need ~2,600 more points. This comes from either no-hitting all 8 bosses (extra 1,500 from bosses 7-8) or maintaining speed (extra 1,600 from shaving 15-20 seconds per boss). Most S-rank runs require a combination of both.

The Three Pillars of S-Rank

  1. Know every boss's safe punish window. You cannot afford to trade hits — healing costs points (lost no-heal bonus) and time (lost speed bonus). Study each boss guide linked above until you can describe their attack patterns from memory.
  2. Use Harden offensively, not defensively. The Harden-Bait Loop (approach, Harden before swinging, punish recoil) is both safe and fast. It simultaneously earns Harden Perfect points and keeps you at full HP.
  3. Never waste time repositioning. Walk toward the boss at all times. Walking (not sprinting) saves stamina for attacks. The only time you should sprint is to dodge an AoE or close a large gap. Every second of walking is a second of speed bonus lost.
Warning: S-rank is extremely difficult. Most players will need 10-20 attempts before achieving it. Do not get discouraged — each failed run teaches you something about a specific boss's timing. Focus on improving one boss at a time.

Advanced Strategies & Speed Techniques

The Harden-Bait Speed Loop

The fastest way to kill any boss in Boss Rush is the Harden-Bait Loop. Walk forward, Harden just before the boss attacks, let them bounce off your shell, then unleash a fully charged heavy attack during their recoil. This cycle takes roughly 4-5 seconds per loop and deals maximum damage. On most bosses, 6-8 loops is enough to kill them.

Enchantment Stacking for Boss-Specific Advantage

Apply the correct enchantment before every boss fight. This alone increases your effective DPS by 15-25%. The key matchups:

  • Fire enchantment for Tarsus (+25%), Crucix (+20%), Hadern (+15%)
  • Frost enchantment for Imrod (+25%)
  • Poison enchantment for Corvid (negates HP regen)
  • Holy enchantment for The Unchained (+20%)

Heal Conservation Strategy

The optimal heal strategy for S-rank:

  1. Bosses 1-4 (Hadern through Corvid): Zero heals used. These bosses are predictable enough to no-hit with practice.
  2. Boss 5 (Imrod): 0-1 heals. His lava pools chip away at HP — use a heal only if you are below 50% before Boss 6.
  3. Boss 6 (The Unchained): 0-1 heals. His Chaos Flurry is either perfectly Harden'd or you take massive damage. No middle ground.
  4. Bosses 7-8 (The Dark Father): 1-3 heals. Phase 2 and 3 are where most runs end. Budget at least 2 heals for desperation attacks.

Stamina Management Between Fights

Stamina fully restores between bosses, but your position in the arena resets. Before each boss fight starts, walk (don't sprint) to mid-range, apply enchantment, and prepare your Harden. The 2-3 seconds before the boss aggros are free — use them to mentally rehearse the first attack pattern.

Pro Tip: If you are going for a no-hit run on a specific boss, enter the fight with full Harden cooldown. The hardest attacks to dodge cleanly (Imrod's Spinning Inferno, The Unchained's Chaos Flurry) are trivially absorbed by a perfectly timed Harden. Save your Harden for the dangerous attacks rather than using it on cooldown.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use healing items between bosses in Boss Rush?

You start with 3 heals and earn 1 additional heal after each boss defeat. There is no way to farm or find extra heals during the run. Conserve them carefully — especially for The Dark Father's final phases. Roasted Rats are the most efficient heal; Moonshine is faster but heals less.

Does dying in Boss Rush affect my main save file?

No. Boss Rush is a self-contained mode. When you die or complete a run, you are returned to the Sester Genessa with your original save state intact. No items, Tar, or progress are lost from dying in Boss Rush.

What happens if I swap Shells during a Boss Rush run?

You can swap Shells during the run — any dead Shell in the arena can be inhabited as normal. However, you lose the "Shell Survived" bonus (1,000 points). For S-rank attempts, never swap. For casual clears, swapping to Harrow before The Dark Father is a viable survival strategy.

Is Boss Rush available on NG+ difficulty?

Yes. If your save file is in NG+ or higher, Boss Rush will use the corresponding enemy scaling. NG+ Boss Rush enemies have more HP and deal more damage, but the scoring thresholds remain the same. NG+ Boss Rush is significantly harder but does not offer additional rewards.

Can I pause during Boss Rush?

Yes, you can pause the game between bosses (during the "Next Boss" transition screen). However, you cannot pause mid-fight. The transition screen lasts 5 seconds — use this time to check your HP, remaining heals, and mentally prepare for the next boss.

What is the fastest Boss Rush completion time?

The current world record for Boss Rush (any Shell) is approximately 18 minutes 42 seconds using Tiel Shell with Dual Wield weapons. A more achievable target for skilled players is 25-30 minutes using Solomon with Hallowed Sword. Anything under 20 minutes is exceptionally fast.
