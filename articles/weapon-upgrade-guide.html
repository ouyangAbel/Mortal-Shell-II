How Weapon Upgrades Work

In Mortal Shell II, every weapon can be upgraded from its base level all the way to +10 at Sester Genessa's workbench. Each upgrade tier increases base damage and improves the weapon's stat scaling letter, turning an early-game weapon into an endgame powerhouse. The system is straightforward but demands rare materials at higher tiers—making planning essential.

Upgrades are permanent and bound to the weapon, not the Shell. You can swap Shells freely without losing weapon progress. However, each weapon has a unique scaling profile that favors specific Shells, so upgrading blindly without understanding scaling can waste precious materials.

The Upgrade NPC: Sester Genessa

Every Sester Genessa location across Fallgrim, the Temple Grounds, the Crypt of Martyrs, the Seat of Infinity, and the Shifting Archives serves as an upgrade station. You do not need to return to a specific NPC—any Sester can upgrade any weapon if you have the materials and Tar. The upgrade menu appears under the "Improve" tab when interacting with a Sester.

Pro Tip: You can preview the damage increase before committing materials. The upgrade screen shows your weapon's current damage, post-upgrade damage, and updated scaling letter. Never upgrade without checking whether the scaling improvement actually benefits your current Shell's stat distribution.

Quenching Acid (+1 to +3)

Quenching Acid is the most common upgrade material and drops frequently from standard enemies across Fallgrim. Brigands, Leapers, and Grishas all have a chance to drop it. You can also find it in loot chests scattered throughout the swamp and the surrounding caves. A single playthrough of Fallgrim typically yields 6-8 Quenching Acids without dedicated farming.

Cost per upgrade:

  • +1: 1 Quenching Acid + 300 Tar
  • +2: 2 Quenching Acid + 600 Tar
  • +3: 3 Quenching Acid + 1,000 Tar

Eminent Upgrade (+4 to +6)

Eminent Upgrades become available as you progress into the Temple Grounds and Crypt of Martyrs. They drop from elite enemies—particularly Crypt Wraiths, Temple Sentinels, and the mini-boss variants that guard key corridors. The drop rate is lower than Quenching Acid, so deliberate farming may be necessary if you want to push a weapon to +6 before reaching later areas.

Cost per upgrade:

  • +4: 1 Eminent Upgrade + 2,000 Tar
  • +5: 2 Eminent Upgrade + 3,500 Tar
  • +6: 3 Eminent Upgrade + 5,000 Tar

Legendary Upgrade (+7 to +10)

Legendary Upgrades are the scarcest materials in the game. They appear exclusively in the Seat of Infinity, the Shifting Archives, and as rewards for defeating major bosses. Pushing a weapon from +6 to +10 requires a total of 10 Legendary Upgrades—roughly one full clear of the Seat of Infinity plus a boss rush. If you are targeting a specific +10 weapon for endgame or NG+, plan your farming routes early.

Cost per upgrade:

  • +7: 2 Legendary Upgrade + 7,000 Tar
  • +8: 2 Legendary Upgrade + 9,000 Tar
  • +9: 3 Legendary Upgrade + 12,000 Tar
  • +10: 3 Legendary Upgrade + 15,000 Tar
Warning: Legendary Upgrades are finite per playthrough. There are approximately 12-14 available in a single cycle, and pushing two weapons to +10 requires 20. Choose your endgame weapon carefully before committing Legendary Upgrades—you cannot refund them.

Weapon Upgrade Paths Explained

Unlike some soulslikes that offer branching upgrade paths, Mortal Shell II follows a linear upgrade path for each weapon. There are no infusions or elemental branching—each weapon has a single path from base to +10. However, the scaling letters change as you upgrade, which dramatically shifts which Shells benefit most from each weapon.

Every weapon starts with base scaling in 1-2 stats (Strength, Dexterity, or a hybrid). As you upgrade, scaling letters improve: E → D → C → B → A → S. The jump from C to B scaling is the most impactful—typically adding 15-20% more damage per stat point invested. This means the +4 to +6 upgrade range is where weapons truly transform.

Best Material Farming Routes

When you need specific materials, targeted farming beats random exploration. Here are the most efficient routes for each tier.

Quenching Acid — Fallgrim Swamp Circuit

Start at the Fallgrim Sester, head through the swamp toward the Grisha cave entrance, loop back past the Brigand camp, and return to Sester. Each circuit takes roughly 3 minutes and yields 1-2 Quenching Acids from Brigand and Leaper drops. Rest at Sester to respawn enemies and repeat. This is also an excellent Tar farming route—see the Tar Farming Guide for optimization.

Eminent Upgrade — Temple Grounds Gauntlet

From the Temple Grounds Sester, push through the main corridor past the two Temple Sentinels and into the side chamber with the Crypt Wraith. Both Sentinels and the Wraith have elevated Eminent Upgrade drop rates. A full clear takes 5-7 minutes and yields 0-2 Eminent Upgrades per run. The drop rate is approximately 30% per elite kill.

Legendary Upgrade — Seat of Infinity Loop

The Seat of Infinity contains the densest concentration of Legendary Upgrade pickups. From the entrance Sester, take the left path through the outer ring, collecting the two guaranteed pickups near the gravity-well corridors. Then push through the inner sanctum for a third pickup guarded by elite enemies. One full loop takes 8-12 minutes and yields 2-3 Legendary Upgrades. See the Map Guide for exact pickup locations.

Pro Tip: Boss defeats always drop upgrade materials. Hadern drops Quenching Acid, Crucix and Tarsus drop Eminent Upgrades, and The Unchained and The Dark Father drop Legendary Upgrades. If you are comfortable with a boss, farming them is faster than enemy grinding.

Upgrade Tips & Common Mistakes

Do Not Spread Materials Across Multiple Weapons

The most common mistake new players make is upgrading 3-4 weapons to +4 instead of pushing one weapon to +7 or higher. The scaling improvements at higher levels are multiplicative, not additive. A single +10 weapon will outperform two +5 weapons in every scenario. Pick your main weapon early and commit.

Match Weapon Scaling to Your Shell

Eredrim has high Strength, Tiel has high Dexterity, Solomon has balanced stats, and Harrow favors Stamina. Upgrading a Strength weapon on a Tiel Shell wastes scaling potential. Check the Shell Compendium for stat distributions before committing rare materials.

Consider Attack Speed When Evaluating Damage

A weapon with 300 AR that attacks once per second (300 DPS) is not necessarily better than a weapon with 230 AR that attacks twice per second (460 DPS). Against fast bosses with small punish windows, faster weapons with lower AR often deal more total damage per opening. Upgrade based on your most-played content—slow weapons for bosses with large recovery windows, fast weapons for aggressive bosses.

Do Not Forget Weapon Abilities

Several weapons have unique special attacks that scale with upgrade level. The Smoldering Mace's ground slam, the Abyssal Siphon's life-drain pulse, and the Hallowed Sword's wind cleave all become significantly more powerful at higher upgrade levels. These abilities are often more impactful than raw AR numbers suggest.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I undo a weapon upgrade in Mortal Shell II?

No. Weapon upgrades are permanent and irreversible. Once you commit materials, there is no refund or downgrade option. This is why it is critical to choose your endgame weapon before spending Legendary Upgrades. If you are unsure, hold your materials until you have tested each weapon's moveset at base level.

What happens to my weapon upgrade in New Game Plus?

All weapon upgrades carry over into NG+ completely intact. Your +10 weapon will still be +10 when you start a new cycle. This makes NG+ significantly easier if you have invested in upgrades. See the New Game Plus Guide for full details on what carries over.

Is it worth upgrading the Hallowed Sword past +6?

Yes, if you are running a Strength-based Shell like Eredrim or Harrow. The Hallowed Sword's scaling jumps from B to A between +6 and +10, adding roughly 95 total AR. Its unique wind cleave ability also scales with upgrade level. However, if you prefer a faster playstyle, the Martyr's Blade may be a better long-term investment.

How many Legendary Upgrades exist per playthrough?

Approximately 12-14 Legendary Upgrades are available per cycle. This includes pickups in the Seat of Infinity (6-7), Shifting Archives (3-4), and boss drops (3). Since pushing one weapon from +6 to +10 requires 10 Legendary Upgrades, you can max exactly one weapon per playthrough with a small margin for error.

Do elemental enchantments stack with weapon upgrades?

Yes. Elemental enchantments (Fire, Frost, Holy, Poison) add bonus damage on top of your weapon's upgraded base damage and scaling. A +10 Smoldering Mace with a Fire enchantment deals both the weapon's physical damage and the enchantment's fire damage independently. Enchantments last 60 seconds and can be reapplied freely—always enchant before boss fights.

Which weapon should I upgrade first for a first playthrough?

The Martyr's Blade is the safest first-upgrade choice. Its balanced Strength/Dex scaling works well with every Shell, its medium attack speed suits most boss encounters, and it does not require a specific build to be effective. Once you understand the game's systems, you can specialize with a second weapon on NG+.
