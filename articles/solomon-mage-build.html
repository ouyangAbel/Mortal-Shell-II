Why Solomon? The Scholar's War Philosophy

Solomon is Mortal Shell II's designated caster Shell — built around the Resolve resource system and spell amplification. Where other Shells use Resolve as a secondary mechanic (occasional weapon arts or emergency heals), Solomon turns Resolve into a primary damage source. With the largest Resolve pool (5 full bars at max upgrade), innate Resolve regeneration in combat, and ability tree nodes that amplify spell damage by up to 60%, Solomon can chain 3-4 high-level spells per boss fight — each dealing 800-1200 damage.

The Solomon mage build plays differently from every other Shell. Your weapon is a Resolve generator first and a damage source second. You weave spells between melee combos, banking Resolve for burst windows during boss recovery phases. A well-timed Arcane Barrage into Flame Pillar combo at 4x Resolve amplification can delete 25% of a boss's HP in under 3 seconds.

Solomon rewards game knowledge above all else. You need to know when to cast — interrupting a spell animation with a boss attack kills you as quickly as any Tiel misplay. But for players who master the timing, Solomon delivers the highest burst damage in Mortal Shell II.

Pro Insight: Solomon's Resolve regenerates passively during combat — roughly 0.5 bars per 15 seconds at max upgrade. This means you gain one full spell cast for every 30 seconds of active fighting. On longer boss encounters (The Dark Father, 8-10 minutes), this passive generation alone provides 4-5 extra spells — the equivalent of 4000+ free damage.

Resolve System Deep Dive — Banking & Amplification

Resolve is the core resource for Solomon's damage output. Understanding generation, banking, and amplification is essential:

SourceResolve GeneratedNotes
Successful Parry1.0 barHighest per-action generation. Learn parry timings.
Harden Counter (break from hit)0.5 barsReliable, safe generation. Pair with Harden-Bait Loop.
Enemy Kill0.3 barsMinor but adds up in mob zones.
Backstab0.7 barsSolomon's base. Tiel's Assassin Resolve gives +50% more.
Passive Regen (Resonant Flow II)0.5 bars/15s"Free" Resolve during combat. 2 full bars per minute.
Resolve Tonic (consumable)2.0 bars instantlyEmergency Resolve. Carry 2. Use before burst windows.

Resolve Banking: The Resolve Bank ability stores excess Resolve earned beyond your 5-bar cap. If you clear a zone at 5 bars and generate 2 more bars from kills, those 2 bars are banked. When you enter the next boss fight, you start at 5 bars (max) PLUS 2 banked bars appear after 10 seconds — giving you 7 effective bars for the fight. This is the single most important preparation step for Solomon: always farm to full + bank before bosses.

Critical: Banked Resolve decays if you fast-travel or die. It persists through zone transitions on foot. Before The Dark Father (final boss), clear the entire Shifting Archives zone on foot without fast-traveling — you can enter the fight with 8+ effective Resolve bars.

Resolve Farming — Maximize Generation Per Fight

For boss fights, enter with 5 bars + banked Resolve. Here is the optimal pre-boss routine:

  1. Clear the boss run-back area of all enemies. Each kill generates 0.3 bars. A typical run-back has 8-10 enemies = 2.4-3.0 bars.
  2. Parry every enemy during clear. Parry generates 1.0 bar — the highest per-action rate. If you parry 5 enemies during clear, that is 5 additional bars (some will be wasted to overflow, but Bank stores them).
  3. Pop Resolve Tonic before fog gate. +2 bars instantly. Combined with clearing, you can enter with 5 bars active + 3-4 bars banked.
  4. Do NOT fast-travel. Fast-travel clears Banked Resolve. Walk the route.

For zone exploration, the rhythm is: fight → generate 2-3 bars → cast a spell to clear a tough enemy → repeat. Solomon's passive regen (0.5 bars/15s) means you always have at least 1 spell available every 30 seconds of exploration, even without active generation.

FAQ

Is Solomon viable for a first playthrough?

Solomon is viable but has a steeper learning curve than Harrow or Eredrim. You need to learn both melee combat and spell timing. If you enjoy caster playstyles, Solomon is the most rewarding Shell in the game. If you want simplicity, start with Harrow and switch to Solomon in NG+.

What is the best single spell for DPS?

Flame Pillar (2 bars, Pyromancer's Brazier reduced to 1.5 bars) deals the highest single-cast damage (~550 base, 1116 with full amplification). It also leaves a burning ground effect dealing 15% of initial damage per second for 5 seconds. Total damage per cast: ~1950 at max amplification. For more details on consumables that boost fire damage, see the combat tips guide.

How do I deal with Solomon's average HP?

Solomon has ~145 base HP — middle of the pack. The key is range: most spells can be cast from 8-10 meters, well outside melee range. Use Frost Nova to root enemies before casting, or Harden mid-cast for safety. Do not try to cast while a boss is in active attack frames.

Does Spell Echo stack with Grand Arcanum?

Yes. Spell Echo (25% chance for free cast) can trigger on a Grand Arcanum-boosted spell. When it does, you get the full amplified damage without spending Resolve. This is pure RNG but mathematically adds ~6.25% more damage output over a long fight.

What consumables should Solomon prioritize?

Resolve Tonic ×2 (mandatory — this is your "mana potion"), Stamina Tonic ×1 (for extended parry chains), and Sharpening Stone (applies to melee, not spells, but helps during Resolve downtime). If using Pyromancer's Brazier, carry Fire Oil for even more Flame Pillar damage.

Can Solomon function without a catalyst?

No — without a catalyst you cannot cast spells, and Solomon's weapon damage without spell support is the lowest in the game. The Bone Talisman (earliest catalyst) is found in Fallgrim's Central Tower — prioritize acquiring it before attempting any boss as Solomon.
