Smoldering Mace Overview — The Inferno Hammer

The Smoldering Mace is Mortal Shell II's dedicated fire weapon — a massive warhammer wreathed in eternal flame, found in the Temple Grounds behind the Magma Gate (requires the Forge Key from Imrod's arena). At Tier 5, it deals 460 base charged heavy damage with an additional 8% fire DoT per tick over 6 seconds (3 ticks). Its charged heavy creates a 5-meter AoE shockwave that deals 60% of the charged heavy's damage to all enemies in range — the only weapon in the game with an innate AoE component.

The Smoldering Mace excels in two scenarios that challenge other heavy weapons: crowd control and sustained chip damage against evasive bosses. The AoE shockwave staggers nearby enemies even on a whiffed swing, making it the best mob-clearing weapon in the game. The fire DoT continues damaging bosses even when you are dodging or healing, providing consistent pressure that no other weapon can match.

The trade-off: stamina cost is steep at 32 per swing, and the 1.5s charged heavy windup, while faster than the Martyr's Blade (1.8s), still requires careful timing. The Smoldering Mace also has zero parry capability — you cannot parry with this weapon. You trade defense for relentless offense.

Fire DoT Mechanics — Burn, Ignite & AoE Shockwave

The Smoldering Mace's fire damage operates on three layers:

1. Direct Fire Damage: Every swing deals split physical/fire damage (60/40 ratio). Enemies weak to fire (Crucix, Tarsus, Corvid) take +15-25% bonus damage from the fire portion.

2. Burn DoT: Each hit applies a 6-second burn. At Tier 5, this burns for 8% of the hit's damage every 2 seconds (3 ticks = 24% total bonus damage per hit). Burn stacks refresh duration but do not stack intensity. A charged heavy dealing 460 direct damage also deals ~110 bonus damage over 6 seconds from burn ticks.

3. Ignite AoE Shockwave: Charged heavies release a 5-meter circular shockwave dealing 60% of the charged heavy's damage (276 at Tier 5) to ALL enemies in range, plus applying full burn to each. This is the Smoldering Mace's defining feature — against The Dark Father's Phase 3 clones (4-5 enemies clustered), a single charged heavy can hit all of them for 460 + 276 AoE = 736 damage to the primary target and 276 to each clone. Total damage output in a multi-target scenario can exceed 1600 per swing.

Pro Insight: The AoE shockwave hits through walls and pillars. In the Crypt of Martyrs and Shifting Archives, you can charge a heavy outside a doorway and hit enemies on the other side without exposing yourself. This is the safest way to clear trap rooms and ambush spawns.

Optimal Combo Strings & AoE Clearing

Single-target boss combo: Charged heavy → light → light (total 96 stamina). Applies burn 3 times. With Eredrim's damage multipliers, this string deals ~1100 direct + ~260 burn = ~1360 total in one punish window. Follow with Harden to recover stamina, repeat.

Mob clear combo: Charged heavy (AoE shockwave hits all enemies in 5m) → if any survive, charged heavy again. Two charged heavies clear most Fallgrim and Crypt trash packs. Burn finishes any stragglers.

Dark Father Phase 3 specific: Position yourself at the center of the clone cluster. Charged heavy → clones staggered → charged heavy again while they recover. Two AoE heavies hit all 5 targets for ~3200+ total damage across the encounter.

FAQ

Where is the Magma Gate key?

The Forge Key drops from Imrod in the Seat of Infinity. After defeating Imrod, the key appears on a pedestal at the back of his arena. Return to Temple Grounds and use the key on the large iron gate in the Foundry district (northeast section, marked by glowing orange lava flows). The Smoldering Mace rests on an anvil inside.

Does Fire Oil stack with Smoldering Mace's innate fire?

Yes, and it is highly recommended. Fire Oil adds +20% fire damage, which applies to both the direct hit AND the burn ticks. With Fire Oil active, a Tier 5 charged heavy deals 460 × 1.2 = 552 direct + (552 × 0.24) = 132 burn damage = 684 total per swing. Combined with Eredrim's Brutal Strikes, this reaches ~820 effective damage.

Is Smoldering Mace or Martyr's Blade better?

Smoldering Mace for: mobs, Tarsus, Crucix, The Dark Father. Martyr's Blade for: Imrod, single-target stagger chains, Harrow tank builds. The Smoldering Mace has slightly lower single-target DPS (burn DoT takes 6s to fully tick) but dramatically higher multi-target damage. Ideally, upgrade both and swap based on the encounter.

Can the AoE shockwave friendly-fire?

No. The shockwave only damages enemies. You can swing freely in tight corridors without worrying about NPCs or co-op partners.

How does burn interact with boss healing mechanics?

Burn damage partially negates boss healing. Corvid's Shroud of Darkness has a passive 2% HP/sec regeneration. At Tier 5, Smoldering Mace burn deals ~8% × 3 ticks = 24% bonus damage over 6 seconds, which outpaces her 12% regen over the same period. The burn effectively cancels her healing and then some.

What is the best accessory for Smoldering Mace?

Firebrand Ring (+20% fire damage) is the definitive choice, found in Temple Grounds behind a fire-resistant door. Combined with Fire Oil (+20%), total fire damage bonus reaches +40% multiplicatively: 460 × 1.4 = 644 direct hit, 644 × 0.24 = 155 burn = 799 total per charged heavy. This is competitive with Martyr's Blade single-target damage while retaining the AoE advantage.
