1. Overview — How Secret Areas Work in Mortal Shell II

Mortal Shell II follows the proud Soulslike tradition of hiding substantial amounts of content behind illusory walls, breakable barriers, obscure ledge drops, and environmental puzzles. Across the five major zones — Fallgrim, Temple Grounds, Crypt of Martyrs, Seat of Infinity, and Shifting Archives — there are exactly 22 secret areas that contain unique weapons, consumables, upgrade materials, and in two cases, entire hidden boss encounters. Missing these secrets means missing roughly 15% of the game's total content, including two Shells and three unique weapons that cannot be acquired anywhere else.

How to Identify Secret Areas

Mortal Shell II uses five distinct mechanisms to hide its secrets. Learning to recognize each one is essential for a complete playthrough:

Pro Tip — The Glow Rule: Every secret area in Mortal Shell II has a subtle environmental tell. Look for faint amber or teal glow bleeding through cracks or around the edges of walls that seem slightly out of place. The developers placed a small light source behind every illusory wall — if you see colored light where there is no obvious source, attack that wall.

2. Fallgrim — 6 Secret Locations

Fallgrim, the sprawling opening zone, hides more secrets than any other area. Six hidden locations are scattered across the swamp, forest, and outpost perimeter. Some are accessible immediately; others require the Mist Key acquired from the Hadern fight.

2.1 Fallgrim Tower — Basement Armory (Illusory Wall)

Location: Inside Fallgrim Tower, ground floor, behind the staircase. Face the wall directly opposite the main entrance, three steps to the left of the stairwell.

Detection: Roll into the wall. If dust particles appear, it is real. If not — it is the illusory wall. Attack it with any weapon to dispel.

Contents: Smoldering Mace (weapon) — this is the earliest point you can acquire the Smoldering Mace without purchasing it. Also contains 2x Quenching Acid and 1x Glimpse of Futility.

Note: This secret is specifically designed as a tutorial for the illusory wall mechanic. The Sester Genessa in Fallgrim Tower will mention "walls that do not feel right" after your first visit, which is the game's hint.

2.2 The Poisoned Hollow (Breakable Barrier)

Location: From Fallgrim Tower, exit toward the swamp. Follow the left cliff wall until you find a cluster of three mushrooms near a cracked stone barricade.

Detection: The barricade has visible cracks and a faint amber glow behind it. You can also hear a distinct humming sound when nearby.

Contents: Tainted Nektar (consumable — permanently increases poison resistance by 8%), Ash-Walker Tar x3, and a Golden Bell (summons a spectral guide for 60 seconds).

Requirement: Must have cleared the Brigand camp nearby, as breaking the barrier draws aggro from two Swamp Leapers.

Warning — Poison Resistance Check: The Poisoned Hollow itself is a toxic zone that drains HP rapidly. You MUST have at least 60% poison resistance (via Tainted Nektar or armor) to survive long enough to loot everything. Entering without preparation will kill you before you can pick up the items.

2.3 The Brigand's Hoard (Ledge Drop)

Location: After clearing the Brigand camp (the large camp with the bonfire to the northeast of Fallgrim Tower), walk to the cliff edge behind the largest tent. Look down — there is a narrow ledge with two torches.

Contents: 3,000 Tar, Brigand's Key (opens the locked chest in Fallgrim Tower), and the Rusted Ring (equippable — increases item discovery by 5%).

2.4 The Petrified Grotto (Illusory Wall + Lever Puzzle)

Location: In the forest section between the Brigand camp and the Temple Grounds entrance, locate the petrified tree with a face carved into its trunk. Behind this tree, attack the rock wall.

Puzzle: Inside the grotto, there are three levers. The correct order is: Left → Right → Left. Incorrect sequence teleports you back to the grotto entrance.

Contents: Obsidian Shell Shard (unlocks the Hadern Shell early), Quenching Acid x3, and a Glimpse of Reverie.

2.5 The Sunken Shrine (Ledge Drop)

Location: In the deepest part of the swamp, where the water turns dark and movement speed is halved. Look for a stone archway partially submerged. Walk off the ledge behind the archway.

Contents: Martyr's Blade (weapon) — alternative acquisition. Also contains the Sealed Envelope, a key item required for an NPC questline. Without this item, you cannot complete Vlas's quest.

2.6 The Hermit's Cave (NPC Trigger)

Location: East of Fallgrim Tower, past the first Grisha encounter. A cave entrance is blocked by roots that retract only when you equip the Rusted Ring (from the Brigand's Hoard).

Contents: The Hermit NPC — a vendor who sells Quenching Acid (3,000 Tar each), Glimpses (5,000 Tar each), and the Withered Talisman (accessory — parry window +3 frames). Speak to him three times across different visits to unlock his full inventory.

For more on NPC interactions and questlines, see the Maps & Exploration Guide and the Beginner's Guide.

3. Temple Grounds — 4 Secret Locations

The Temple Grounds, the second major zone, blends verticality and atmospheric lighting to hide its secrets. Pay close attention to elevated ledges and walls that look out of place against the temple's symmetrical architecture.

3.1 The Ossuary Alcove (Illusory Wall)

Location: In the main temple hall with the kneeling statues, face the northern wall between the third and fourth statue. The wall texture is slightly lighter than its neighbors — attack it.

Contents: Hallowed Sword (if not already acquired), Glimpse of Wisdom x2, and the Prayer Beads accessory (increases Resolve generation by 8%).

3.2 The Sunbeam Chamber (Lever Puzzle)

Location: From the Ossuary Alcove, exit and turn right. Climb two flights of stairs to the chamber with the massive sundial. There are four levers around the room.

Puzzle: Pull levers in the order the sunbeam touches them as it moves across the floor: Northwest → Southwest → Southeast → Northeast. Each correct pull moves the sunbeam to the next position. Completing the sequence opens a trapdoor in the center of the sundial.

Contents: Solar Crest (key item for accessing Seat of Infinity early), Quenching Acid x2, and the rare Eclipsed Gem (weapon infusion — adds holy damage).

3.3 The Fallen Bell Tower (Ledge Drop)

Location: After defeating the Temple Knight miniboss on the upper ramparts, do NOT take the ladder down. Instead, walk to the broken section of the rampart to your left. Drop to the roof of the bell tower below.

Contents: Bell Keeper's Key, 2x Superior Moonshine, and the Ring of Echoes (accessory — dodge i-frames +2).

3.4 The Crypt-Keeper's Study (Illusory Wall)

Location: In the library room (northwest wing, identifiable by the scattered scrolls and broken lectern), attack the bookshelf on the eastern wall. It swings open like a door.

Contents: The Crypt-Keeper NPC who offers a side quest to retrieve three Lost Tome pages scattered across the Temple Grounds. Completing the quest rewards the Tome of the Forgotten (consumable — grants one free ability unlock). Also contains a Glimpse of Futility.

4. Crypt of Martyrs — 5 Secret Locations & 1 Hidden Boss

The Crypt of Martyrs is the densest zone for secrets. The catacomb-like layout with its winding corridors and repeating architecture is deliberately disorienting — perfect for hiding secrets in plain sight.

4.1 The Boneweaver's Lair (Illusory Wall)

Location: From the Crypt entrance bonfire, take the left corridor. After the second skeleton ambush, count six paces forward. The wall on your right is the illusory one — it lacks the moss texture present on all other walls in this hallway.

Contents: Boneweaver Set (armor — significantly reduces physical damage), Bone Shard x5, and the Necromancer's Eye (accessory — reveals hidden enemies on the minimap).

4.2 The Charnel Pit (Breakable Barrier)

Location: In the large circular chamber with the central sarcophagus (before the Tarsus boss arena), look for a cracked section of the western wall. It emits a faint green light.

Contents: Rotting Essence (weapon infusion — adds poison buildup to all attacks), Glimpse of Wisdom x1, and a Golden Bell.

Pro Tip — Maximizing the Rotting Essence: The poison buildup from Rotting Essence stacks with the Tiel Shell's inherent poison damage, creating a combined poison rate that procs in three hits against most enemies. This is one of the strongest PvE infusions in the game, especially in NG+ where enemies have bloated HP pools. See the Shell Compendium for Tiel build optimization.

4.3 The Martyr's Rest (Ledge Drop)

Location: After defeating Tarsus, backtrack to the boss arena. There is now an opening on the northern side where the ice wall melted. Drop down the revealed pit.

Contents: Tarsus's Shattered Blade (key item — can be reforged into a unique weapon variant at the Forge in Fallgrim Tower), the Frost Amulet (accessory — frost resistance +25%), and 5,000 Tar.

4.4 The Alderman's Vault (Illusory Wall)

Location: In the corridor leading to the Crypt Wraith ambush (the one with the collapsing floor), turn around and face the direction you came from. The wall directly ahead, flanked by two unlit torches, is illusory.

Contents: Hammer & Chisel (weapon) — alternative acquisition. Also contains Quenching Acid x3 and 2x Superior Moonshine.

4.5 The Forgotten Sepulcher (Hidden Boss — The Alderman)

Location: After discovering the Alderman's Vault (section 4.4), interact with the sarcophagus at the back of the room. This triggers a message: "The Alderman stirs." Exit the room and re-enter — the sarcophagus is now open, and The Alderman, a hidden miniboss, emerges.

Boss Details: The Alderman is a spectral knight wielding a rusted greatsword. He has approximately 4,500 HP and uses a mix of delayed horizontal cleaves and a rapid triple-thrust combo. His most dangerous attack is a charged overhead slam that creates a shockwave — dodge sideways, not backwards.

Rewards: Defeating the Alderman drops the Rusted Greatsword (unique weapon — cannot be upgraded but deals pure physical damage that bypasses all armor), the Alderman's Ring (accessory — +10% damage against spectral enemies), and Glimpse of Reverie x2.

Strategy: The Alderman is vulnerable to holy damage. If you acquired the Eclipsed Gem from the Temple Grounds Sunbeam Chamber, infuse your weapon before this fight. Harden through his triple-thrust combo and punish during his long recovery. Do not attempt to parry the shockwave slam — it is unparryable. For more boss strategies, see the Boss Guides.

5. Seat of Infinity — 3 Secret Locations

The Seat of Infinity is a vertical zone built around floating platforms and gravity hazards. Its secrets are the riskiest to access — every ledge drop in this zone can be fatal if you misjudge the landing.

5.1 The Astral Observatory (Illusory Wall)

Location: After riding the second gravity lift, you enter a circular chamber with a star-map projection on the ceiling. Face the constellation of three aligned stars on the southern wall — attack it.

Contents: Astral Prism (consumable — reveals all illusory walls in the current zone for 120 seconds), Glimpse of Wisdom x1, and the Stargazer's Cloak (armor piece — increases Tar gain by 15%).

5.2 The Void Bridge (Breakable Barrier)

Location: In the floating platform section, defeat the Temple Warden on the third platform. Look across the void — there is a barely visible shimmering bridge leading to a small island platform. The barrier blocking access is on your current platform's north edge.

Contents: Void Crystal (key item for the "Echoes of Nothing" achievement), Glimpse of Futility x2, and the Void-Touched Targe (off-hand — parry window +2 frames, but reduces max HP by 8%).

Danger — Fatal Fall: The Void Bridge is a one-way trip. After crossing, you must use a Homeward Bone or the Golden Bell to return. If you do not have either, you are trapped. Always carry at least one Homeward Bone before attempting this secret.

5.3 The Eternal Gallery (Illusory Wall)

Location: In the final chamber before the Imrod boss fight (the room with the four weapon displays), attack the empty fifth display pedestal on the eastern wall. It is an illusory wall.

Contents: Imrod's Ember (key item — used to upgrade fire-infused weapons by one tier beyond normal limits), Quenching Acid x4, and the King's Brand ring (accessory — fire resistance +20%).

6. Shifting Archives — 4 Secret Locations & 1 Hidden Boss

The Shifting Archives, the final explorable zone before the endgame sequence, uses temporal mechanics to hide its secrets. Some areas only become accessible during specific "time phases" triggered by lever pulls throughout the zone.

6.1 The Past Alcove (Time-Phase Illusory Wall)

Location: In the archive's central hub (the circular room with the grandfather clock), set the time phase to Past using the lever in the southwest corner. Then proceed to the eastern corridor. A wall that was solid in the Present phase is now an illusory wall.

Contents: Temporal Hourglass (consumable — freezes all enemies in the current room for 8 seconds), Glimpse of Wisdom x1, and the Hourglass Pendant (accessory — Harden cooldown reduced by 12%).

6.2 The Future Vault (Time-Phase Breakable Barrier)

Location: Set the time phase to Future and return to the central hub. The previously intact bookshelf on the northern wall is now decayed and breakable.

Contents: Future-Sight Lens (accessory — reveals enemy attack hitboxes as red outlines for 3 seconds after dodging), Quenching Acid x2, and the Tome of Eternity (consumable — resets all ability points for one Shell).

6.3 The Archivists' Quarters (Illusory Wall)

Location: After the first Archives Warden encounter, proceed down the hallway lined with bookshelves. The third bookshelf on the left has a book protruding slightly — pull it (interact button) to open a hidden passage.

Contents: The Head Archivist NPC who sells rare consumables: Superior Moonshine (3,000 Tar), Golden Bell (5,000 Tar), and the Scroll of Unmaking (10,000 Tar — removes all infusions from a weapon, refunding half the materials).

6.4 The Chronos Chamber (Hidden Boss — The Archivist Prime)

Location: After purchasing the Scroll of Unmaking from the Head Archivist (section 6.3), speak to him again and select the "What are you protecting?" dialogue option. He gives you the Temporal Key. Return to the central hub, set the time phase to Past, and use the Temporal Key on the grandfather clock.

Boss Details: The Archivist Prime is a time-manipulating boss with approximately 6,200 HP. He has two phases:

  • Phase 1 (100%-60% HP): Uses rapid book-throw projectiles (dodge sideways), a clock-hand slash (parryable), and a temporal slow field (run out of its radius — it has a 3-second windup).
  • Phase 2 (below 60% HP): Splits into two temporal echoes that attack simultaneously. Only the echo with a blue outline takes real damage; the red-outline echo is invulnerable. The echoes swap outlines every 8 seconds.

Rewards: Defeating the Archivist Prime drops the Chrono Staff (unique weapon — a casting weapon that fires slow-moving temporal orbs; cannot be parried by enemies), the Archivist's Cowl (armor — increases ability damage by 18%), and Glimpse of Reverie x3.

Pro Tip — The Chrono Staff Combo: The Chrono Staff's temporal orbs can be fired while Harden is active. This means you can Harden through an enemy combo while simultaneously charging and releasing orbs, dealing damage from complete safety. Pair with the Hourglass Pendant from section 6.1 for a Harden cooldown of approximately 6.5 seconds. This build turns the Chrono Staff into the safest boss-killing weapon in the game. See the Weapons Catalog for full stats.

7. Hidden Bosses — Complete Encounter & Reward Guide

Mortal Shell II has exactly two hidden bosses that are not part of the main story path. Both are completely missable, and neither is required for any achievement — they exist purely for the challenge and their unique reward loot.

Which Hidden Boss to Tackle First

The Alderman is accessible much earlier (as soon as you reach the Crypt of Martyrs) and is significantly easier. Defeat him first for the Rusted Greatsword, which deals pure physical damage and is excellent against the Archivist Prime's phase 2 echoes (since armor-bypassing damage affects both echoes equally). The recommended order is: Alderman → acquire Rusted Greatsword → Archivist Prime.

9. Frequently Asked Questions

Are any secret areas permanently missable?

Yes. The Shifting Archives secret areas (sections 6.1-6.4) are permanently inaccessible once you defeat The Dark Father and trigger the ending sequence. All other secret areas remain accessible until you enter NG+. If you are trophy-hunting or want to collect every unique item, complete the Shifting Archives secrets before the final boss. Additionally, the Alderman hidden boss in the Crypt of Martyrs becomes inaccessible if you defeat Tarsus without triggering the sarcophagus interaction first.

Do I need a specific weapon to break illusory walls?

No. Any melee attack — light, heavy, or weapon art — will dispel illusory walls. Rolling into them also works. However, breakable barriers (the cracked walls with visible damage) require a heavy attack or a weapon art; light attacks will bounce off. If a wall doesn't break on the first hit, switch to a heavy attack before assuming it is a real wall.

How much total Tar can I earn from secret areas?

Excluding enemy drops and boss rewards, the secret areas contain a combined total of 28,000 Tar in direct pickups. Including the two hidden boss Tar drops (8,000 + 12,000), the total reaches 48,000 Tar — enough to purchase roughly 16 Quenching Acids or 9 Glimpses from vendors. For maximizing your Tar income, see the Tar & Glimpses Farming Guide.

Are the hidden bosses required for any achievements or trophies?

No. Neither The Alderman nor The Archivist Prime are required for any achievement or trophy in Mortal Shell II. They exist purely as optional challenge encounters with unique rewards. However, if you are seeking the platinum trophy, collecting the unique weapons from these bosses (Rusted Greatsword, Chrono Staff) counts toward the "Armory" achievement for acquiring all weapons. See the complete Trophy Guide for full achievement requirements.

Can I revisit secret areas after entering NG+?

All secret areas reset in NG+, and all unique items within them respawn. This means you can acquire duplicates of one-per-playthrough items like the Tainted Nektar and Obsidian Shell Shard, stacking their permanent bonuses. A full clear of all 22 secret areas in NG+ will grant you a second Tainted Nektar (bringing total poison resistance to +16%) and a second Obsidian Shell Shard (allowing Hadern Shell unlock even earlier if you missed it the first time). See the New Game Plus Guide for details on what carries over.

Do the secret areas change in NG+?

The locations of all secret areas remain identical in NG+. However, enemy placements within three secret areas change at NG+3 and above: The Boneweaver's Lair (section 4.1) adds a Crypt Wraith patrolling the entrance corridor; the Charnel Pit (section 4.2) spawns two additional Swamp Leapers; and the Chronos Chamber (section 6.4) gives The Archivist Prime a third temporal echo in Phase 2 instead of two. The puzzle solutions (lever sequences, item triggers) remain unchanged across all NG+ cycles.

Leave No Stone Unturned

The true depth of Mortal Shell II lies in its secrets. Explore every corner, attack every suspicious wall, and claim the hidden treasures that most players never find.
