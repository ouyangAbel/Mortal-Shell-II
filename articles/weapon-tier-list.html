How We Rank Weapons — Criteria Explained

Choosing the best weapon in Mortal Shell II is not as simple as picking the one with the highest raw damage. A weapon's true power comes from the intersection of multiple factors, and understanding these criteria is essential before diving into the tier list. Every weapon in the game has strengths — our ranking evaluates how those strengths perform across the widest range of scenarios, from Fallgrim trash mobs to The Dark Father on NG+7.

We evaluate each weapon on five core dimensions:

  • DPS (Damage Per Second): Not just raw damage per hit, but damage output over time accounting for attack speed, combo length, and recovery frames. A weapon that hits for 150 damage at 1.2 attacks/sec out-damages one hitting for 250 at 0.6 attacks/sec.
  • Stamina Efficiency: How much damage does each stamina point buy? Weapons with low stamina cost per combo allow longer offensive windows and safer stamina management — critical in Mortal Shell II where the One-Bar Rule governs survival.
  • Range & Hitbox: Longer weapons control more space, interrupt enemy approaches, and punish from safer distances. Hitbox width matters for crowd control in Fallgrim and the Crypt of Martyrs.
  • Move Set Versatility: Does the weapon have horizontal sweeps for groups? Thrusts for narrow corridors? Launchers for aerial combos? A versatile move set handles every situation.
  • Shell Synergy: How well does the weapon pair with each Shell's passive abilities? Eredrim's Brutal Strikes amplifies heavy weapons; Tiel's agility favors fast weapons. The best weapon for your build depends on your Shell.
Pro Insight: Weapon upgrade level significantly impacts rankings. A +5 C-tier weapon can outperform a +0 S-tier weapon. This tier list assumes weapons at comparable upgrade levels (+3 to +5). See our Weapon Upgrade Guide for material farming routes.

S-Tier Weapons — The Meta Picks

Hallowed Sword

The Hallowed Sword is the most well-rounded weapon in Mortal Shell II and the reason it tops our tier list. Its light attack chain is fast (4 hits in ~2.8 seconds), its heavy attack has surprising range for a straight sword, and its stamina cost is among the lowest in the game. But what truly elevates the Hallowed Sword to S-tier is its parry compatibility — one of only three weapons that can parry, giving it a defensive option no greatsword or mace can match.

At +5 with a Fire enchantment, the Hallowed Sword's DPS rivals weapons with much higher raw damage numbers because of its speed advantage. It works with every Shell: Tiel appreciates the fast combos, Eredrim benefits from the parry window, Solomon pairs it with Resolve abilities, and Harrow uses it as a safe, consistent option.

Best For: Every build. The Hallowed Sword has no weakness. If you are unsure what to use, use this. See our Hallowed Sword Upgrade Guide for the full breakdown.

Smoldering Mace

The Smoldering Mace is the best heavy weapon in the game and the undisputed king of burst damage. Its charged heavy attack deals the single highest hit damage of any weapon — enough to stagger-lock most regular enemies and take 12-15% of a boss's health bar in one swing. The innate fire buildup means every hit stacks burning, adding damage-over-time that compensates for the slower attack speed.

Where the Mace truly shines is in Harden-Cancel combos. Start a heavy swing, Harden through an enemy attack, and your swing resumes at full power — effectively dealing massive damage with zero risk. Eredrim's Brutal Strikes passive (+20% charged attack damage) turns the Smoldering Mace into a boss-killing machine. Against Tarsus and Imrod, charged heavies to the back deal over 500 damage per swing at +5.

A-Tier Weapons — Excellent Choices

Martyr's Blade

The Martyr's Blade trades the Hallowed Sword's versatility for raw reach and crowd control. Its wide sweeping attacks cover a 180-degree arc in front of you, making it the best weapon for clearing groups in Fallgrim and the Crypt of Martyrs. The innate frost buildup slows enemies on hit, reducing their attack speed and making every subsequent dodge easier.

Where the Martyr's Blade falls slightly below S-tier is its stamina consumption. Full combos eat 70-80% of a base stamina bar, meaning you must commit the One-Bar Rule aggressively. With Tiel Shell's large stamina pool or Solomon's stamina recovery passive, this weakness is mitigated. For Harrow and Eredrim users, the Martyr's Blade demands disciplined hit-and-run tactics rather than sustained combos.

Abyssal Siphon

The Abyssal Siphon is the most unique weapon in Mortal Shell II — a whip-scythe hybrid that steals health on hit. Every successful attack restores 3-5% of your max HP, turning offense into defense. Against regular enemies, this sustain makes you nearly immortal. Against bosses, the healing per hit is smaller but still meaningful over a 4-minute fight.

The trade-off is range dependency. The Siphon's optimal range is mid-range (2-4 meters), and its damage drops sharply at close quarters where the whip cannot fully extend. Mastering spacing is mandatory. In the hands of a skilled player who understands enemy attack ranges, the Abyssal Siphon is arguably S-tier. For most players, the spacing requirement keeps it firmly in A. See our full Abyssal Siphon Guide for advanced techniques.

Hadern's Greatsword

Hadern's Greatsword is the premier stagger weapon. Its heavy attacks break enemy poise faster than any other weapon, interrupting boss attack chains that other weapons cannot. Against Hadern, Crucix, and The Unchained, a well-timed heavy can cancel their combo entirely — a defensive tool disguised as an offensive weapon.

The greatsword's weakness is its slow recovery. Miss a heavy attack and you are locked into ~1.8 seconds of recovery frames — an eternity against fast bosses like Corvid. The solution is to never swing without Harden backup. Start your heavy, Harden through any retaliation, and let the swing complete. This playstyle is stamina-intensive but devastatingly effective.

B-Tier Weapons — Solid & Reliable

Eredrim's Warhammer

A pure damage stick with the highest single-hit physical damage in the game outside of charged Smoldering Mace attacks. The Warhammer excels at one thing: hitting things very hard, very slowly. Its move set is limited (mostly overhead slams and horizontal sweeps), and its stamina cost is punishing. Pair exclusively with Eredrim Shell for maximum charged attack damage, or use as a secondary weapon for weapon-swap combos — open with the Warhammer's stagger, switch to a fast weapon for the finish.

Solomon's Catalyst

The Catalyst is a hybrid melee/casting weapon that unlocks Resolve-based abilities. Its melee damage is mediocre, but it enables Solomon Shell's full magical toolkit: ranged fireballs, frost nova, and healing wards. If you are playing a Solomon Mage Build, the Catalyst is mandatory. For any other build, it is not worth the weapon slot.

Dual Daggers

The fastest weapon class in Mortal Shell II. Dual Daggers attack at roughly 2.5 hits per second, stacking elemental enchantments and on-hit effects faster than anything else. The problem: each hit deals minimal damage, and the range is abysmal (under 1.5 meters). You must be inside an enemy's attack range to deal damage, which means constant Harden usage to survive. Best as a secondary weapon for swap combos — stagger with a heavy weapon first, then switch to daggers for rapid finisher damage.

C-Tier Weapons — Niche Picks

Bone Halberd

The longest weapon in the game with excellent range, but its damage is mediocre and its move set is predictable. The Halberd's thrust-heavy combo is perfect for narrow corridors (Crypt of Martyrs), but most of Mortal Shell II's combat happens in open arenas where its range advantage is less impactful. A viable pick for specific encounters but outclassed in general use.

Tiel's Thorn

A fast, poison-coated dagger designed for the Tiel Assassin playstyle. The poison buildup is useful against high-HP enemies, but the raw damage is the lowest of any weapon. Requires perfect play — you must maintain aggression to keep poison ticking while dodging everything because you cannot afford to trade hits. High skill ceiling, low floor. See our Tiel Assassin Build Guide for the full strategy.

Brigand's Cleaver

An early-game weapon that gets outclassed by every weapon above it once upgrades begin. The Cleaver has acceptable damage and speed but no special properties — no elemental buildup, no parry, no healing, no stagger advantage. It exists to teach you the basics. Replace it as soon as you find any weapon in B-tier or above.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best weapon in Mortal Shell II for beginners?

The Hallowed Sword. It has the best balance of speed, damage, stamina efficiency, and defensive utility (parry). It works with every Shell and requires no special playstyle to be effective. Start with the Hallowed Sword, learn the game's mechanics, then experiment with specialized weapons once you understand boss patterns and your preferred playstyle.

Is the Smoldering Mace better than the Hallowed Sword?

It depends on your playstyle and Shell. The Smoldering Mace deals more burst damage per hit and pairs better with Eredrim Shell's charged attack bonus. The Hallowed Sword has higher sustained DPS, lower stamina cost, and parry capability. For boss speedkills, the Mace wins. For general progression and survivability, the Sword wins. Most top players carry both and swap situationally.

What weapon has the highest damage per hit in Mortal Shell II?

Eredrim's Warhammer has the highest raw single-hit damage on a normal attack. The Smoldering Mace's fully charged heavy attack deals the highest single-hit damage in the game when paired with Eredrim Shell's Brutal Strikes passive (+20% charged damage). Against a vulnerable boss back, this combination can exceed 500 damage in one swing at +5 upgrade level.

Does weapon upgrade level matter more than weapon choice?

Up to a point, yes. A +5 Brigand's Cleaver out-damages a +0 Hallowed Sword. However, once all weapons are at comparable upgrade levels (+3 to +5), weapon choice becomes the dominant factor. Prioritize upgrading your primary weapon first, then worry about tier rankings. See our Weapon Upgrade Guide for efficient material farming.

Which weapon is best for New Game Plus in Mortal Shell II?

For NG+ and beyond, the Smoldering Mace and Hallowed Sword remain S-tier because their core strengths (burst damage and versatility, respectively) scale well with increased enemy HP. The Abyssal Siphon rises in value on higher NG+ cycles because its health steal provides sustain against enemies that hit harder and take longer to kill. Avoid C-tier weapons on NG+3 and above — the damage penalty becomes too severe.

Can I beat Mortal Shell II with any weapon?

Yes. Every weapon in the game is viable for completing the main story and all bosses. Tier rankings reflect efficiency and ease of use, not viability. Players have completed NG+7 no-hit runs with Brigand's Cleaver. Skill, knowledge of boss patterns, and proper Shell pairing matter far more than weapon choice. Pick the weapon that feels right for your playstyle and master it.
