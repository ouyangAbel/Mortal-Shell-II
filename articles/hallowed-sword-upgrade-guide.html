Hallowed Sword Overview — The Reliable Standard

The Hallowed Sword is Mortal Shell II's starting weapon and the most versatile blade in the game. Found in Fallgrim's Central Tower within the first 15 minutes of gameplay, it serves as the baseline by which all other weapons are measured. Its defining characteristics: fast attack speed (1.0s light combo, 1.2s charged heavy), wide parry window (18 active frames — the second widest in the game behind only the Sanctified Mace shield bash), and balanced stamina consumption (15 per light swing, 28 per heavy).

What makes the Hallowed Sword special is not raw damage — at Tier 5, its 380 base charged heavy damage is mid-tier. The strength is in its flexibility. It performs well with every Shell, supports every infusion type, and has the lowest stat requirements of any weapon in the game. You can take the Hallowed Sword from the tutorial to The Dark Father and never feel underpowered, provided you upgrade it consistently.

Pro Insight: The Hallowed Sword's parry window is its true endgame asset. At 18 active frames, it is the easiest weapon to parry with. Combined with Solomon's Resolve generation from successful parries (1 full bar per parry), the Hallowed Sword becomes a Resolve engine for spell-heavy builds. More on this in the Parry & Resolve Guide.

Total materials to Tier 5: 16× Iron Shard, 9× Steel Ingot, 9× Tempered Alloy, 1× Pure Titanite Slab, 17,200 Tar. This is significantly cheaper than heavy weapons (Martyr's Blade: 26,000 Tar). The Hallowed Sword is the most economical weapon to max out.

Stamina cost reduction: Note that stamina per light swing drops from 18 to 15 as you upgrade. This is unique to the Hallowed Sword — other weapons do not get stamina efficiency improvements from upgrades. At Tier 5, you get one extra swing per full stamina bar compared to Tier 0.

If you use Harrow or Eredrim (slow, 2-3 hit combos), Frostbite may never proc during a punish window. Choose Fire instead for consistent value-per-swing. Parry Window Data — Hallowed Sword's Hidden Strength

The Hallowed Sword's 18-frame parry window is its defining endgame characteristic. Here is how it compares to other weapons:

WeaponActive Parry FramesNotes
Sanctified Mace (Shield)22Easiest parry. No damage return.
Hallowed Sword18Second easiest. Returns damage on successful parry.
Hallowed Daggers12Fastest recovery. Harder to time.
Shadow Fang10Tightest window. High risk, high reward.
Martyr's Blade8Minimal window. Parry not recommended.

At 18 frames (roughly 0.3 seconds at 60 FPS), the Hallowed Sword's parry window is 50% wider than daggers and over 2x wider than greatswords. This makes it the ideal "training wheels" weapon for learning boss parry timings. Once you can consistently parry every Hadern attack with Hallowed Sword, you have the muscle memory to attempt tighter windows with other weapons.

FAQ

Should I infuse Hallowed Sword or go Balanced?

If your primary Shell is Solomon: Holy Infusion. Harrow or Eredrim: Fire Infusion. Tiel: Balanced (the +5% crit stacks with Tiel's Keen Edge for a total +20% crit chance on back attacks). If you are unsure or plan to swap Shells frequently, Balanced is the safest choice since it has no elemental weaknesses.

Where can I find the Hallowed Sword if I missed it?

It is unmissable — it sits on an altar in the Central Tower of Fallgrim, directly in the path of the main quest. If you somehow walked past it, return to the Central Tower and check the altar room on the ground floor. It glows with a faint golden light and is the only interactable object in the room.

Is Hallowed Sword viable in NG+7?

Yes, but you will feel the lower damage compared to heavier weapons. In NG+7, a fully buffed Hallowed Sword charged heavy deals ~1346 damage with optimal stacking (vs Martyr's Blade's ~6350). The difference is you land 3-4 Hallowed Sword heavies in the time Martyr's Blade lands one. DPS is comparable; burst is lower. Hallowed Sword also retains its parry utility, which becomes more valuable as enemy damage increases.

What is the best consumable for Hallowed Sword?

Sharpening Stone (+15% crit chance) synergizes with Balanced infusion's +5% and Solomon's base crit. Fire Oil (+20% damage) pairs with Fire Infusion for maximum damage-per-swing. Frost Vial is unnecessary if you chose Frost Infusion — the weapon applies frost naturally.

Can I parry boss attacks with Hallowed Sword?

Yes, and this is the weapon's primary endgame niche. Every boss attack except grab attacks and AoEs is parryable with Hallowed Sword's 18-frame window. Hadern's 3-hit combo, Crucix's sword swings, and Tarsus's Frost Greatsword are all parryable. Successful parries generate 1 full Resolve bar for Solomon and stagger the boss for 1.5s. See the Parry & Resolve Guide for boss-specific parry timings.

Should I dual-wield Hallowed Swords?

Dual-wielding is possible (equip two Hallowed Swords in each weapon slot), but not recommended. The Hallowed Daggers are purpose-built for dual-wielding with faster combos and poison application. Dual Hallowed Swords deal more damage per hit but the combo speed is 30% slower than daggers — overall DPS is lower. Use one Hallowed Sword + one catalyst (for Solomon) or one Hallowed Sword + one heavier weapon (for Harrow/Eredrim swap combos).
