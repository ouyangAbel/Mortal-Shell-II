Why Eredrim? The Unstoppable Force Philosophy

Eredrim is Mortal Shell II's pure damage Shell — the heaviest hitter in the game. With the second-highest base HP (~200 at max), the highest stagger potential on every attack, and an ability tree that stacks damage multipliers multiplicatively, Eredrim deletes bosses in fewer attack cycles than any other Shell. Where Tiel lands 6 backstabs for 15,000 damage, Eredrim lands 3 charged heavies for the same 15,000 — and each hit staggers the boss out of their attack animation.

The Eredrim strength build abandons subtlety entirely. You do not dodge through attacks — you swing through them. Eredrim's stagger threshold bonuses mean your attacks interrupt enemy combos at a rate 2-3x higher than other Shells. Against humanoid enemies and most bosses, a well-timed charged heavy will cancel their attack and leave them vulnerable for a full combo. This is the closest Mortal Shell II gets to a "bully" playstyle.

The trade-off: Eredrim is slow. Base movement speed is the lowest in the game, stamina pool is limited (~100), and Harden cooldown is longer (~6.5s). You commit to every attack. But when a single charged heavy with full damage stacking hits for 1800+ damage, you do not need many attacks.

Pro Insight: Eredrim's stagger is not RNG — it operates on a hidden "poise damage" system. Each weapon deals a base poise damage value per hit. Eredrim's passive "Heavy Hands" multiplies poise damage by 1.4x. Combined with "Brutal Strikes" (+20% charged attack damage), a single charged Martyr's Blade heavy deals enough poise damage to stagger Hadern, Crucix, and Tarsus out of any non-super-armor animation.

Damage Stacking — How to Reach 3.5x Base Damage

Eredrim's damage output comes from multiplicative stacking across multiple categories. Here is the complete calculation for a charged heavy attack against a boss below 30% HP:

With a Martyr's Blade charged heavy dealing ~510 base damage, the full-stacked hit lands for ~1805 damage — about 22% of a boss's total HP in a single swing. On NG, most bosses die in 4-5 successful charged heavies. On NG+7, the kill count rises to 12-15, but the time-to-kill remains competitive because Executioner II accelerates the final 30% of every fight.

Warning: Rampage stacks reset on death or fast-travel. Before boss fights, clear the run-back area without dying to maintain 3 stacks. Rampage alone adds 15% damage — the difference between a 5-cycle kill and a 6-cycle kill. See the combat tips guide for run-back clearing strategies.

Stagger System Deep Dive — Thresholds & Chain Stuns

Every enemy in Mortal Shell II has a hidden "poise" value that depletes when hit. When poise reaches zero, the enemy staggers — interrupting their current action and opening a 1-2 second punish window. Eredrim, with Heavy Hands III, deals 60% more poise damage than baseline. Here are the stagger thresholds for key enemies:

Key insight: With Heavy Hands III, Eredrim can stagger The Unchained during Chaos Flurry. Land 2 charged heavies before the Flurry starts, and the third charged heavy mid-Flurry will break his poise — canceling the entire attack and leaving him open. This is the only Shell/build combination that can consistently interrupt Chaos Flurry through raw poise damage.

Execution Chains — The Brutal Strikes Loop

The optimal Eredrim combat loop against bosses:

  1. Harden-Bait opener: Walk in, Harden, let the boss hit you. Counter Strike is not in Eredrim's kit but the stagger from Harden recoil creates a 1.2s window.
  2. Charged heavy: Immediately begin charging. Colossus ensures you cannot be staggered during charge. Release when fully charged.
  3. Stagger confirms: If the boss staggers (poise depleted), you have ~1.5s. If your stamina permits, charge a second heavy. Crushing Blow (+25% vs staggered) applies to this follow-up.
  4. Harden regeneration: After 1-2 heavies, your stamina should be low (~20 remaining). Harden. Stamina regenerates during Harden. Enemy hits your shell and recoils.
  5. Repeat from step 2.

This loop works against every boss except Imrod (lava pool punishes stationary Harden). Against Imrod, substitute Harden with a dodge-roll and use single charged heavies rather than attempting the full loop.

Phase transition skip: Most bosses transition phases at HP thresholds (75%, 50%, 25%). Eredrim can sometimes skip phase transitions with a massive damage spike. If a boss is at 53% HP and your full-stacked charged heavy deals 22%, the boss drops from 53% to 31% — skipping the 50% phase trigger entirely. The Dark Father's Phase 2 (shadow clones at 50%) is the most valuable skip. Land a full-stacked heavy when he is at 52% HP to jump straight to Phase 3, bypassing the clone phase completely.

FAQ

Is Eredrim good for a first playthrough?

Yes, alongside Harrow. Eredrim teaches you patience and timing — you cannot spam attacks, so every swing must count. The high HP provides forgiveness for mistakes, and the damage output means fights end faster (fewer opportunities to die). If you enjoy slow, heavy-hitting playstyles, Eredrim is ideal.

How do I deal with fast bosses as Eredrim?

Corvid and Hadern are Eredrim's worst matchups. Against fast bosses, use Hallowed Sword (2H) instead of Martyr's Blade — the faster charged heavy (1.2s vs 1.8s) lands in the shorter punish windows. Also, do not attempt charged heavies during neutral phases. Wait for Harden-bait recoil windows where the boss is guaranteed to be stationary for 1.5s.

Is Berserker's Charm worth the risk?

For experienced players, yes. 10% extra damage at below 50% HP is significant in the stacking formula. Eredrim's base HP of 200+ means 50% HP is still 100 — more than Tiel's entire HP pool. You can function safely at half HP. For newer players, swap to Guardian's Ring (10% DR) instead.

What is the best consumable loadout for Eredrim?

Fire Oil (mandatory, +20% damage), Stamina Tonic (offsets low stamina), Healing Salve ×2 (high HP pool needs big heals). If using Martyr's Blade, the frost application is sufficient — skip Frost Vial. Sharpening Stone is unnecessary since Eredrim's damage comes from multipliers, not crit chance.

How do I maintain Rampage stacks before bosses?

Clear the boss run-back area without dying. Each kill adds 10 seconds to the Rampage timer and refreshes all stacks. The timer is generous — you can clear the run-back, walk to the fog gate, apply consumables, and still have 20-30 seconds of Rampage remaining. If the run-back is long (Shifting Archives to Dark Father), kill enemies along the way to refresh the timer.

Can Eredrim use the Annihilator execution on bosses?

Yes, and this is the capstone ability's primary use. At 10% HP, any charged heavy that connects will trigger an instant-kill animation — even on The Dark Father. This skips the final 10% of every boss fight, which is typically their most dangerous phase. In NG+7, where bosses gain new attack patterns at low HP, Annihilator is the single best ability in the game.
