Why Farm Tar & Glimpses?

Tar is the universal currency of Mortal Shell II — you need it for weapon upgrades, Shell ability unlocks, consumable stockpiles, and key vendor purchases. Glimpses are even more precious: each Shell has a deep ability tree, and fully unlocking just one Shell costs dozens of Glimpses.

If you're hitting a wall at a boss, or can't afford that next weapon upgrade tier, the right farming route turns an hour of frustration into 10 minutes of progress. This guide covers every viable farming spot — some yield over 3,000 Tar per run in the late game.

Related: Beginner's Guide for core mechanics, Best Builds to spend your farmed resources wisely.

How Tar & Glimpses Work

Tar drops from defeated enemies and can be found as consumable items throughout the world. You lose all carried Tar on death, but you have one chance to retrieve it by returning to your death location — exactly like Souls in Dark Souls. If you die again before retrieval, it's gone forever.

Glimpses are earned primarily from boss kills and special Glimpse consumables found in the world. Unlike Tar, Glimpses are not lost on death — spend them at a Shell statue as soon as you have enough for an ability unlock. Some elite enemies also drop Glimpses, which is the basis for targeted farming.

Key Rule: Never carry more Tar than you're willing to lose. Bank it at a Shell statue (by purchasing abilities or items) before attempting dangerous sections.

Best Early Game Farming Spots

Fallgrim Outskirts — Brigand Loop

Availability: From the start of the game (after tutorial)
Yield: ~180-220 Tar per run | ~2 min per run
Glimpses: Rare drop from Brigand Captains

From the Fallgrim Central Shell statue, head east toward the ruined gatehouse. You'll encounter:

  1. 2 Brigands (60 Tar each)
  2. 1 Brigand Captain (80 Tar, small chance of Glimpse)
  3. 2 more Brigands near the wooden barricade

Loop back to the statue, rest to respawn enemies, and repeat. This is the safest early farm — the enemies are slow, telegraphed, and die to 3-4 hits from any starting weapon. Use it to afford your first weapon upgrade and a stack of healing items before your first boss.

Fallgrim Swamp — Leaper Pack

Availability: After reaching Fallgrim Swamp
Yield: ~300-350 Tar per run | ~2.5 min per run
Glimpses: Occasional from Leaper Alphas

From the Swamp Lookout statue, head south into the mist. A pack of 4-5 Leapers spawns near the broken tower. Leapers are fragile — a single Harden-counter kills them outright. The Alpha variant drops more Tar and occasionally a Glimpse. This route slightly edges out the Brigand loop on Tar-per-minute once you're comfortable with Leaper attack patterns.

Best Mid Game Farming Spots

Crypt of the Damned — Wraith Hallway

Availability: After defeating Crucix, the Twiceborn
Yield: ~600-750 Tar per run | ~3 min per run
Glimpses: 1 guaranteed per 3-4 runs (Wraith Knights)

This is the first farming route that meaningfully accelerates your build. From the Crypt Depths statue, take the left corridor into the Hall of Martyrs. The route:

  1. 3 Wraiths in the hallway (90 Tar each)
  2. 1 Wraith Knight at the end (180 Tar, ~25% Glimpse drop rate)
  3. 2 more Wraiths in the side chamber (90 Tar each)

The Wraith Knight is the prize — it has the highest Glimpse drop rate of any non-boss enemy in the mid game. Tip: Use a Greatsword here; the wide sweep one-shots Wraith packs and staggers the Knight on the second hit.

Frozen Sanctum — Frost Guard Patrol

Availability: After reaching Frozen Sanctum
Yield: ~500-650 Tar per run | ~2.5 min per run
Glimpses: Low

From the Sanctum Gate statue, follow the wall toward the ice bridge. You'll encounter 4 Frost Guards in sequence. Each drops 120-140 Tar. The route is linear with no branching — perfect for braindead farming while listening to a podcast. Faster than the Crypt route in pure Tar-per-minute, but worse for Glimpses.

Pro Tip: Equip the Gilded Mask consumable (drops from Crypt chests) to boost Tar gain by 30% for 3 minutes. Pop it right before a farming session to maximize the Crypt or Sanctum route.

Best Late Game Farming Spots

Watcher's Spire — Ascendant Gauntlet

Availability: After reaching Watcher's Spire upper levels
Yield: ~1,200-1,500 Tar per run | ~4 min per run
Glimpses: 1-2 per run (Ascendants + Watcher Knights)

This is the best all-around farming route in the game. From the Spire Apex statue, descend through the gauntlet:

  1. 2 Ascendant Priests (200 Tar each, high Glimpse drop rate)
  2. 1 Watcher Knight (280 Tar, guaranteed Glimpse on first kill per cycle)
  3. 3 Watcher Adepts in the library (140 Tar each)
  4. 1 more Ascendant Priest at the balcony

The route loops perfectly — by the time you clear the balcony, the statue is a 15-second sprint away. With the Gilded Mask active, this route produces over 1,800 Tar per run.

Abyssal Rift — Abyssal Knight Farm

Availability: Late game, after unlocking the Rift
Yield: ~2,500-3,200 Tar per run | ~6 min per run
Glimpses: 2-4 per run

The highest raw yield in the game, but also the most dangerous. From the Rift Entrance statue, clear the Abyssal Knight patrol path:

  1. 1 Abyssal Knight (450 Tar, 50% Glimpse drop)
  2. 2 Abyssal Hounds (180 Tar each)
  3. 2 Abyssal Knights in the throne room (450 Tar each)

This route is for endgame builds only — the Abyssal Knights hit hard enough to two-shot most Shells. Bring a maxed weapon and at least 4 healing items. The payoff is unmatched: 3,000+ Tar in under 7 minutes, plus enough Glimpses to unlock a full ability tier in 3-4 runs.

Dedicated Glimpses Farming

Method 1: Boss Remembrance (Most Efficient)

After defeating any major boss, you can use the Remembrance item at a Shell statue to refight the boss. Each refight awards 2-3 Glimpses on victory. The most efficient boss to farm:

  • Imrod, the Unrepentant — Fastest kill time (~90 seconds with a +8 weapon), awards 3 Glimpses per clear
  • Tarsus, the First Martyr — Slightly longer but more predictable patterns, 3 Glimpses

With practice, you can clear Imrod in under 2 minutes per cycle for roughly 90 Glimpses per hour — enough to nearly max a Shell's entire tree.

Method 2: Watcher Knight Loop

The Watcher Knight in the Spire Apex route (see above) drops a Glimpse on the first kill of each farming cycle. Combined with Ascendant Priest drops, this nets 2-3 Glimpses per 4-minute run while also producing substantial Tar — the most time-efficient dual-purpose farm.

What to Buy First — Spending Priority

Having thousands of Tar doesn't help if you spend it on the wrong things. Here's the optimal spending order:

  1. Weapon upgrades — The single biggest power spike. A +5 weapon does roughly 40% more damage than base. Prioritize your main weapon before anything else.
  2. Core Shell abilities — Unlock the first 2-3 nodes in your Shell's tree. These are cheap (1-2 Glimpses each) and provide immediate survivability gains.
  3. Healing consumables — Maintain a stack of at least 6 Weltcaps or Roasted Rats. Buy them from the merchant in Fallgrim if you're low.
  4. Max Shell tree — Once your weapon is +8 or higher, dump Glimpses into finishing your Shell's tree for the powerful capstone abilities.
  5. Secondary weapons — Only upgrade a backup weapon after your primary is maxed. Situational weapons (like a strike weapon for armored enemies) are worth bringing to +5.

See the Builds guide for specific Shell + weapon combinations that maximize your investment.

Advanced Farming Tips

Use the Gilded Mask Strategically

The Gilded Mask item boosts Tar gain by 30% for 3 minutes. Don't pop it randomly — activate it right as you approach the first enemy of your route. With the Spire Gauntlet route, that 30% bonus translates to an extra ~400 Tar per run. Gilded Masks are found in Crypt chests and purchasable from the late-game merchant for 500 Tar each — they pay for themselves in one good run.

Stack Shell Passive Bonuses

Certain Shells have passives that improve farming efficiency:

  • Solomon — +15% Tar from all sources (unlock node 4). The single best farming Shell.
  • Genessa — Passive HP regen reduces healing item consumption during long sessions.
  • Azuran — Ranged attacks let you pull enemies safely in dangerous zones like the Abyssal Rift.

Don't Skip Respawn Manipulation

Enemies respawn when you rest at a Shell statue. To minimize downtime between runs:

  • Clear the route, sprint back to the statue, rest, and immediately sprint out
  • Kill enemies near the statue last so the corpse run is shorter
  • If a route has a one-way drop, plan your path so you end near the statue

When to Stop Farming

Diminishing returns hit hard. Here's when to move on:

  • Early game: Farm until your weapon is +3 and you have 6 healing items. Move on.
  • Mid game: Farm until your weapon is +6 and your Shell has 5+ abilities unlocked.
  • Late game: Farm until your weapon is maxed. Glimpses for secondary Shells are optional.

Over-farming burns you out and ruins the game's pacing. Mortal Shell II is balanced so that natural progression provides enough Tar for a focused build. Farm only when you're genuinely stuck or want to experiment with a second Shell.
