What Is New Game Plus?

After defeating The Dark Father and watching the credits roll, Mortal Shell II presents you with a choice: start Journey 2 (New Game Plus) immediately, or remain in your current world to finish collecting items before transitioning. NG+ restarts the story from Fallgrim while preserving most of your hard-earned progress — but at a price: every enemy hits harder, has more health, and some nasty surprises await.

Mortal Shell II's NG+ system is more nuanced than most soulslikes. It doesn't just inflate numbers — certain new enemy variants appear exclusively in NG+, bosses gain additional attack strings, and some items relocate. The game supports up to NG+7 (Journey 8), at which point scaling stops increasing. Every cycle beyond that remains at NG+7 difficulty.

What Carries Over — The Complete List

Understanding exactly what comes with you into the next cycle is critical for preparation. Here is the definitive carryover list:

Category Carries Over? Details
All Weapons Yes Every weapon in your inventory stays, including upgrade levels and infused enchantments
All Shells Yes All unlocked Shells remain available with their full ability trees intact
Shell Abilities Yes Every purchased ability node persists — no need to re-unlock anything
Tar (Currency) Yes All carried Tar transfers. Deposit excess at a Shell statue before transitioning for safety
Glimpses Yes All Glimpses carry over — spend them before NG+ if you want immediate ability unlocks
Consumable Items Yes Mortal Tokens, healing items, resins, buffs — your entire inventory bag travels with you
Quenching Acids Yes Weapon upgrade materials carry over; NG+ also adds new acid sources
Familiarity (Weapon) Yes Weapon familiarity unlocks persist — your maxed-out Hallowed Sword stays maxed
Player Level Yes Your overall progress and stats carry forward; no stat reset occurs
Tainted Nektar Yes If you've collected any, they persist across cycles for alternate endings
Pro Insight: Before entering NG+, visit every vendor and buy out their stock. Consumables and upgrade materials are cheaper in NG than NG+, and some vendors' inventories change between cycles. Stockpile Roasted Rats, Superior Moonshine, and Ethereal Dried Meat — you'll burn through healing in later cycles.

What You Lose — What Resets

Just as important as what carries over is knowing what disappears. These items and states reset completely:

  • Key Items & Quest Items: All keys, quest-relevant items (Gland of Sight, Sacred Incense, etc.), and story-progression items are removed from your inventory.
  • Map Fog & Fast Travel Points: The world map re-fogs entirely. Every fast travel point must be re-discovered by visiting its location.
  • NPC Quest Progress: All NPC questlines reset to their initial state. If you were mid-way through Sester Genessa's or Vlas's quest, you start fresh.
  • Boss Defeats: All bosses respawn. This is the primary source of additional Glimpses in subsequent cycles.
  • Shortcuts & Doors: All opened shortcuts (Fallgrim Tower gates, Temple Grounds passages, Crypt levers) reset to their default locked/closed positions.
  • Weapon & Shell Pickups: Duplicate weapon and Shell locations in the world still contain their items — pick them up again for a small Tar bonus (significantly reduced from first-acquisition value).
  • Lore Scrolls & Collectibles: Lore items and journal entries remain in your collection log but physically respawn in the world.

Difficulty Scaling — How Enemies Change

Mortal Shell II's NG+ scaling follows a curve that increases enemy health and damage multiplicatively per cycle. Here's the breakdown:

Cycle Enemy HP Multiplier Enemy Damage Multiplier Tar Drop Bonus Glimpse Drop Bonus
NG (Journey 1) 1.0x 1.0x — —
NG+1 1.4x 1.35x +40% +25%
NG+2 1.7x 1.65x +65% +40%
NG+3 2.0x 1.95x +85% +55%
NG+4 2.3x 2.2x +105% +65%
NG+5 2.55x 2.45x +120% +75%
NG+6 2.75x 2.65x +135% +80%
NG+7 (Max) 2.9x 2.8x +150% +85%

The jump from NG to NG+1 is the most dramatic — a 40% HP and 35% damage spike that catches unprepared players off guard. NG+2 through NG+5 add roughly 0.2-0.3x per cycle, tapering off at NG+7. Note that boss HP scaling is slightly higher than regular enemies: roughly +0.15x above the listed multiplier for major bosses.

Warning: From NG+3 onward, many regular enemy attacks become one-shot kills against low-Vigor builds. The Eredrim Shell's massive HP pool becomes increasingly valuable. Players relying on Tiel's mobility may need to switch to Harrow or Eredrim for survivability around this cycle threshold.

New Enemy Placements & Surprises

Cold Symmetry didn't just inflate numbers for NG+. Starting in NG+1, you'll encounter new enemy variants and adjusted placements that force you to reconsider every zone you thought you knew:

NG+1 Additions

  • Fallgrim Outskirts: A Crimson Brigand (armored variant with fire-enchanted axe) patrols the road near the first Shell statue. He drops 3x the Tar of a normal Brigand and a guaranteed Quenching Acid.
  • Temple Grounds: Shade Monks now appear in groups of 3 instead of 2, and two additional crossbow cultists guard the upper balcony.
  • Abandoned Chamber (Hadern boss): Hadern gains one new attack string: a 4-hit combo ending in an unblockable overhead slam. His second-phase aggression window is 20% shorter.
  • Crypt of Martyrs: Two Wraith Sentinels (elite wraiths with spears) replace standard Wraiths in the long hallway leading to Tarsus.

NG+2 Additions

  • Seat of Infinity: Echo Specters — translucent enemy variants that phase in and out of visibility — patrol the mirror hallways. They can only be hit while materialized.
  • Shifting Archives: A Gilded Nocteserper (golden variant with double health and poison clouds) replaces the standard mini-boss. It drops the unique Gilded Fang crafting component.

NG+3 and Beyond

  • Frenzied variants: From NG+3, certain elite enemies spawn with a glowing red aura — they deal 30% more damage, attack 15% faster, and drop significantly more Tar and Glimpses. Think of them as Mortal Shell II's equivalent of Dark Souls' red phantom enemies.
  • Boss add spawns: Select bosses gain additional phase summons. Crucix the Twiceborn summons 2 additional spiderlings per wave starting NG+3. Imrod's lava arena spawns fire elementals during his Forge's Fury phase.
  • The Dark Father (NG+3+): The final boss gains a fourth phase — "Echo of the Founders" — a brief but intense gauntlet where he cycles rapidly between his three forms. This phase is exclusive to NG+3 and above and rewards a unique cutscene variant.

NG+ Strategies — Builds & Preparations

Best NG+1 Shell Choices

Your NG Shell choice likely carried you through Journey 1. NG+1 demands a different calculus because enemies hit hard enough to punish fragile builds:

  • Harrow (recommended): The durability Shell shines in NG+ where survival is paramount. Its high HP pool and defensive passives mean you'll survive 3-4 hits instead of 1-2. The extended Harden duration (1.8s) gives more wiggle room against faster NG+ enemies.
  • Solomon (balanced): If you're comfortable with the game's rhythm, Solomon's well-rounded stats and strong Arcane scaling let you leverage weapon arts more frequently, which becomes critical when basic attacks take longer to kill.
  • Eredrim (tank): The slowest but tankiest Shell. From NG+3 upward, Eredrim becomes the go-to for players who prefer trading hits over perfect dodging. Stack Vigor and Endurance; your damage comes from counter-attacks, not combos.

Pre-NG+ Preparation Checklist

Before talking to the Old Prisoner to initiate NG+, complete this checklist:

  1. Max out at least 2 weapons to +10. You'll find additional Quenching Acids in NG+, but it's easier to farm them in NG.
  2. Buy all consumables from every vendor — especially Mortal Tokens (buy limit resets each cycle).
  3. Collect all Tainted Nektar if pursuing alternate endings — they carry over between cycles.
  4. Complete NPC questlines you're midway through. Progress resets, so finish or deliberately abandon them.
  5. Fully unlock your main Shell's ability tree. Glimpses are more abundant in NG+ via boss kills, but having your core abilities ready from the start is a huge advantage.
  6. Kill all optional bosses. Some optional bosses (The Enslaved Grisha, Ven Nocteserper) drop unique items you won't see again until the next cycle — and in NG+ they're much harder.
  7. Farm the late-game Tar route (Crypt of Martyrs loop) for a comfortable cushion. Aim for at least 10,000-15,000 spare Tar entering NG+.
Pro Insight: The single best pre-NG+ investment is maxing the Hallowed Sword. Its balanced moveset, fast Harden recovery, and wide sweeping R2 make it the most consistent weapon across all NG+ cycles. Even if you prefer specialized weapons, keep a +10 Hallowed Sword in your inventory as a fallback.

NG+ Combat Mindset Shift

In NG, you can get away with aggressive play — rushing in, trading hits, relying on Harden to bail you out. NG+ punishes this approach severely. Key mindset shifts:

  • Harden is now defensive-first. In NG, Harden could be used offensively (mid-combo canceling). In NG+, save Harden for actual defense unless you're certain of the punish window.
  • Respect every enemy. Even basic Brigands can two-shot a medium-Vigor build in NG+2. Stop running past trash mobs — they'll catch you.
  • Learn to parry. Parry (Resolve) was optional in NG. In NG+3+, parrying becomes the most resource-efficient way to deal with elite enemies. The Tarnished Seal increases parry window by 3 frames — equip it.
  • Pull, don't push. Use ranged consumables (Throwing Knives, Fire Bombs) to lure single enemies out of groups. The Harden Escape gank technique from the Combat Tips guide still works, but it's no longer free — each hit you tank while hardened against multiple enemies in NG+ can still chip 15-20% HP.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I go back to NG after starting NG+?

No. Once you transition into Journey 2, there is no way to return to your Journey 1 world. The game warns you explicitly before the transition. If you missed collectibles or want to finish side content, do it before speaking to the Old Prisoner about "beginning anew."

Do I have to beat the game again to reach the next NG+ cycle?

Yes. Each cycle requires a full clear — defeat The Dark Father — to unlock the next one. There are no shortcuts or boss-skip mechanics. Speedrunners can clear NG+ cycles in under 2 hours with optimized routing.

Do weapon upgrades cap out at +10 across all cycles?

Correct. +10 is the maximum weapon upgrade tier regardless of cycle. NG+ does not introduce +11 or beyond. Instead, the additional weapon power comes from stacking Shell ability synergies and using NG+-exclusive weapons like Martyr's Blade and Abyssal Siphon.

Is NG+ required for the Platinum trophy?

Yes. At minimum, you need to reach NG+2 to collect all weapons (Martyr's Blade is NG+2 exclusive). The Obsidian Form skin is not required for Platinum, but the "All Weapons" trophy is. Completing NG+3 for the Dark Father's Remnant also unlocks a trophy tied to the secret ending.

Do boss soul weapons from NG carry into NG+?

Every weapon you've acquired remains in your inventory. If you traded a boss soul for a weapon in NG, that weapon stays. Bosses drop their souls again in NG+, allowing you to acquire the alternate weapon choice — so you can own both the boss weapon and the alternate option by NG+2.

What's the best Tar farming route in NG+?

The Crypt of Martyrs loop remains optimal, but at NG+3+ the Frenzied Wraith Sentinels in the long hallway drop 800-1,200 Tar each (up from 350 in NG). A full Crypt clear in NG+5 can net over 15,000 Tar. Pair with the Silver Covenant Ring (+20% Tar from defeated enemies) and the Greed Shell ability (Solomon tree, +15% Tar) for maximum yield.
