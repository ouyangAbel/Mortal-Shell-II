How Runes & Enchantments Work

Runes are one of the most impactful customization systems in Mortal Shell II, allowing you to modify your weapons and Shells with powerful passive effects. Unlike simple stat upgrades, runes fundamentally alter how your character plays — adding elemental damage, boosting survivability, enhancing resource generation, and unlocking entirely new combat interactions. Understanding the enchantment system is essential for conquering higher NG+ cycles and optimizing your build for specific encounters.

Every weapon in Mortal Shell II has rune slots that unlock as you upgrade it. A base weapon starts with one slot; upgrading to +5 adds a second slot, and upgrading to +10 unlocks the third and final slot. Shells also have their own dedicated rune slot, accessible from the Shell ability tree. Runes can be freely swapped at any Shell statue (bonfire equivalent), making experimentation low-cost and encouraged.

The enchanting process is handled by Sester Genessa, the game's primary NPC vendor who appears at key Shell statues throughout the world. After acquiring a rune, you bring it to Genessa to infuse it into an available slot. Each rune occupies one slot and can be removed or replaced without destroying the rune itself — it returns to your inventory for reuse.

Rune Categories Explained

Runes in Mortal Shell II fall into three distinct categories, each serving a different role in your build:

  • Offensive Runes (Red): Boost raw damage, add elemental effects, increase critical hit chance, or enhance weapon arts. These are your primary damage multipliers.
  • Defensive Runes (Blue): Increase damage resistance, boost healing received, extend Harden duration, or grant damage reduction after specific triggers. Essential for survivability-focused builds.
  • Utility Runes (Green): Improve Tar and Glimpse gain, increase stamina recovery, extend consumable duration, or add quality-of-life effects. Often underestimated but incredibly powerful in the right context.

Each rune has a rarity tier — Common (white border), Uncommon (green border), Rare (blue border), and Legendary (gold border). Higher rarity runes provide stronger effects but are harder to find. Legendary runes are typically one-of-a-kind pickups from hidden areas or boss encounters.

Pro Tip: Runes are not consumed on equip. Once you acquire a rune, you own it permanently. Feel free to swap runes between weapons and builds at will — there is zero penalty for experimentation.

The Enchanting Process — Step by Step

Infusing runes into your gear requires visiting Sester Genessa at any Shell statue. The process is straightforward:

  1. Visit a Shell statue where Sester Genessa is present (she appears after your first encounter in Fallgrim Tower).
  2. Select "Enchant" from her dialogue options. This opens the enchanting interface showing all your weapons, Shells, and available rune slots.
  3. Choose the weapon or Shell you want to enchant. Available rune slots are displayed as empty circles beneath the item; filled slots show the currently equipped rune.
  4. Select an empty slot and browse your rune inventory. Runes that are compatible with the item type are highlighted; incompatible runes (e.g., weapon-only runes on a Shell) are greyed out.
  5. Confirm the infusion. Some runes require a small Tar cost to infuse (typically 200-500 Tar for Common, 1,000-2,000 for Rare, and 3,000 for Legendary). Removing a rune is always free.
Warning: Rune infusions are per-weapon, not per-slot type. If you have two Hallowed Swords (one in each hand via dual-wielding), each has its own independent rune loadout. Swapping weapons mid-combat does NOT carry runes from the old weapon — plan your loadout before boss encounters.

Best Rune Combos Per Build

The real power of the rune system emerges when you stack complementary runes across your weapon slots and Shell. Here are the optimal combinations for each major build archetype:

Tiel Assassin Build (DPS / Poison Focus)

Tiel's high stamina and fast dodge recovery make it perfect for hit-and-run tactics with damage-over-time effects:

  • Weapon Slot 1: Venomous Edge (poison DoT on every hit)
  • Weapon Slot 2: Critical Eye (+15% crit for burst damage)
  • Weapon Slot 3: Vampiric Strikes (sustain through lifesteal)
  • Shell Rune: Endurance (Tiel burns stamina fast; the recovery boost keeps you mobile)

This combo turns Tiel into a poison machine that heals while enemies slowly die. The critical hits provide burst windows when the poison alone isn't enough.

Eredrim Tank Build (Meat Shield / Counter-Attack)

Eredrim's massive HP pool and slow attacks benefit from runes that reward trading hits:

  • Weapon Slot 1: Berserker's Rage (+20% damage when low HP — Eredrim can survive at low HP longer than any other Shell)
  • Weapon Slot 2: Vampiric Strikes (lifesteal to recover after trades)
  • Weapon Slot 3: Bulwark (50% DR after parry — pairs with Eredrim's parry-focused ability tree)
  • Shell Rune: Martyr's Resolve (40% DR below 20% HP + Harden reset — the ultimate clutch rune)

Solomon Mage Build (Weapon Arts / Arcane Focus)

Solomon's high Arcane scaling makes weapon arts devastating. Stack runes that enhance art damage and Resolve generation:

  • Weapon Slot 1: Soul Reaver (+25% weapon art damage, +10 Resolve per hit)
  • Weapon Slot 2: Conduit of Ruin (lightning bolt every 5th hit for massive AoE)
  • Weapon Slot 3: Flamebrand (adds Fire damage, scales with Arcane stat)
  • Shell Rune: Lorekeeper (extra Glimpses for faster ability tree progression)

Harrow Tank Build (Unkillable Fortress)

Harrow is the ultimate defensive Shell. Stack every defensive rune to become virtually immortal:

  • Weapon Slot 1: Iron Skin (+6% physical DR, stacks with Harrow's passive)
  • Weapon Slot 2: Harden Mastery (more Harden uptime = more invulnerability windows)
  • Weapon Slot 3: Undying Will (survive one lethal hit per encounter)
  • Shell Rune: Martyr's Resolve (40% DR below 20% HP — Harrow can comfortably sit at low HP to trigger this)
Pro Tip: Don't forget you can swap runes at Shell statues before boss encounters. Keep a "farming" loadout (Silver Tongue + Scavenger + Keen Eye) for exploration, then switch to your combat loadout when entering a boss arena. There's no cost to swapping.

Advanced Enchanting Techniques

Rune Stacking Rules

Mortal Shell II allows partial stacking of rune effects. If you equip two runes with similar effects (e.g., two physical damage boosts), the second rune provides diminishing returns — 75% of its listed value. Three identical rune types stack at 50% effectiveness. This means variety in your rune loadout is almost always better than doubling down on one stat.

Elemental Synergy

When you inflict two different elemental status effects on the same enemy, a synergy reaction triggers:

  • Fire + Ice = Steam: Creates a visibility-blocking cloud that reduces enemy accuracy by 30% for 6 seconds
  • Fire + Poison = Miasma: Doubles poison DoT damage and extends duration to 12 seconds
  • Ice + Lightning = Shatter: Frozen enemies hit by lightning damage take 300% increased damage from the triggering hit

The Shatter reaction is the most powerful combo in the game. Pair Frostbite (Ice rune) with Conduit of Ruin (Lightning) on a fast-hitting weapon to repeatedly trigger Shatter for devastating burst damage.

Rune Transfer Between NG+ Cycles

All runes you've collected carry over into NG+ permanently. They are never lost on death or cycle transition. This means by NG+3, you should have every rune in the game and can freely mix-match for any encounter. Legendary runes from NG-exclusive drops (like Conduit of Ruin from The Unchained in NG+) can only be acquired once but persist forever afterward.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I lose runes if I die before reaching a Shell statue?

No. Runes are added to your inventory permanently the moment you pick them up. Unlike Tar (which drops on death and must be recovered), runes are safe the instant you acquire them. You can never lose a rune through any game mechanic.

Do runes affect weapon art damage specifically?

Only runes that explicitly mention weapon arts (like Soul Reaver) boost weapon art damage. Generic damage runes like Crimson Fang or Berserker's Rage apply to normal attacks and heavy attacks but do NOT increase weapon art damage. Check each rune's description carefully for "weapon art" language.

Can I equip the same rune on multiple weapons simultaneously?

No. Each rune exists as a single copy in your inventory. If you have one Critical Eye rune and two weapons, you can only infuse it into one weapon at a time. To swap it, visit Genessa and remove it from the first weapon before infusing it into the second. This is free and instant.

What happens to runes on a weapon I sell or discard?

Runes on a sold or discarded weapon automatically return to your inventory. You never lose runes through weapon management. However, you should always manually remove runes before selling to avoid confusion about your current loadout.

Are there rune synergies with specific Shells?

There are no hidden Shell-rune synergies beyond what the rune descriptions state. However, certain runes are practically better on certain Shells. For example, Berserker's Rage is best on Eredrim (who can survive at low HP) and Martyr's Resolve is best on Harrow (who can comfortably sit at 20% HP due to his massive HP pool). Read our Shell Compendium for detailed Shell stat breakdowns.

Do runes work differently in PvP / Invasions?

Yes. In PvP encounters, all rune effects are reduced by 50% to maintain balance. Defensive runes like Undying Will and Martyr's Resolve still function but at half effectiveness. Offensive runes like Conduit of Ruin deal 50% less bonus damage against other players. This applies automatically whenever you enter an invasion or PvP arena.
