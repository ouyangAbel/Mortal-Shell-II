How Weapon Scaling Works

Every weapon in Mortal Shell II has innate scaling grades tied to three core stats: Strength (STR), Dexterity (DEX), and Arcane (ARC). These grades determine how much bonus damage a weapon gains from each of your Shell's invested stats. A weapon with an S-grade in STR will deal dramatically more damage when wielded by a Shell with high Strength — while the same weapon on an ARC-focused Shell will hit like a wet noodle.

Understanding weapon scaling is the single most impactful thing you can do to increase your damage output. A properly scaled weapon at +5 can out-damage a poorly matched weapon at +10. Before you invest precious Quenching Acids into upgrades, make sure the weapon's scaling aligns with your Shell's stat distribution.

Mortal Shell II displays scaling grades on the weapon detail screen — look for the small letter (E through S) next to each stat icon. The game does not show you the exact numerical bonus, which is why this guide exists: to decode the hidden math behind every swing.

Pro Insight: Scaling grades can change with weapon upgrades. Some weapons start at C-grade STR but improve to B-grade at +5 and A-grade at +8. Always check scaling at multiple upgrade tiers before committing to a build path.

Scaling Grades Explained — From E to S

Mortal Shell II uses a letter-grade system identical to the Soulslike tradition. Each letter represents a range of scaling coefficients that multiply against your invested stat points:

Grade Scaling Coefficient Bonus per Stat Point (approx.) Rating
S 140–180% 3.5–4.5 AR per point Exceptional
A 100–139% 2.5–3.4 AR per point Excellent
B 75–99% 1.9–2.4 AR per point Good
C 50–74% 1.2–1.8 AR per point Average
D 25–49% 0.6–1.1 AR per point Below Average
E 1–24% 0.1–0.5 AR per point Minimal

AR stands for Attack Rating — the number displayed on your weapon card. The scaling bonus is added on top of the weapon's base AR. A weapon with 120 base AR and S-grade STR scaling on a Shell with 40 STR might display 240+ total AR, more than doubling its output.

Warning: Don't confuse scaling grade improvements with base AR improvements when upgrading. A weapon going from +4 to +5 might increase its base AR by 12 but also shift its STR scaling from C to B — the combined effect is much larger than the base AR change alone suggests.

The Damage Formula — Behind the Numbers

Mortal Shell II's damage calculation follows this structure:

Total AR = Base AR + (Stat Value × Scaling Coefficient)

Actual Damage = Total AR × Motion Value × Enemy Defense Modifier × Buff Multipliers

Let's break each component down:

  • Base AR: The weapon's innate damage at its current upgrade level. This increases with each Quenching Acid investment and is independent of your stats.
  • Stat Value: Your Shell's invested points in STR, DEX, or ARC. Soft caps apply at 20, 35, and 50 for each stat.
  • Scaling Coefficient: The hidden multiplier tied to the letter grade. S-grade at +10 might reach 170%; E-grade stays below 24%.
  • Motion Value: A hidden per-attack multiplier. Light attacks typically have MVs of 100%, heavy attacks 150–200%, and weapon arts 200–350%. This explains why a heavy attack deals far more than a light attack even with the same weapon.
  • Enemy Defense Modifier: Each enemy has flat and percentage-based damage reduction. Heavier weapons with higher per-hit damage penetrate flat defense more effectively.
  • Buff Multipliers: Shell abilities, consumable resins, weapon enchantments, and conditional bonuses (e.g., bonus damage from behind, damage while hardened) stack multiplicatively.
Pro Insight: Because flat defense is subtracted from each hit, fast multi-hit weapons (like dual daggers) are penalized more heavily against armored enemies than slow heavy hitters (like the Smoldering Mace). Against late-game armored foes, a single 400-damage heavy swing outperforms four 120-damage light hits — the math favors burst over volume.

The Familiarity System — Mastery Through Use

Beyond scaling, every weapon in Mortal Shell II has a hidden Familiarity meter that fills as you land hits with it. This is the game's "weapon mastery" system — the more you use a weapon, the more you unlock. Familiarity progresses through five tiers:

Tier Hits Required Cumulative Unlock
Tier 1 — Novice 50 hits 50 Minor AR bonus (+3%)
Tier 2 — Adept 150 hits 200 Weapon-specific passive effect revealed
Tier 3 — Skilled 300 hits 500 Scaling grade improvement (one stat +1 grade)
Tier 4 — Expert 500 hits 1,000 Unique weapon art modification
Tier 5 — Master 1,000 hits 2,000 Mastery bonus: +8% AR, visual effect on weapon

Familiarity is per-weapon and persists across NG+ cycles. Once you've mastered a weapon, that mastery is permanent. The hit counter only increments on hits that deal damage — hitting a shield, hitting during an enemy's i-frames, or hitting a dead enemy does not count.

Warning: Familiarity hits are only counted when you land attacks on living enemies. Whiffing attacks, hitting walls, or attacking corpses does not progress the meter. If you're farming familiarity, target durable enemies like Feral Cryptkeepers or the respawning mobs near Fallgrim Tower.

Stat Optimization — How to Build Around Scaling

Knowing the scaling grades is only half the battle. You need to understand soft caps to avoid wasting stat points:

Stat Soft Cap 1 Soft Cap 2 Hard Cap Recommended
STR 20 (100% efficiency) 35 (65% efficiency) 50 (30% efficiency) 35 for most builds, 50 for pure STR
DEX 20 (100% efficiency) 35 (65% efficiency) 50 (30% efficiency) 35 for hybrid, 50 for Tiel builds
ARC 20 (100% efficiency) 35 (70% efficiency) 50 (35% efficiency) 35 for hybrid, 50 for Solomon mage

The golden rule: invest to the first soft cap (20) in your primary stat, then push to 35 if your weapon has A or S scaling. Going beyond 35 to 50 is only worthwhile for dedicated single-stat builds where the weapon has S-grade scaling and you have no other stats worth investing in.

For dual-stat weapons like Smoldering Mace (STR/ARC), it's often better to have 30/30 in both stats than 50/10 — the combined scaling from both stats at moderate investment usually exceeds the diminishing returns of a single stat pushed to hard cap.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does weapon familiarity reset in NG+?

No. Familiarity is permanently tied to each weapon and persists across all NG+ cycles. Once you hit Tier 5 Mastery on a weapon, that status is permanent for the rest of your save file. This makes early investment in your primary weapon extremely rewarding across the entire game.

Is it better to upgrade a weapon or invest in stats first?

In the early game (before +5), upgrading your weapon provides more damage per resource spent than leveling stats. A +3 weapon with 15 STR out-damages a +1 weapon with 25 STR in most cases. Once you reach +5 and the scaling grades improve, stat investment becomes more efficient. Prioritize upgrades first, then shift to stats once you hit +5 to +7.

Do dual-stat weapons actually deal more total damage?

At equivalent total stat investment, dual-stat weapons (like Smoldering Mace with STR/ARC) typically deal 5–12% more damage than single-stat weapons — but only if you invest in both stats evenly. The catch is that you sacrifice survivability stats (VIT, STA) to fund both damage stats. For glass-cannon builds, dual-stat is superior. For tanky builds, single-stat weapons are more efficient.

Can I see my familiarity progress in-game?

Partially. The weapon detail screen shows a small progress bar under the weapon name, but it doesn't display exact hit counts. The bar fills in five segments corresponding to the five tiers. For precise tracking, you'll need to count hits yourself or use community-made calculators that estimate progress based on playtime and combat frequency.

Does the scaling grade shown include familiarity bonuses?

No. The scaling grade displayed on the weapon card is the base grade at the current upgrade level. Familiarity Tier 3's +1 grade bonus is applied invisibly — you won't see the letter change, but the damage calculation uses the improved grade. This means a weapon showing "B" scaling might actually be calculating as "A" if you've reached Tier 3 familiarity.

What happens if I switch weapons after reaching Tier 5 Mastery?

Your mastery on the old weapon is preserved. Each weapon tracks its own familiarity independently. Switching weapons means starting the new weapon's familiarity from zero, but you can always switch back and your Tier 5 mastery will still be there. Many players master 2–3 weapons over a full playthrough to have options for different encounter types.
