Consumable Categories — What They Do and When to Use Them

Mortal Shell II divides consumables into five distinct categories, each serving a specific role in your survival toolkit. Unlike other soulslikes where consumables feel optional, the right consumable at the right moment in Mortal Shell II can make the difference between a one-shot death and a clean boss kill. Understanding each category is step one to mastery.

1. Healing & Recovery Items

The backbone of any loadout. These restore HP, remove status effects, or grant temporary health regeneration. Roasted Rat and Mortal Ichor are your bread-and-butter; Superior Mortal Ichor is the boss-fight upgrade. Always keep at least two healing items mapped to your quick-slot bar.

  • Roasted Rat: Restores 45% HP over 5 seconds. Cheap to craft, abundant ingredients.
  • Mortal Ichor: Restores 60% HP instantly. The standard healing consumable for mid-game.
  • Superior Mortal Ichor: Restores 100% HP instantly + 5-second regen buff. Boss-fight essential.
  • Antidote Tincture: Cures poison buildup and grants 60s poison resistance. Mandatory in Crypt of Martyrs.
  • Purification Incense: Removes all negative status effects (poison, frost, burn, curse). At 3 ingredients per craft, it is expensive — save for emergencies.

2. Offensive Buffs

These boost your damage output, attack speed, or critical hit chance for a limited duration. Pop these just before a boss punish window for maximum efficiency.

  • Berserker Draught: +25% damage dealt, +15% damage taken for 30s. High-risk, high-reward.
  • Sharpshard Oil: +30% critical hit chance for 45s. Devastating with Tiel Shell's innate crit bonuses.
  • Ragefire Elixir: Adds fire damage to all attacks for 60s. Stacks with weapon enchantments.
  • Frostbite Tonic: Adds frost buildup to all attacks for 60s. Slows enemy movement and attack speed on proc.

3. Defensive Buffs

Reduce incoming damage, boost Harden durability, or grant temporary invulnerability frames. These are your "oh no" buttons — use them when you know a big hit is coming.

  • Ironhard Tonic: -30% damage taken for 45s. The single most reliable defensive consumable.
  • Stoneskin Salve: Doubles Harden duration for 30s. Perfect for learning new boss patterns.
  • Ethereal Shroud: Grants 2 seconds of invulnerability on use. Expensive to craft but can save a no-hit run.

4. Thrown Weapons & Bombs

Ranged options that deal damage, apply status effects, or create area denial. Great for pulling enemies, finishing low-HP bosses, or dealing with swarms.

  • Tar Grenade: 150 AoE physical damage. Cheap, spammable, excellent for clearing Leaper packs.
  • Firebomb: 200 AoE fire damage + burn DoT. Melts Imrod's adds and crypt wraiths.
  • Frost Bomb: 180 AoE frost damage + slow field for 8s. Turns fast bosses into target practice.
  • Poisoned Throwing Knife: 80 damage + poison (8s, 5% HP/sec). Kite and watch enemies melt.
  • Weighted Kunai: 120 damage + stagger. Interrupts enemy combos at range.

5. Utility & Quality-of-Life

These don't fit neatly into the above categories but are invaluable in specific situations.

  • Tar-Finding Charm: +50% Tar from defeated enemies for 120s. Use before farming routes.
  • Glimpse Lure: Attracts one nearby enemy toward you for 10s. Isolate enemies from packs before engaging.
  • Homeward Bone Shard: Teleport to the last visited Sester Genessa without losing Tar. Craftable alternative to the limited-use Homeward Bone.
  • Repair Powder: Restores 30% weapon durability. Essential for long exploration runs with low-durability weapons like daggers.
Pro Insight: Offensive and defensive buffs stack multiplicatively, not additively. Berserker Draught (+25%) plus Sharpshard Oil (+30% crit) plus Ragefire Elixir (fire DoT) on a Tiel Shell produces DPS numbers that trivialize most boss fights. The system rewards aggressive buff stacking — learn the combinations.

The six core crafting ingredients and their primary sources:

  • Herbs: Every zone. Dense patches near water in Fallgrim. Most common ingredient — never buy these, just pick them.
  • Tainted Blood: Drops from all humanoid enemies. Brigands in Fallgrim are the best early source (80% drop rate).
  • Sturdy Bone: Drops from large enemies (Flame Wardens, Frost Wraiths, Leaper Elders). Also found in skeletal remains throughout zones.
  • Crystal Resin: Gathered from glowing crystal nodes in Crypt of Martyrs and Shifting Archives. Also a rare drop from Crypt Wraiths.
  • Ember Dust: Drops from fire-based enemies in Seat of Infinity. Imrod's arena has respawning ember-construct adds — the best farming location.
  • Frozen Core: Drops from ice-based enemies in Crypt of Martyrs. Tarsus's arena and the Frost Wraith hallway before Crucix are prime farming spots.

Best Ingredient Farming Routes — Tar-Efficient Gathering

Here are the most Tar-efficient farming routes for each key ingredient. All yields assume you are using a Tar-Finding Charm (doubles your profit on the kill drops). Time estimates are per loop; you can chain loops indefinitely since enemies respawn when you rest at a Sester Genessa checkpoint.

Herbs Route — Fallgrim Basin Loop

Yield: 35–45 Herbs per 3-minute loop | Tar (from kills): ~600

Start at Fallgrim Outskirts Genessa. Run southwest toward the basin — the pond edge has 12–15 herb nodes. Continue west along the cliff wall: another 10 nodes. Loop back through the central clearing (8 nodes) and return to Genessa. Rest to respawn. Three loops yield enough Herbs to craft 30+ consumables.

Tainted Blood Route — Brigand Camp Clearing

Yield: 18–24 Tainted Blood per 2.5-minute loop | Tar: ~800

From Fallgrim Outskirts Genessa, head east into the Brigand encampment. There are 8 Brigands total (2 patrol, 6 stationary). Each drops 1–2 Tainted Blood at 80% rate. Kill all 8, collect, return to Genessa and rest. This is also an excellent early-game Tar farming spot — pair with a Tar-Finding Charm for 1,600+ Tar per loop.

Crystal Resin Route — Crypt Wraith Hallway

Yield: 8–12 Crystal Resin per 4-minute loop | Tar: ~1,200

From the Crypt of Martyrs mid-level Genessa, proceed through the Wraith Hallway (the long corridor with 6 crypt wraiths). Each wraith has a ~40% drop rate for Crystal Resin. At the hallway's end, there are 3 crystal nodes on the walls (guaranteed 1 Resin each). Kill wraiths, harvest nodes, rest at Genessa. This is the single best Crystal Resin source in the game.

Ember Dust Route — Imrod's Arena Adds

Yield: 10–15 Ember Dust per 3-minute loop | Tar: ~1,000

After defeating Imrod, his arena becomes a respawning zone for Ember Constructs. From the Seat of Infinity forge Genessa, enter Imrod's arena. Five constructs spawn in a fixed pattern. Each drops 1–3 Ember Dust at ~60% rate. Full clear takes under 3 minutes with a fire-enchanted weapon. Rest at forge Genessa to reset. This also doubles as a decent Tar farming route for late game.

Frozen Core Route — Tarsus's Chamber Run

Yield: 6–10 Frozen Cores per 3.5-minute loop | Tar: ~900

From the Temple Grounds upper Genessa, backtrack toward Tarsus's chamber. The antechamber has 4 Frost Wraiths — each drops 1–2 Frozen Cores at 50% rate. Two additional wraiths spawn in the side corridor. This is narrow but repeatable. Pair with a fire-enchanted weapon for one-shot kills on wraiths.

Warning: The Crypt Wraith Hallway is dangerous at low levels. Wraiths have a grab attack that ignores Harden. Clear them one at a time using Glimpse Lures, or come back after upgrading your weapon to +3. Attempting to rush through all 6 at once before level 25 will likely end in lost Tar.

Boss-Specific Buff Loadouts — What to Pop Before Every Fight

Every boss in Mortal Shell II has a consumable combination that exploits their specific weaknesses. Below are the optimal 3-buff stacks to apply before crossing the fog gate. Apply in the listed order — defensive buff first (longest duration), then offensive buffs.

Why these combinations work:

  • Hadern: His damage output is modest — Ironhard Tonic negates 30% of it while Ragefire melts his moderate HP pool in under 60 seconds. You do not need a third buff for the tutorial boss.
  • Crucix: Antidote Tincture is mandatory because Phase 2 spider form applies poison with every bite. Without it, you spend half the fight managing poison instead of dealing damage. Ragefire exploits his fire weakness (20% bonus). See the full Crucix guide for phase-specific strategies.
  • Tarsus: Stoneskin Salve doubles Harden duration, letting you tank his entire frost greatsword combo safely. Ragefire's +25% damage against frost enemies stacks with Berserker Draught's +25% universal — the result is a boss fight that ends before the blizzard phase triggers.
  • Imrod: Ironhard Tonic reduces his massive physical damage. Frostbite Tonic slows his already-slow attacks to a crawl. Sharpshard Oil's crit bonus works wonders against his large hitbox — every hit has a high chance of landing on a weak point.
  • Corvid: Stoneskin Salve extends Harden through her multi-hit dive combos. Poisoned Throwing Knives deal passive damage while she flies — she cannot avoid the DoT ticks. Ironhard Tonic helps survive the Shroud of Darkness phase.
  • The Unchained: The most expensive loadout for the most dangerous boss. Ethereal Shroud gives you 2 seconds of invulnerability to survive the Chaos Flurry. Sharpshard Oil + Berserker Draught combine for catastrophic DPS during his long recovery windows, which can skip his third phase entirely.
  • The Dark Father: Ironhard Tonic handles all three phases' physical damage, Ragefire adds consistent bonus damage since he has no elemental resistance, and Sharpshard Oil's crits shorten Phase 3 — the most dangerous phase. A balanced, cost-effective stack for the final boss.
Pro Insight: Buffs tick down during cutscenes and phase transitions, but not during fog gate entry. Time your application: pop defensive buff first (45s duration), walk through fog (no timer tick during transition), pop offensive buffs immediately when the fight starts (30–60s). This gives you the maximum overlap window where all 3 buffs are active simultaneously — typically 20–30 seconds of "god mode."

Optimal Consumable Loadouts Per Shell

Each Shell has quick-slot strengths that favor different consumable combinations. Harrow can carry more healing items; Tiel's speed favors thrown weapons; Eredrim's tankiness lets you skip defensive buffs and stack pure offense. Here are the optimal 4-slot quick-bar loadouts for each Shell.

Harrow — The Survival Loadout

Harrow's passive "Fortified Reserves" grants +1 quick-slot capacity (5 slots total) and +20% healing effectiveness. Lean into this.

  • Slot 1: Superior Mortal Ichor (main heal — 120% effective with Harrow passive)
  • Slot 2: Mortal Ichor (backup heal for long fights)
  • Slot 3: Ironhard Tonic (stack with Harrow's innate tankiness for -50% total damage reduction)
  • Slot 4: Stoneskin Salve (extended Harden already synergizes with Harrow's longest base Harden duration — 1.8s base → 3.6s with Salve)
  • Slot 5: Purification Incense (emergency status cleanse)

Tiel — The Speed Demon Loadout

Tiel's high mobility and rapid attack speed favor aggressive thrown-weapon tactics and crit-stacking. His low HP pool means you need burst healing, not regen.

  • Slot 1: Superior Mortal Ichor (instant 100% heal — Tiel cannot afford slow regen)
  • Slot 2: Sharpshard Oil (stacks with Tiel's innate +15% crit chance for a total +45% crit rate)
  • Slot 3: Berserker Draught (Tiel takes more damage anyway — lean into the glass cannon with +25% damage)
  • Slot 4: Poisoned Throwing Knife (Tiel's speed lets you throw between dodge animations — no other Shell can weave knives this smoothly)

See the Tiel Assassin Build for the full ability tree and weapon pairing.

Eredrim — The Face-Tank Loadout

Eredrim has massive HP and the highest physical damage output in the game. He does not need defensive buffs — stack pure offense and healing.

  • Slot 1: Superior Mortal Ichor (the only Shell that can survive long enough for the 5-second regen to matter)
  • Slot 2: Berserker Draught (+25% on top of Eredrim's already massive base damage is devastating)
  • Slot 3: Ragefire Elixir (fire DoT stacks additively with Eredrim's "Brutal Strikes" charged attack bonus)
  • Slot 4: Tar Grenade (clear adds while maintaining position — Eredrim's slow dodge makes repositioning costly)

See the Eredrim Strength Build for the full ability tree breakdown.

Solomon — The Caster Loadout

Solomon's high Resolve pool makes him the best Shell for spell-casting. His consumable loadout focuses on Resolve generation and spell damage amplification.

  • Slot 1: Superior Mortal Ichor (Solomon's moderate HP pool benefits most from instant heals)
  • Slot 2: Ironhard Tonic (compensate for Solomon's lower physical defense during casting animations)
  • Slot 3: Sharpshard Oil (spell crits benefit from the +30% crit chance)
  • Slot 4: Frost Bomb (drop a slow field, then safely cast high-damage spells while enemies trudge through it)

See the Solomon Mage Build for Resolve-stacking strategies.

Advanced Crafting Tricks Pros Use

Trick 1: Pre-Craft Bulk Before Boss Runs

Boss fights consume consumables fast — 3–5 per attempt is normal. Instead of crafting between attempts, pre-craft 10 copies of your loadout before even approaching the fog gate. This eliminates the frustration of running out mid-learning session. A full boss learning session (10–15 attempts) requires 30–50 crafted items. Farm ingredients for 30 minutes, craft everything at once, then focus entirely on learning the boss.

Trick 2: The 3-Second Buff Chain

There is a hidden buff-application rhythm that minimizes animation time. The sequence: pop Ironhard Tonic (animation: 1.8s), immediately pop Ragefire Elixir (animation: 1.2s), then pop Sharpshard Oil (animation: 0.8s, can be roll-canceled at 0.5s). Total animation lock: ~3.5 seconds. If you do this while the boss is in their intro animation (Hadern's sword draw, Crucix's transformation, The Dark Father's descent), you finish all three buffs before the first attack lands.

Trick 3: Poison Stacking on Multi-Phase Bosses

Poison DoT ticks continue through phase transitions — unique among status effects. Against Crucix (Phase 1 human → Phase 2 spider), apply Poisoned Throwing Knives at the end of Phase 1. When Phase 2 starts, Crucix has already lost 25–30% of his Phase 2 HP from passive poison damage before you swing once. This works on any boss with a mid-fight phase transition (Crucix, Tarsus at 60% HP, The Dark Father at Phase 3).

Trick 4: Free Healing During Enemy Death Animations

When the last enemy in a group dies and the "Enemy Felled" message appears, your character gains ~1.5 seconds of invulnerability. This is a free window to use a healing consumable — the animation fully resolves during the invulnerability period, and you emerge at full HP when the "Enemy Felled" text fades. Practice this timing on Brigand packs in Fallgrim.

Warning: Do not use Ethereal Shroud during boss phase transitions. The 2-second invulnerability window begins the moment you use it, and phase transition cutscenes consume the timer. You will exit the cutscene with zero invulnerability remaining and a wasted 800-Tar consumable. Only use Shroud during active combat.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which consumable should I always carry, no matter the build?

Superior Mortal Ichor. It is the most Tar-efficient full heal in the game (100% HP instant + regen for 400 Tar). Even if you never use any other consumable, always have 3 Superior Mortal Ichor on your quick-bar. It works on every Shell, every boss, every zone. If you craft nothing else, craft this.

Can I craft consumables during boss fights?

No. The crafting menu is inaccessible during combat. You must pre-craft everything before entering the fog gate. This is why pre-farming and bulk-crafting (see Advanced Trick 1 above) is essential — once you are in the boss arena, what you brought is what you have.

Which crafting ingredients should I never buy from merchants?

Herbs — they are absurdly abundant in every zone, available in patches of 10–15 nodes within 30 seconds of any Genessa. Buying Herbs at 30 Tar each is the worst Tar-to-value trade in the game. Sturdy Bone is also overpriced at merchants (200 Tar) versus a 90% drop rate from any large enemy. Only buy Crystal Resin (300 Tar) and Ember Dust (250 Tar) from merchants if you are desperate — their drop rates are moderate, and the farming routes take time.

Do buff durations stack if I use two of the same buff?

No. Using a second Ragefire Elixir while the first is still active refreshes the duration to 60 seconds — it does not add 120 seconds. Overlapping buffs of the same type is wasteful. Wait for the first buff's visual indicator to expire (your weapon's elemental glow fades) before reapplying.

Which buff combination produces the highest DPS in the game?

Berserker Draught (+25% damage) + Sharpshard Oil (+30% crit) + Ragefire Elixir (fire DoT) on a Tiel Shell with Hallowed Sword at +5 upgrade. The mathematical ceiling: ~2,800 DPS against bosses neutral to fire. Against fire-weak bosses (Crucix, Tarsus), this climbs to ~3,600 DPS — enough to kill Tarsus before his blizzard phase even begins. This is the setup speedrunners use for Any% clears.

Are there any consumables that are objectively useless and should be ignored?

Glimpse Lure is the most niche consumable. It has exactly one use case: isolating a single Wraith from the Crypt hallway before you are high enough level to handle all six at once. Outside the Crypt of Martyrs, you will almost never use it. Repair Powder is similarly narrow — only relevant if you main daggers or dual-wield weapons with low durability. If you use Hallowed Sword or Martyr's Blade (high durability), you can safely ignore Repair Powder entirely. Do not waste Tar crafting items you will never use.
