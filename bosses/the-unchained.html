Boss Overview

The Unchained is an ancient being sealed at the bottom of the Abyssal Rift. This fight is the ultimate skill check before the final boss — it tests every mechanic you've learned: Harden timing, dodge precision, pattern recognition, stamina management, and patience. There is no cheese strategy. You must execute.

Location: Abyssal Rift Difficulty: 5/5 Rec. Level: 35-40 Weakness: Holy, perfect parry Best Shell: Solomon

The arena is a dark circular platform floating in the abyss. The edges are not walled — you can fall off. The arena has a subtle glow pattern in the floor that helps with spatial awareness but no environmental hazards. The Unchained has no phases or transformations; instead, he gains access to his full moveset from the start and uses every attack in unpredictable combinations. The fight's difficulty comes from the variety and complexity of his combo chains, not from phase transitions.

Preparation & Recommended Loadout

Recommended Shell

Solomon is the best Shell for this fight. His balanced stats handle the varied demands of the encounter, and his Resolve bonus means more weapon ability uptime for burst damage during Chaos Flurry recovery windows. Harrow is the runner-up for his higher HP pool that forgives the inevitable mistakes during the learning phase.

Recommended Weapon

Holy-enchanted weapon deals 25% bonus damage to The Unchained. A +8 or higher weapon is recommended. The Sacred Hammer is ideal — its blunt damage type adds another 15% bonus, and its weapon ability provides a burst damage window during Chaos Flurry recovery. If unavailable, any holy-enchanted weapon at +8 works.

Items to Bring

  • Weltcaps (x8): Maximum. You will use all of them.
  • Roasted Rat (x4): Burst healing is critical during Chain Cyclone phase.
  • Holy Salve (x2): Adds holy damage if your weapon lacks natural enchantment.
  • Abyssal Ward (x1): Rare consumable that reduces all dark damage by 30% for 120 seconds. If you have one, save it for below 40% HP.

Full Moveset Breakdown

Chaos Flurry

The Unchained's signature attack: a 5-hit sequence with a specific rhythm. This is the fight-deciding move. Every player must learn this rhythm to survive. Detailed breakdown below.

Abyssal Grasp

The Unchained extends a shadowy hand and pulls you toward him from any distance in the arena, then follows up with a devastating overhead slam. The pull deals no damage, but the follow-up slam can one-shot under-leveled characters. Response: When you see the shadow hand extend, immediately Harden. You'll be pulled into his slam, it bounces off your stone shell, and you get a free 2-3 hit counterattack. This is actually one of your safest damage windows — treat it as a gift, not a threat.

Dark Detonation

The Unchained plants his feet and charges dark energy for ~2 seconds, then releases a full-screen AoE explosion. The charge is visible as dark particles converging on his body. Response: If Harden is available, use it. The explosion is a single-hit attack that Harden fully absorbs. If Harden is on cooldown, sprint to the arena edge — the damage falls off slightly at maximum range (you'll still take ~40% HP damage instead of ~80%).

Shadow Chains

The Unchained swings his chains in a wide horizontal arc, covering approximately 270 degrees. Moderate damage, fast recovery. Response: Dodge through the chains (toward him) or Harden. The gap behind him (the remaining 90 degrees) is always safe. If you're behind him, this is a free punish.

Void Slam

A single overhead slam with his fist that creates a small shockwave on impact. The shockwave radius is about 3 meters. Response: Dodge sideways. The slam itself has a long recovery (~2 seconds) — this is one of his most punishable moves.

Chain Cyclone (Below 40% HP)

The Unchained's desperation attack. He swings his chains in a massive expanding spin that covers most of the arena for ~4 seconds. Detailed survival guide below.

Chaos Flurry: The Rhythm Guide

Chaos Flurry is the single most important attack to learn in this fight. Here's the exact rhythm:

Hit #TypeSpeedTimingYour Response
1Horizontal sweepFast0.0sObserve
2UppercutFast0.4sObserve
3ThrustSlow1.0sHarden
4Spin attackFast1.4sDodge backward
5Heavy slamHeavy2.0sPunish during recovery

The Optimal Response:

  1. Stand at mid-range when the flurry begins
  2. Let hits 1 and 2 pass (they won't reach you at mid-range)
  3. Harden hit 3 (the slow thrust) — this is the most telegraphed and easiest to Harden
  4. Immediately dodge backward for hit 4 (the fast spin)
  5. Step forward and punish during hit 5's recovery (~1.5 seconds)

Why hit 3? Hit 3 is the slowest in the sequence, giving you the most reaction time. Hit 1 is too fast to react to from neutral. Hit 4 cannot be Hardened because you're still in Harden recovery from hit 3. The hit 3 → dodge hit 4 → punish hit 5 sequence is the highest-damage response.

Critical Warning: Do NOT try to attack between hits 1-4. The timing gaps are too tight. Attempting to sneak in a hit between hits 2 and 3 will get you caught by the thrust. Wait for hit 5's recovery.

Chain Cyclone Survival Guide

Chain Cyclone triggers at 40% HP and repeats every ~30 seconds until the fight ends. The attack lasts ~4 seconds and covers most of the arena with spinning chains.

The Safe Zone

The hitbox is weakest at the cyclone's midsection — not at the center (where The Unchained is) and not at the edge (where the chains reach). The safe zone is a ring at roughly 40-60% of the arena radius from center. Time your dodge to pass through the chains into this inner ring.

Execution

  1. When you see him wind up (chains gathering), start moving to the midsection
  2. As the chains begin spinning, dodge forward through the first chain sweep
  3. You're now in the midsection safe zone. Wait for the cyclone to end (~4 seconds)
  4. The moment the chains stop, rush in for 2-3 hits during his recovery

Alternative (simpler but less damage): Sprint to the arena edge. The outermost ring of the arena is safe from most chain hits, though the very tips of the chains may clip you. You take ~20% HP damage instead of full damage. This is the safer option for learning.

Expert Tip: If you can consistently reach the midsection safe zone, Chain Cyclone becomes a damage opportunity. The Unchained is stationary during the spin, and you can land 2-3 hits on him from the safe zone as the chains pass over/under you.

Detailed Strategy

Phase-less Fight

The Unchained has no phase transitions. He uses his full moveset from 100% HP, and his AI selects attacks semi-randomly. The only threshold is Chain Cyclone at 40%. Your strategy must adapt to his random selection:

AttackPriorityResponseDamage Window
Chaos FlurryHighest threatHarden hit 3, dodge hit 4, punish hit 51.5s
Abyssal GraspBest opportunityHarden on pull, counter slam2-3s
Dark DetonationMedium threatHarden or sprint to edge1s (if close)
Shadow ChainsLow threatDodge behind, punish1.5s
Void SlamLow threatDodge sideways, punish2s
Chain CycloneSurvival priorityReach midsection or edge2s after cyclone

Resource Management

This is a war of attrition. The Unchained has a large HP pool and the fight typically lasts 8-12 minutes. Healing management is critical:

  • Use Weltcaps for chip damage (10-30% HP losses)
  • Use Roasted Rats for emergency burst heals (50%+ HP losses)
  • Only heal during confirmed safe windows: post-Chaos Flurry, post-Void Slam, post-Abyssal Grasp
  • Never heal during Chain Cyclone recovery — he may follow up with Chaos Flurry immediately

No-Hit Walkthrough

The Unchained no-hit is one of the hardest challenges in the game. Key principles:

  1. Chaos Flurry: The mid-range positioning trick (standing so hits 1-2 miss naturally) makes this attack much more manageable. Only hits 3-5 need active defense.
  2. Abyssal Grasp: Free damage. Harden on the pull animation start. Never fail to punish this — it's the safest and longest window.
  3. Chain Cyclone: Learn the midsection safe zone. Getting clipped by chains in the outer ring ends the no-hit run. Practice the forward-dodge-through-chains timing.
  4. Dark Detonation: Always keep Harden available for this. If Harden is on cooldown when he starts charging, you must sprint to the edge. The 40% damage from edge-range still ends a no-hit run.
  5. Never greed. One hit per Void Slam window. Two hits per Abyssal Grasp window. One hit per Shadow Chains window. Patience is the only path.

FAQ

Can I parry Chaos Flurry instead of Hardening?

Technically yes, but the timing is extremely tight on hits 1-4. Hit 3 (the slow thrust) is the only one with a parry window generous enough for consistent execution. Harden is strongly recommended over parry for this fight.

How do I avoid falling off the arena?

The arena edge has a subtle raised lip. If you dodge backward near the edge, you can fall off. Always dodge sideways or forward when near the edge. During Abyssal Grasp, you're pulled toward the center, so falling off is not a risk from that attack.

Is The Unchained optional?

Technically yes. You can skip the Abyssal Rift entirely and go directly to the final boss through the First Flame entrance. However, the Abyss ability tier unlocked here provides significant power for the final fight.
