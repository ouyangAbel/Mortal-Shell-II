Boss Overview

Crucix, the Twiceborn, is the final boss of the Crypt of the Damned and the first major skill check in Mortal Shell II. This two-phase fight begins as a straightforward humanoid knight encounter before transforming into one of the most intense spider-form battles in the game. Many players hit their first wall here.

Location: Crypt of the Damned Difficulty: 3/5 Rec. Level: 10-15 Weakness: Fire, thrust Best Shell: Solomon

The arena is a large rectangular crypt chamber with stone pillars along the edges. The pillars provide momentary cover in Phase 1 but are destroyed during the Phase 2 transition, so do not develop a pillar-dependent strategy. The floor is flat with no environmental hazards in either phase.

Preparation & Recommended Loadout

Recommended Shell

Solomon is the top pick. His balanced HP/stamina profile handles both phases well, and his higher Resolve stat means more weapon ability uptime, which matters for burst damage during Crucix's vulnerable windows. Eredrim is a solid alternative if you prefer raw survivability for Phase 2 learning attempts.

Recommended Weapon

Bring a weapon with fire enchantment if available. Crucix takes 20% bonus fire damage in both phases. The Molten Sword is ideal. If you don't have fire access, any weapon with good thrust attacks works — Crucix is weak to thrust damage, taking an additional 15% bonus from thrust-type attacks.

Items to Bring

  • Weltcaps (x6-8): Phase 2 will likely cost you 3-4 heals while learning.
  • Roasted Rat (x3): Burst healing for emergency moments in Phase 2.
  • Fire Salve (x1): If available, pop it at the Phase 2 transition for bonus fire damage on your weapon.
  • Poison Cure (x2): Phase 2's Venom Spray inflicts poison. Having cures prevents tick damage from stacking.

Phase 1: The Human Knight (100%-60% HP)

Crucix enters the arena as a heavily armored knight wielding a sword and shield. He is methodical and defensive, using his shield to block your attacks if you strike at the wrong time. Phase 1 is about patience and punishing specific openings.

Shield Charge

Crucix raises his shield and rushes forward in a straight line, finishing with a wide horizontal sweep. The charge covers about 60% of the arena width. Response: Dodge sideways as soon as he starts moving. Do not try to Harden through the charge itself — the shield bash drains extra stamina even through Harden. Wait for the sweep at the end, then punish during the recovery.

Sword 3-Hit Combo

Stab → slash → stab with even ~0.6s timing between each hit. The third stab has the longest recovery (about 0.8s). Response: Harden the first stab, walk backward to avoid the slash, then step forward and punish after the third stab. This is your primary damage window in Phase 1.

Holy Blade

Crucix plants his sword in the ground, charges for ~1.5 seconds, and releases a light-wave projectile that travels across the arena. You can hear a chime sound when the charge starts. Response: This is actually a gift. During the 1.5-second charge, Crucix cannot move or turn. Rush to his side or behind and land 3-4 free hits. When the projectile fires, a simple sidestep dodges it.

Pro Tip: If you stay close to Crucix during Phase 1, he rarely uses Holy Blade. The AI prefers Holy Blade when you're at range. Use this to control his attack selection by staying mid-range and only closing in for punishes.

Phase 2: The Spider Transformation (Below 60% HP)

At 60% HP, a cutscene triggers: Crucix discards his shield and transforms into a massive spider-like abomination. The arena pillars are destroyed. This is where the real fight begins.

Phase 2 Crucix is significantly faster, has more health than you'd expect, and introduces poison mechanics. However, he also has larger punish windows after certain attacks.

Leaping Crush

Crucix rears up on his hind legs, pauses for ~0.8 seconds, then leaps and slams down dealing AoE damage in a large radius. Response: Double roll backward the moment he leaves the ground. The AoE radius is larger than it looks. After the slam, he has a 2-second recovery with his legs splayed — this is your biggest damage window in the entire fight. Rush in and land 3-4 hits.

Venom Spray

Cone-shaped poison spray that covers approximately 120 degrees in front of him. Leaves damaging pools on the ground for ~10 seconds. Response: Run behind him or to the arena edges. The spray cannot hit targets directly behind his body. If you're caught in the cone, immediately use a Poison Cure to prevent tick damage from compounding.

Spider Rush

Lightning-fast forward charge with a claw swipe at the end. This is his most dangerous attack because it comes out with very little telegraph (~0.4 seconds). Response: Harden the moment you see him twitch forward. The timing is tight but consistent once you learn the visual cue. If your Harden is on cooldown, a panic dodge sideways is your only option.

Claw Combo

A 3-hit ground claw combo with a "fast-slow-fast" rhythm. Hits 1 and 3 are quick lateral swipes; hit 2 is a slower overhead slam. Response: Harden between hits 2 and 3. The slow hit 2 gives you time to activate Harden, absorb hit 3, then counterattack.

Detailed Strategy

Phase 1 Goal: Conserves Resources

Phase 1 should cost you zero to one heal. The attacks are well-telegraphed and the punish windows are generous. Your goal is to reach Phase 2 with all your consumables intact.

Phase 2 Goal: Stay Close, Stay Calm

The instinct when fighting a giant spider is to run away. This is exactly what Crucix wants. His ranged attacks (Venom Spray, Spider Rush at distance) are far deadlier than his melee. Stay close, circle around his body, and punish the big attacks:

  1. Leaping Crush is your primary damage window. Bait it by staying at mid-range, then close in after the slam.
  2. Spider Rush is your secondary window. Harden, he bounces off, you counter.
  3. Claw Combo is a neutral exchange. Harden and trade.
  4. Venom Spray is the one attack you disengage for. Get behind, wait for it to end, re-engage.

At ~40% HP, Crucix begins chaining Leaping Crush into Venom Spray. As long as you dodge the jump, the spray won't reach you because you'll be behind him from the dodge. Don't panic and run away after dodging the jump.

No-Hit Walkthrough

Crucix no-hit requires consistent Spider Rush Hardening. The strategy:

  1. Phase 1: Bait Holy Blade from range for safe 3-4 hit punishes. Never engage in melee unless he uses Holy Blade. Slow but extremely safe.
  2. Phase 2 opening: Immediately close distance. Stay at melee range to force Claw Combos instead of Spider Rushes.
  3. Phase 2 loop: Circle-strafe around his body. When he does Claw Combo, Harden hit 2, absorb hit 3, hit once. When he Leaping Crushes, dodge behind, hit twice. When he Venom Sprays, you're already behind him from circling.
  4. Spider Rush response: If you're at melee range, he rarely uses Spider Rush. If he does, Harden on the twitch. This is the hardest part of the no-hit run.
Warning: The no-hit run takes 8-12 minutes. Venom Spray pools persist on the ground and slowly reduce your safe space. Be mindful of arena positioning throughout the fight.

Rewards & What to Do Next

  • Tar: ~600 Tar
  • Glimpses: 3 Glimpses
  • Unlocks: Deeper Crypt areas, new Shell ability tier

After defeating Crucix, you have access to deeper areas of the Crypt and can progress toward the Frozen Sanctum or Blazing Forge. We recommend heading to the Frozen Sanctum next for a natural difficulty curve. Before that, consider upgrading your weapon to at least +3 using our Tar Farming Guide.

FAQ

Can I stagger Crucix out of his transformation?

No. The Phase 2 transformation is scripted and uninterruptible. Use the cutscene time to heal or rebuff.

Does fire damage work in both phases?

Yes. Crucix takes 20% bonus fire damage in both human and spider forms. Enchant your weapon with fire before the fight.

I keep getting one-shot by Leaping Crush. Help?

Leaping Crush's hitbox is active even during the descent, not just on impact. You need to be outside the radius before he starts falling, not just before he lands. Double roll immediately when he leaves the ground.
