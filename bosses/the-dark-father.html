Boss Overview

The Dark Father is the final boss of Mortal Shell II, encountered at the heart of the First Flame. As the climax of the entire game, this fight incorporates elements from every boss you've faced: Hadern's Harden timing, Crucix's transformation surprise, Tarsus's environmental pressure, Imrod's arena management, Corvid's speed, and The Unchained's combo complexity. Three distinct phases, each completely changing the combat rhythm, make this the longest and most demanding fight in the game.

Location: The First Flame Difficulty: 5/5 Rec. Level: 40+ Weakness: Harden baiting, patience, all elements Best Shell: Harrow

The arena is the First Flame cathedral: a massive circular chamber with a burning flame at the center. The flame does not damage you but provides ambient light that dims progressively as the fight enters later phases. The arena floor is flat stone with no hazards. The fight has no time limit, no DPS check, and no arena destruction. This is a pure endurance fight — survival and consistency are all that matter.

Preparation & Recommended Loadout

Recommended Shell

Harrow is the best Shell for this fight. His high HP pool absorbs the combo damage you'll inevitably take while learning the three phases, and his Harden cooldown is standard, which is sufficient for all three phases' attack tempos. His passive health bonus effectively gives you an extra mistake per phase. Solomon is the alternative if you prefer balanced stats and higher weapon ability uptime.

Recommended Weapon

Fully upgraded (+10) weapon is mandatory. The Dark Father has the largest HP pool in the game, and the three-phase structure means the fight can last 15-20 minutes with an under-leveled weapon. For enchantment: all elements work equally in Phase 3 (fire, frost, holy all deal bonus damage). Use your highest-damage enchantment. The Sacred Hammer at +10 with holy enchantment is optimal for its burst weapon ability.

Items to Bring

  • Weltcaps (x8): Maximum. Budget 2-3 per phase.
  • Roasted Rat (x4): Maximum. Critical for Phase 3 emergencies.
  • Holy Salve (x2): Adds bonus damage. Pop one at Phase 2 transition, save one for Phase 3.
  • Abyssal Ward (x1): If available, save for Phase 3. 30% damage reduction for 2 minutes is massive.

Pre-Fight Checklist

  • Weapon at +10
  • Shell ability tree fully unlocked (all nodes)
  • All consumable slots filled
  • Inventory clear of junk items (prevents accidental wrong-item usage)

Phase 1: Humanoid Form (100%-70% HP)

The Dark Father appears as a towering armored figure wielding a greatsword similar to Hadern's but faster and more aggressive. Phase 1 is a pure melee duel that tests your Harden fundamentals.

Greatsword Combos

He uses two combo patterns:

  • 2-Hit Combo: Slash → Slam. Fast, standard timing. Harden the slash, punish after the slam.
  • 3-Hit Combo: Slash → Slash → Slam. The two slashes are fast; the slam is delayed. Harden the first slash, walk backward to avoid the second, step forward and punish after the slam.

Flame Wave

He drags his sword along the ground and releases a flame wave that travels across the arena. Telegraphed by the sword-drag (~1 second). Response: Jump or dodge sideways. At mid-range, a simple sidestep avoids it. He uses this when you're at range to punish passive play.

Counter-Stance

Occasionally, The Dark Father holds his sword in a defensive position for ~2 seconds. If you attack during this, he parries and counters for massive damage. Response: Do not attack. Wait for the stance to end. He'll drop the stance and follow up with a punishable attack. The stance visually looks like his sword held diagonally across his body with a faint glow.

Phase 1 Strategy

Stay at mid-range. Bait his combos. Harden the first hit, punish the recovery. Do not attack during Counter-Stance. Do not get greedy — one extra swing in Phase 1 can cost 30% HP from his counter. Phase 1 should be a warmup. If you use more than 1-2 heals here, practice more before proceeding.

Phase 1 Transition: At 70% HP, he sheathes his sword and begins to transform. This is a 3-second window. Use it to heal, rebuff (pop Holy Salve), or reposition to the arena center.

Phase 2: Shadow Form (70%-30% HP)

The Dark Father discards his physical form and becomes a shadow being. He can teleport, fires ranged shadow barrages, and his melee attacks become faster and less telegraphed. Phase 2 is the hardest phase for most players because teleportation breaks your spatial awareness.

Shadow Teleport

He dissolves into shadow and reappears behind you within ~0.8 seconds, immediately attacking. Response: Listen for the teleport audio cue: a low hum that starts when he dissolves, followed by a sharp sound when he materializes. Harden ~0.5 seconds after the low hum starts. Direction doesn't matter — Harden protects from all sides.

Shadow Barrage

Fires a rapid series of shadow projectiles from a distance. The projectiles are fast but travel in a straight line. Response: Strafe sideways continuously. They cannot track. If you're close, he won't use this attack.

Shadow Slash

A faster version of his Phase 1 slash with less telegraph (~0.3 seconds). Used immediately after teleport. Response: Same as teleport response — Harden on the teleport audio cue and the slash will connect with your stone shell.

Shadow Grab

A new attack: he extends a shadowy hand to grab you (similar to The Unchained's Abyssal Grasp). If connected, he deals heavy damage and heals himself for ~3% HP. Response: Dodge sideways. The grab has poor lateral tracking. If you see the hand extend, a quick sidestep avoids it entirely.

Phase 2 Strategy

The core of Phase 2 is audio reaction. Stop relying on visual cues and listen:

  1. Low hum: He's teleporting behind you. Harden.
  2. Swoosh sound: Shadow Barrage incoming. Strafe.
  3. Silence + visual shimmer: Shadow Grab. Dodge sideways.

Close distance whenever possible. At melee range, he uses Shadow Slash instead of Shadow Barrage, which is easier to counter. After every successful Harden counter, land 1-2 hits and immediately prepare for the next teleport.

Warning: Shadow Grab heals him. If you get grabbed 3-4 times, you've effectively added an extra minute to the fight. Prioritize dodging grabs over dealing damage.

Phase 3: Ultimate Form (Below 30% HP)

At 30% HP, The Dark Father fuses shadow and flame into his Ultimate Form. He can use melee and ranged attacks simultaneously, and his attack tempo increases dramatically. This is the final exam of everything you've practiced across the entire game.

What's New

  • Dual-Mode Attacks: He swings his sword in melee while firing shadow projectiles at range. You must dodge the melee AND avoid the projectiles.
  • Flame Aura: His body is wreathed in fire. Standing near him deals tick damage (~2% per second). You must attack in quick bursts and retreat.
  • Empowered Teleport: He can now teleport twice in quick succession. After the first teleport, immediately expect a second.
  • Ultimate Combo: A devastating 7-hit combo that combines flame and shadow attacks. The rhythm is fast-fast-slow-pause-fast-fast-heavy. Harden on the third hit (slow), dodge the pause, then Harden again on the sixth hit. Punish after the seventh hit's recovery.

Phase 3 Strategy

  1. One hit and retreat. This is the mantra of Phase 3. His Flame Aura punishes extended melee contact. Land one hit, back out. Repeat.
  2. Pop your second Holy Salve immediately at the transition. The bonus damage shortens Phase 3, which is the most dangerous phase.
  3. Use your weapon ability during his Ultimate Combo recovery window. The 7-hit combo has a ~2-second recovery after the final hit — this is your biggest damage window.
  4. Double teleport awareness. After the first teleport Harden, keep Harden held. He may teleport again immediately. If your Harden breaks after the first, the second teleport attack hits you.
  5. Don't change your rhythm. The Dark Father's third form deliberately baits greed. He has more stamina than he appears to. Hit once and retreat. Never swing twice.

Detailed Strategy

Phase 1: Build Confidence

Phase 1 is straightforward. Execute the Harden-punish loop flawlessly. Use 0-1 heals. This phase exists to build your rhythm for the harder phases ahead. If Phase 1 takes more than 3 minutes, your weapon may be under-leveled.

Phase 2: Audio is King

Close your eyes if you have to. The low hum teleport cue is the most important audio signal in the entire game. Once you can consistently Harden the teleport attack, Phase 2 becomes manageable. The Shadow Grab is the only truly dangerous attack — dodge it at all costs.

Phase 3: Patience Over DPS

Phase 3 is designed to kill greedy players. Every attack is a trap for the over-eager. The Flame Aura means you cannot trade blows. The dual-mode attacks mean you cannot tunnel-vision on one defense. The double teleport means you cannot assume safety after one Harden.

The path to victory: one hit at a time. One heal at a time. One dodge at a time. If you can maintain this discipline for 5-8 minutes, you win.

No-Hit Walkthrough

The Dark Father no-hit is the ultimate challenge in Mortal Shell II. A full run takes 15-25 minutes of flawless execution across three phases.

Phase 1 No-Hit

Only punish after the 3-Hit Combo's final slam. Never attack after the 2-Hit Combo — he sometimes chains into a Flame Wave faster than you can react. Bait Counter-Stance and wait it out. Slow but safe.

Phase 2 No-Hit

Audio-only defense. Harden every teleport. Strafe every barrage. Sidestep every grab. Attack only during Shadow Slash recovery (1 hit per window). The fight slows to a crawl but every hit is safe.

Phase 3 No-Hit

The hardest 5 minutes in the game. Rules:

  • One hit per punish window. Always.
  • After every hit, immediately prepare for double teleport.
  • Never stand still. Flame Aura tick damage counts as a hit.
  • During Ultimate Combo: Harden hits 3 and 6. This requires frame-perfect timing on the gap between hits 6 and 7.
  • Shadow Grab must be dodged on reaction, not prediction. If you dodge early, he adjusts.
Final wisdom: The Dark Father's third form deliberately baits greed. He has more stamina than he appears to. Victory belongs to the most patient. If you can resist the urge to swing twice, you will prevail.

Rewards & Ending

  • Tar: ~8,000 Tar
  • Glimpses: 12 Glimpses
  • Ending: Triggers the game's ending cinematic and credits
  • New Game+: Unlocks after credits, retaining all Shell abilities and weapon upgrades
  • Remembrance: The Dark Father Remembrance item allows refighting the boss at any Shell statue

FAQ

Is there a phase 4?

No. The Dark Father has exactly 3 phases. Once you push him below 30%, Phase 3 continues until he's dead.

Can I use all my healing items in Phase 1 and still win?

Technically yes, but it's not recommended. Phase 3 is designed to require healing. Budget at least 3-4 heals for Phase 3, even on a skilled run.

What if I die during the ending cinematic?

The ending cinematic is not interactive. Once Phase 3 ends, you've won. Enjoy the credits.

Is New Game+ harder?

Yes. Enemies deal ~30% more damage and have ~50% more HP. Boss attack patterns are identical but the punishment windows are tighter because you can absorb fewer mistakes. Your retained Shell abilities and weapon upgrades partially offset this.
