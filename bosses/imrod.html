Boss Overview

Imrod is a colossal fire giant who guards the Blazing Forge. His arena is a lattice of metal walkways over active lava flows, and his attacks progressively destroy sections of the walkway, shrinking your safe space. The fight features a brutal DPS check at 30% HP that has ended many runs.

Location: Blazing Forge Difficulty: 4/5 Rec. Level: 25-30 Weakness: Frost, blunt Best Shell: Tiel

The arena starts as a large cross-shaped metal walkway over lava. Imrod's Lava Call attack permanently destroys walkway sections, reducing your movement options throughout the fight. Arena management is as important as combat skill in this encounter. The fight has a strict time limit in its final phase — if you can't finish Imrod within ~30 seconds of his Forge's Fury activation, the lava will consume the entire arena.

Preparation & Recommended Loadout

Recommended Shell

Tiel is the best Shell for Imrod. His high stamina supports the constant dodging and repositioning the fight demands, and his shorter Harden cooldown lets you match Imrod's moderate attack tempo. Tiel's lower HP is offset by the fact that most of Imrod's attacks should be dodged, not tanked. Solomon is a solid alternative for more balanced stats.

Recommended Weapon

Frost-enchanted weapon is critical. Imrod takes 25% bonus frost damage. The Frozen Shiv or any frost-enchanted fast weapon pairs perfectly with Tiel's stamina. Blunt-type weapons deal an additional 15% bonus. If you have a frost-enchanted hammer or mace, this fight becomes significantly easier.

Items to Bring

  • Weltcaps (x6): You'll need 3-4 over the course of the fight.
  • Roasted Rat (x3): Burst healing for Forge's Fury phase mistakes.
  • Fire Resist Salve (x2): Reduces fire damage by 50%. Pop one at the start and one at the Forge's Fury transition. This is nearly mandatory.
  • Frost Salve (x2): If your weapon lacks natural frost enchantment, these add frost damage for 60 seconds each.

Phase 1: The Fire Giant (100%-30% HP)

Flame Hammer

Imrod's bread-and-butter attack. He raises his massive hammer overhead, holds for ~1 second, and brings it down in a devastating slam. The impact zone creates a small fire puddle. Response: Sidestep and counter-attack his back. The hammer gets stuck in the ground briefly, giving you 1.5 seconds to land 2-3 hits from behind.

Spinning Inferno

Imrod roars and begins a 360-degree spin attack that lasts ~3 seconds, covering his entire body radius with fire. Response: Get far away immediately. Do NOT try to Harden through it — the multi-hit nature means Harden absorbs only the first tick, and the remaining hits deal full damage during your recovery. Run. This attack is signaled by a distinctive roar ~0.8 seconds before the spin begins.

Lava Call

Imrod stomps the ground and summons persistent lava pools at his feet. These pools remain for the rest of the fight and deal heavy damage if you walk through them. Each Lava Call destroys 1-2 walkway sections. Response: Kite Imrod to clean sections of the arena before he uses this attack. The AI uses Lava Call approximately every 45-60 seconds. Keep mental track of when the next one is coming and position accordingly.

Back Weakness

Imrod's back takes 30% bonus damage from all sources. This is separate from and stacks with frost/blunt bonuses. Tiel's stamina allows you to consistently circle behind for back-stabs.

Key Insight: Imrod's weak point is his back. Every punish should be from behind. The ideal loop: dodge his attack sideways, end up behind him, hit 2-3 times on his back, retreat before the next attack.

Phase 2: Forge's Fury (Below 30% HP)

At 30% HP, Imrod ignites his entire body in flames. This is a hard DPS check: all his attacks gain 50% range and bonus fire damage, lava pools spread faster, and you have approximately 30 seconds before the arena is completely covered in lava. You must finish the fight in this window.

What Changes

  • All attack hitboxes increase by 50%
  • Flame Hammer creates larger fire puddles
  • Spinning Inferno duration increases to ~4 seconds
  • Lava Call spawns more pools and destroys more walkway per use
  • New: Flame Trail — his movement leaves a burning trail on the ground

Forge's Fury Strategy

This phase is about controlled aggression. You cannot play safe — the arena timer is real. The optimal approach:

  1. Pop your second Fire Resist Salve immediately at the transition
  2. Only attack during his post-roar exhaust animation (~2 seconds after he roars at the transition)
  3. Harden his enlarged sweeps but only counter with 1-2 hits, not 3
  4. Use your frost-enchanted weapon's full combo every punish window — the bonus damage is critical
  5. If you brought Eredrim's Shell, his charged heavy during the transition roar can deal 15-20% of Imrod's remaining HP in one hit
Warning: Do not try to tank through Imrod's Forge's Fury attacks even with Fire Resist active. His enlarged hitboxes combined with fire trails make facetanking lethal. You still need to dodge — you just need to be aggressive about attacking during every window.

Arena & Lava Management

The arena starts as a cross-shaped walkway. Imrod's Lava Call permanently destroys sections. Good arena management extends the fight duration you have before Forge's Fury.

Optimal Kiting Path

Kite Imrod in a circular pattern around the arena's outer walkway. When he uses Lava Call, the pools land at his current position — by the time he uses it again, you've moved him to a new section. This keeps the lava concentrated in one area and preserves maximum walkable space for the Forge's Fury phase.

Arena Destruction Timeline

  • 0:00 - 1:00: Full arena, no restrictions
  • 1:00 - 2:30: 2-3 sections lost, slight pathing adjustments needed
  • 2:30 - 4:00: Half the arena gone, must kite carefully
  • 4:00+: Minimal safe space, Forge's Fury incoming

If you reach 4:00 without triggering Forge's Fury, your DPS is too low. Consider upgrading your weapon or switching to a frost-enchanted option.

Detailed Strategy

The Core Loop

  1. Approach Imrod from the front to bait Flame Hammer
  2. Dodge sideways as the hammer comes down
  3. Circle behind and land 2-3 back-stabs with frost weapon
  4. Retreat to mid-range and reposition to a clean arena section
  5. When he roars for Spinning Inferno, full retreat until it ends
  6. Punish the spin recovery with 1 hit, then reposition

DPS Benchmarks

  • +5 frost weapon: Fight should last 3-4 minutes. Tight but manageable Forge's Fury.
  • +8 frost weapon: Fight should last 2-3 minutes. Comfortable Forge's Fury window.
  • +10 frost weapon: You may skip Forge's Fury entirely if your burst is high enough.

No-Hit Walkthrough

Imrod no-hit requires excellent arena awareness. The strategy:

  1. Phase 1: Only punish Flame Hammer from behind. Never attack during or after Spinning Inferno — the fire puddles from his body linger for 0.5 seconds after the spin ends. Wait 1 second after spin to approach.
  2. Lava Call tracking: Count 45 seconds between each Lava Call. Pre-position Imrod to a section you're willing to sacrifice.
  3. Forge's Fury: The fire trail is the no-hit killer. Never walk where Imrod has walked. Attack only from the side during his Flame Hammer recovery. One hit per window. Patience wins.

Rewards & What to Do Next

  • Tar: ~1,800 Tar
  • Glimpses: 5 Glimpses
  • Unlocks: Forge depths, Fire ability tier, Imrod Remembrance item

After Imrod, head to the Watcher's Spire for Corvid. With your upgraded fire/frost weapons, you're well-equipped for the late game. Imrod is also the most efficient boss to farm for Glimpses via Remembrance — see our Tar Farming Guide.

FAQ

Can I prevent lava from destroying the arena?

No. Lava Call always destroys walkway sections. Good kiting minimizes the impact but cannot prevent it entirely.

What happens if the arena is fully covered in lava?

You die. There is no safe spot. The Forge's Fury phase is a hard enrage timer — you must deal enough damage before the arena is gone.

Is Imrod optional?

No. Defeating Imrod is required to access the Watcher's Spire and progress toward the late-game areas.
