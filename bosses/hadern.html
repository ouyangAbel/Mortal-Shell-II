Boss Overview

Hadern is the first boss you encounter in Mortal Shell II, serving as both a tutorial encounter and a genuine test of your Harden mechanic understanding. He guards the entrance to Fallgrim Center and must be defeated before you can access the wider game world.

Location: Fallgrim Center Difficulty: 1/5 Rec. Level: Any Weakness: Harden timing Best Shell: Harrow

Hadern wields a massive greatsword with slow but devastatingly powerful strikes. His moveset is intentionally limited to teach you the core combat loop: observe the telegraph, Harden at the right moment, break free, and punish. While he may feel intimidating on a first encounter, his patterns are highly readable and consistent across every attempt.

The arena is a circular stone platform with no environmental hazards. There are no pillars to hide behind, no elevation changes, and no adds. This is a pure 1v1 duel designed to solidify your fundamentals.

Preparation & Recommended Loadout

Recommended Shell

Harrow is the best Shell for this fight. His high HP pool forgives mistakes during your learning phase, and his Harden cooldown is standard, giving you comfortable timing windows. If you prefer a more aggressive approach, Tiel works as well due to his higher stamina allowing more attack strings between Hadern's combos.

Recommended Weapon

Any starting weapon works. The Hallowed Sword is ideal for beginners because its light attack chain is fast enough to land 2 hits during every punish window. The Smoldering Scepter deals slightly more damage per hit but has a slower chain, meaning you can only safely land 1 hit per window.

Items to Bring

  • Weltcaps (x4-6): Your basic healing consumable. Four is the minimum; six is comfortable.
  • Roasted Rat (x2): Emergency burst heal for Phase 2 mistakes.

Do not waste Tar on buying items specifically for this fight. The free Weltcaps scattered around Fallgrim are more than sufficient.

Phase 1: The Greatsword Duel (100%-50% HP)

Hadern's Phase 1 is deliberately simple. He uses only three attacks in a predictable rotation:

  1. Horizontal Slash (most common)
  2. Overhead Slam
  3. Thrust

He will always open the fight with a Horizontal Slash after a 2-second wind-up. This is your first Harden opportunity. The rhythm in Phase 1 is:

Observe → Harden → Break → Attack (2 hits) → Retreat → Repeat

In Phase 1, Hadern recovers slowly after each attack. You have approximately 1.5 seconds to land your hits before he resets to neutral. Two light attacks with the Hallowed Sword fit perfectly in this window.

Pro Tip: Do not dodge in Phase 1 unless your Harden is on cooldown. Hadern's entire Phase 1 moveset is designed to be Harden-countered. Dodging burns stamina you need for attacking.

Phase 2: Enraged Assault (Below 50% HP)

At 50% HP, Hadern slams his sword into the ground and lets out a roar. This is the Phase 2 transition. He gains one new attack: the 3-Hit Combo.

The 3-Hit Combo consists of:

  1. Slash — horizontal, same as Phase 1
  2. Slam — overhead, delayed by ~0.3s from the slash
  3. Spin — 360-degree spin with extended range

The critical change: his recovery time after single attacks is now faster. Where you had 1.5 seconds in Phase 1, you now have about 1 second. Drop your punish to a single light attack after single strikes, but you can still land 2 hits after the 3-Hit Combo's final spin (which has a longer recovery).

Warning: The spin attack in the 3-Hit Combo has deceptive range. Even if you dodged the slam, the spin can catch you if you moved to his side instead of behind him. Always dodge behind or directly backward.

Detailed Strategy

The Core Loop

Hadern is a rhythm fight. Once you internalize his tempo, he becomes almost mechanical:

  1. Stand at mid-range (about 3 sword lengths away)
  2. Wait for him to commit to an attack
  3. Harden as the swing connects
  4. Break free immediately and land your hits
  5. Walk backward to reset to mid-range
  6. Repeat

What NOT to Do

  • Do not attack first. Hadern will always out-trade you. Let him swing first.
  • Do not panic heal. His arena is small and he will chase you. Only heal during his recovery window after you punish.
  • Do not try to circle-strafe. His tracking on horizontal attacks is strong. Harden is the correct answer, not positioning.
  • Do not get greedy. One extra swing that gets punished costs you a heal. Two extra swings can cost you the run.

No-Hit Walkthrough

Hadern is the easiest boss in the game to no-hit because his attack pool is small and his timing is consistent. The strategy is identical to the normal strategy, just with zero margin for error:

  1. Phase 1: Harden every attack. Break and hit once (not twice, to maximize safety). Retreat. Repeat 8-10 cycles.
  2. Phase 2: For single attacks, Harden and hit once. For the 3-Hit Combo, Harden hit 1, immediately dodge backward for hits 2 and 3, then step in for one hit during recovery.
  3. Patience: A no-hit kill takes 3-5 minutes. Do not try to speed it up. The moment you rush, you get hit.
Expert Tip: For the Thrust attack, you can actually sidestep it without dodging if you're already moving laterally. This saves stamina and gives a slightly longer punish window since you're already beside him.

Rewards & What to Do Next

  • Tar: ~200 Tar
  • Glimpses: 1 Glimpse
  • Unlocks: Access to Fallgrim open world, Shell selection

After defeating Hadern, your immediate priority is to explore Fallgrim and find your preferred Shell. If you're following a specific build, head to the corresponding Shell statue. For general progression, we recommend heading toward the Crypt of the Damned next, as Crucix is the easiest of the three main path bosses.

For farming Tar to upgrade your weapon before the next boss, see our Tar Farming Guide.

FAQ

Can I skip Hadern?

No. Hadern is a mandatory boss that gates access to the rest of the game. However, once defeated, you never need to fight him again (unless you choose to via Remembrance).

What level should I be?

Hadern is designed to be fought at base level with starting equipment. No grinding is necessary.

I keep dying in Phase 2. What am I doing wrong?

The most common mistake is trying to punish Phase 2 the same way as Phase 1. His recovery window is shorter. Drop to single-hit punishes and focus on the 3-Hit Combo pattern, which has the longest recovery and is your safest damage window.
