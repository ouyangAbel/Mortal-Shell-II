Boss Overview

Tarsus guards the deepest chamber of the Frozen Sanctum and is widely considered the hardest mid-game boss. He wields a massive frost greatsword with sweeping area attacks, and his Phase 2 blizzard mechanic adds passive damage and movement slow to an already demanding fight.

Location: Frozen Sanctum Difficulty: 4/5 Rec. Level: 20-25 Weakness: Fire, charged heavy Best Shell: Eredrim

The arena is a large circular frozen chamber with an ice floor. There are no pillars or obstacles. The floor becomes more treacherous in Phase 2 as the blizzard covers it with additional frost patches that reduce traction. The arena has no boundaries that prevent you from kiting, but Tarsus's ranged attacks punish excessive distance.

Preparation & Recommended Loadout

Recommended Shell

Eredrim is the best Shell for this fight. His charged heavy attacks deal massive burst damage during Tarsus's brief vulnerability windows, and his high HP pool absorbs the inevitable blizzard tick damage. His ability to stagger Tarsus during clone spawns is invaluable. Harrow is the runner-up for raw survivability if you're still learning the patterns.

Recommended Weapon

Fire-enchanted weapon is mandatory. Tarsus takes 25% bonus fire damage, the highest elemental weakness of any mid-game boss. The Molten Blade or Ember's Staff are ideal. If neither is available, use any fire consumable on your best upgraded weapon. Without fire damage, the fight takes 40-50% longer and the blizzard phase becomes significantly harder.

Items to Bring

  • Weltcaps (x8): Budget for 4-5 heals in Phase 2 alone.
  • Roasted Rat (x4): Burst healing is critical during clone emergencies.
  • Frost Resist Salve (x2): Reduces blizzard tick damage by 50% for 90 seconds. Pop one at the Phase 2 transition and save one for below 30% HP.
  • Fire Salve (x2): Adds fire damage to your weapon for 60 seconds if you lack a natural fire enchantment.

Phase 1: The Frost Knight (100%-50% HP)

Tarsus's Phase 1 is a heavy-hitting but predictable knight fight. He uses four attacks in a learnable rotation.

Frost Greatsword Sweep

A wide 180-degree horizontal sweep with his massive sword. The frost trail extends the effective hitbox slightly beyond the blade. Response: Step back two paces or Harden through it. If you Harden, immediately counterattack during his 1.2-second recovery.

Ice Spear Volley

Tarsus raises his free hand and fires three homing ice spears in quick succession. Each spear tracks independently and deals moderate frost damage. Response: Strafe sideways at medium range. Do not roll — rolling has i-frames but the spears track through the roll recovery. Continuous strafing causes all three to miss. If one connects, it applies a brief slow effect.

Ice Burst

Tarsus slams his weapon into the ground, releasing a radial ice ring that expands outward. The ring covers the entire arena radius over ~1.5 seconds. Response: You must either Harden through the ring or jump-dodge at the right moment. After the burst, Tarsus is stationary for ~1.5 seconds — this is your primary punish window in Phase 1. Step in for 2-3 hits.

Frozen Embrace

Tarsus charges forward with arms open to grab you. If connected, he deals massive damage and inflicts frostbite. Response: Never stand directly in his charge path. Dodge sideways or, if you're confident, Harden the grab. The grab has a generous hitbox but poor tracking — lateral movement is the safest counter.

Core Rhythm: Tarsus's Phase 1 pattern is sweep → sweep → ice burst. The ice burst is always the recovery window. Learn this rhythm and Phase 1 becomes mechanical.

Phase 2: The Blizzard (Below 50% HP)

At 50% HP, Tarsus plunges his sword into the arena center and channels a massive blizzard. The entire arena is engulfed in swirling snow that deals minor tick damage (~2% HP per second) and applies a 15% movement slow. The blizzard persists for the rest of the fight.

New: Frost Clones

Tarsus creates two frost clones of himself that attack simultaneously. Each clone has 1 HP and can be destroyed with a single hit, but they deal full damage if their attacks connect. Clones spawn every ~20 seconds. Priority: Destroy clones immediately upon spawning. One hit each. Do not let them linger.

New: Burial Blizzard

Massive ice pillars erupt from the arena center in a spiral pattern, covering approximately 70% of the arena floor. The safe zone is at the edges. Response: Sprint to the arena perimeter the moment you see the ice forming beneath your feet. The pillars take ~1.5 seconds to erupt, giving you time to reach safety. While Tarsus channels this attack, he is stationary for ~2 seconds — circle behind him for free hits if you can reach him safely.

Clone Management Guide

Frost clones are the make-or-break mechanic of this fight. Poor clone management is the #1 reason players die in Phase 2.

Clone Behavior

  • Clones spawn in pairs, flanking Tarsus at ~5 meters distance
  • Each clone has 1 HP and dies to any damage source, including a single light attack
  • Clones mirror Tarsus's current attack with a 0.5-second delay
  • Clones cannot be staggered or Hardened through — they hit you during your Harden recovery
  • New clones spawn every ~20 seconds regardless of whether previous ones are alive

Optimal Response

  1. The moment you see the frost shimmer indicating clone spawn, identify the closest clone
  2. Sprint toward it and kill it with one hit — do not Harden or dodge, just attack
  3. Immediately turn and kill the second clone
  4. Resume attacking Tarsus during the remaining interval before next spawn

With Eredrim: His charged heavy attack can hit both clones and Tarsus simultaneously if they're clustered. Position yourself so Tarsus is between you and one clone, then unleash the charged attack.

Critical Warning: Never let both clones survive while Tarsus uses Burial Blizzard. The combined damage from two clone attacks plus the blizzard pillars is enough to instant-kill most Shells.

Detailed Strategy

Phase 1: Execute the Rhythm

Stay at mid-range. Wait for the sweep → sweep → ice burst pattern. Punish after ice burst with 2-3 fire-enhanced hits. When he uses Frozen Embrace, dodge sideways and punish the recovery. Phase 1 should take 2-3 minutes and cost 0-1 heals.

Phase 2: Clone Priority > Damage

The #1 rule of Phase 2: clones always take priority over DPS. Drop your attack on Tarsus immediately when clones spawn. Kill them. Resume. The blizzard tick damage is manageable with Frost Resist Salves — don't panic about the slow drain.

When Tarsus uses Burial Blizzard, run to the edge. After pillars erupt, sprint back to Tarsus for 2-3 hits during his channel recovery. This is actually his most vulnerable moment in Phase 2.

When to Use Eredrim's Charged Heavy

Save Eredrim's charged heavy for two moments:

  1. Post-Ice Burst: The 1.5-second window after Ice Burst is enough to land a fully charged heavy for massive damage.
  2. Clone Spawn: If clones cluster near Tarsus, a charged heavy can kill both clones and damage Tarsus in one swing.

No-Hit Walkthrough

Tarsus no-hit is one of the hardest challenges in the mid-game. Key requirements:

  1. Phase 1: Only punish after Ice Burst (safest window). Never attack after sweeps — occasionally he follows with a quick second sweep you can't react to.
  2. Clone Management: Kill clones the instant they appear. Develop a spawn-timing sense (every ~20 seconds) and pre-position.
  3. Burial Blizzard: Start moving to the edge the moment you see ice particles rising from the floor. The 1.5-second warning is tight.
  4. Ice Spears: Strafe at exactly medium range. Too close and the spears track too fast. Too far and they spread enough to catch strafing.
  5. Patience: A no-hit kill takes 8-12 minutes with fire damage. Accept the pace.

Rewards & What to Do Next

  • Tar: ~1,200 Tar
  • Glimpses: 4 Glimpses
  • Unlocks: Frozen Sanctum depths, Frost ability tier for Shell

FAQ

Do clones inherit Tarsus's elemental weakness?

No. Clones have 1 HP regardless of damage type. Any hit kills them. Save your fire damage for Tarsus himself.

Can I prevent the blizzard from starting?

No. The blizzard triggers at exactly 50% HP and cannot be interrupted. You can, however, burst Tarsus from above 50% to below 30% in a single combo with Eredrim's charged heavy + fire enchantment, skipping most of the clone phase.

Does the blizzard damage stack with frost attacks?

Yes. Being hit by a frost attack during the blizzard applies both the attack's frost damage and the blizzard's tick damage simultaneously. This is why Frost Resist Salves are highly recommended.
