Game Overview & World

Mortal Shell II is a dark fantasy action RPG developed by Cold Symmetry. Set in the decaying realm of Fallgrim, you play as a spirit-like adventurer who can inhabit different physical shells. This sequel expands dramatically on its predecessor — triple the map size, double the weapons, and a multi-branching narrative with alternate endings.

The core experience revolves around three words: Observe, Harden, Counter. Unlike Dark Souls where you can dodge-roll your way through everything, Mortal Shell II demands that you study enemy movements, use Harden at critical moments to absorb damage, then strike back during openings. If you're coming from traditional Souls games, you'll need to unlearn the roll-spam habit.

Tip: The first 2 hours are the hardest. Once Harden timing clicks, the difficulty drops noticeably. Stick with it — this game rewards persistence.

Core Combat System

Stamina — Your Most Precious Resource

Every action — attacking, dodging, hardening, sprinting — costs stamina. When stamina hits zero, you can do nothing. And in this game, doing nothing means death. Golden rule: always keep one bar of stamina for Harden or escape.

Attack Rhythm

Mortal Shell II's combat is slow and deliberate. Even light attacks have noticeable wind-up. This isn't input lag — it's a design choice. You're meant to judge mid-swing whether to commit or cancel into Harden.

The core combat loop: Close distance → attack → watch for enemy retaliation → Harden before impact → counterattack after Harden breaks → retreat to recover stamina → repeat. Master this cycle and you've mastered 80% of the combat.

Parrying

Some weapons (sword-and-shield types, certain straight swords) support parrying. Parries consume Guard Meter, not stamina. When Guard Meter depletes, you stagger. Treat parrying as a supplemental defense — Harden is your mainstay.

Harden — Everything You Need to Know

Harden is the most iconic combat mechanic in Mortal Shell. Understanding it fully is the difference between a player who dies 50 times per boss and one who clears the game with ease.

Harden Fundamentals

  • Invulnerability: While hardened, you are completely immune to all damage, knockback, and status effects. Enemies hit you like hitting a statue.
  • Anytime, anywhere: You can Harden during almost any action — standing idle, mid-attack animation, mid-roll, even after getting hit. The only exceptions are when you're staggered or knocked down.
  • Cooldown: Roughly 6 seconds (varies by Shell). The timer starts when Harden breaks.
  • Duration: The stone state lasts about 1.5 seconds. If an enemy hits you, it breaks automatically (but you still absorb that hit).
  • Mid-attack Harden: The most important technique. You begin an attack, see the enemy winding up to hit you, Harden mid-swing. The enemy bounces off your stone shell, your Harden breaks, and your attack continues — hitting them during their recovery.

Advanced Harden Techniques

Attack-cancel Harden: You can cancel any attack frame into Harden, then resume the strike afterward. This turns slow heavy weapons into deceptively dangerous tools — enemies can't safely counter you because you can Harden at any moment.

Harden Escape: Surrounded by multiple enemies? Harden immediately. Enemies hitting your stone body briefly recoil, giving you a second to reposition.

Harden Baiting: Approach as if to attack, then Harden right before the enemy swings. They waste their attack on your shell. Break Harden and punish during their recovery.

Watch out: Harden is not permanent invincibility. During the cooldown window, you are fully vulnerable. Bosses with rapid multi-hit combos can catch you between Hardens — don't over-rely on it.

Death Penalty & Soul Mechanics

Mortal Shell II uses the classic soulslike death system with a few unique twists:

What Happens on Death

  • You lose all unspent Souls (the game's XP and currency)
  • You keep all equipment, items, and unlocked skills
  • You respawn at the nearest Sanctuary
  • Your Remains are left at the death location — all lost Souls are there

Recovering Souls

Return to where you died and interact with your Remains. But be warned: if you die again before reaching them, those Souls are gone forever. The new death overwrites the old Remains location.

Soul Spending Priority

Spend Souls on Shell skill upgrades first. Any time you're carrying enough Souls for a level-up, head back to the Sanctuary and spend them. Do not explore new territory with a loaded Soul wallet — this is the #1 cause of beginner heartbreak.

Early Game Roadmap

Recommended Exploration Order

  1. Fallgrim Central: Learn basic combat, collect starter weapons and consumables
  2. Crypt of the Damned: First recommended dungeon — moderate enemies, solid straight sword available
  3. Return to Sanctuary to upgrade: Always spend Souls before entering a new zone
  4. Blazing Forge: Unlock weapon upgrades here ASAP
  5. Continue to later zones at recommended levels

Resource Management

  • Healing items: World Mushrooms are your most reliable early-game heal. Collect them and use in safe situations
  • Embers: The primary material for weapon upgrades. Rare early on — invest in a weapon you'll keep using
  • Chain Fragments: Used to unlock Shell skills. Prioritize survival skills (max HP, Harden cooldown reduction)

Common Beginner Mistakes

Mistake #1: Using Harden like a block button

Many newcomers hold Harden preemptively like a shield. Wrong. Harden is an active, cooldown-gated ability. Use it the moment an enemy is about to hit you, not before.

Mistake #2: Charging in blind

Every new area has traps and ambushes. Pause for 3 seconds before entering any new room. Check ceilings for hanging enemies, corners for lurking threats.

Mistake #3: Ignoring Shell skill trees

Shell upgrades impact survivability more than weapon upgrades do. Invest in survival skills (HP, Harden cooldown, stamina recovery) before damage skills.

Mistake #4: Locking into one Shell forever

You can switch Shells at any Sanctuary for free. Different bosses and areas favor different Shells — flexibility dramatically reduces difficulty.

Mistake #5: Skipping NPC dialogue

NPC questlines often unlock hidden weapons and Shells. Talk to every NPC until they repeat themselves. Some require revisiting at different story stages.

Iron rule of Souls spending: Whenever you're holding enough Souls for a level-up, return to the Sanctuary and spend them. Don't be greedy. This rule alone will save you hours of lost Souls.

FAQ

How long is Mortal Shell II?

Main story: ~20-25 hours. Full completion + all trophies: ~40-50 hours. Time varies with your skill level and exploration thoroughness.

Can I get every weapon in one playthrough?

Most, but 3 hidden weapons require special conditions. Focus on clearing the game in NG, then mop up collectibles in NG+.

Is there multiplayer?

Mortal Shell II is purely single-player. No co-op, no invasions. That means no summoning help — you beat every challenge yourself (or with this guide).

What are the PC requirements?

Recommended: GTX 1060 / RX 580 or better, 16 GB RAM. Minimum specs will run but not smoothly. If framerate drops, disable Volumetric Lighting and Screen Space Reflections in settings.
