Mortal Shell II Best Builds

Build System Overview

A Mortal Shell II build has three dimensions: Shell (base stats and passive abilities), Weapon (output style and combat range), and Skill Points (which Shell ability branches to invest in). All three must synergize for maximum effectiveness.

Below are 5 battle-tested builds. Each includes pros, cons, and best use scenarios.

Build 1: Immortal Bulwark — Tank

Tank★★★★★ BeginnerSurvivability: SDamage: BMobility: C
ComponentChoiceWhy
ShellHarrowHighest HP (150). The tankiest Shell in the game.
Main WeaponAbyssal Greatsword +108% lifesteal makes an already unkillable Shell even more unkillable.
SecondaryHollowed Sword +10Stamina regen for endurance fights. Solid backup.

Skill Priority

  1. Ironclad branch: Max first — +20% HP, physical damage reduction. This is non-negotiable.
  2. Tenacity branch: Reduce Harden CD to 5.5s — gives the tank more frequent defensive windows.
  3. Guardian branch: Brief invincibility after taking damage — lifesaver in clutch moments.
  4. Aegis branch: Optional. 10s invincibility is powerful but the 5-min cooldown limits practical value.

Playstyle

Core philosophy: "You can hit me ten times. I only need to hit you once." Use Harrow's massive HP pool to absorb damage, then heal back with Abyssal Greatsword's lifesteal. The longer Harden cooldown (8s → 5.5s) doesn't matter — your HP bar is your real defense.

Combat loop: Close distance → heavy attack → Harden the counter → heavy counterattack → retreat and regen → repeat. Getting hit barely registers. At full investment, you are functionally immortal against standard enemies.

Best Scenarios

Exploring new zones, first-time boss encounters, endurance fights (The Dark Father), NG+ steamrolling.

How it feels: This is the build for players who are tired of dying. It's not the fastest or flashiest, but it's the most reliable build in the game.

Build 2: Shadow Reaper — Assassin

Assassin★★ ExpertSurvivability: DDamage: SMobility: S
ComponentChoiceWhy
ShellTielFastest speed, highest stamina, 2.5s Harden CD (max skills).
Main WeaponShadow Daggers +10Fastest attack speed, backstab instant-kill, invisibility mobility.
SecondaryBerserker Twin Axes +10Swap for burst damage on bosses (stack to full combo).

Skill Priority

  1. Gale branch: Max first — Harden CD to 2.5s. The assassin's entire identity. Must be maxed immediately.
  2. Assassination branch: Backstab damage, double damage on first combat hit. Maximizes burst.
  3. Shadowstep branch: Consecutive roll stamina reduction. Seamless defense paired with 2.5s Harden.
  4. Tempest branch: Death Tempest excels in group fights. Worth the late-game investment.

Playstyle

The assassin plays nothing like the tank — you cannot get hit. At 80 HP, most boss attacks are one-shots. You must execute Harden timing and dodge rhythm perfectly.

Combat loop: High-frequency "attack → Harden → attack" cycling enabled by the 2.5s cooldown. Shadow Daggers' invisibility skill lets you disengage and reposition. On bosses, if you miss a Harden, use invisibility immediately and wait for recovery.

This build has the highest DPS in the game — Shadow Daggers' extreme speed + backstab bonuses + Tiel's base speed. But it's meant for players who've already cleared the game at least once.

Warning: The assassin is NOT a beginner build. If Hadern is still wrecking you, use the tank. The assassin is the ultimate build for players who can already no-hit bosses.

Build 3: Arcane Storm — Mage

Mage★★★ IntermediateSurvivability: CDamage: AMobility: B
ComponentChoiceWhy
ShellAzuranSpell damage specialist. Lowest staff stamina cost.
Main WeaponEmber's Staff +10High-damage homing fireballs + AoE explosion for clearing.
SecondaryFrostweaver Staff +10Frost bolts for slow + freeze. Dual-element coverage for all weaknesses.

Skill Priority

  1. Arcana branch: Spell damage up, spell stamina cost down. The foundation.
  2. Elements branch: Enchantment duration and effect boost. More damage from the safety of range.
  3. Meditation branch: Faster stamina recovery while stationary. Mages need mana regen speed.
  4. Arcanum branch: Arcane Storm — massive AoE. Ultimate crowd-clear.

Playstyle

The mage's core principle is distance — always attack from where enemies can't reach you. Ember's Staff homing fireballs let you damage while moving. For fast enemies, slow them with Frostweaver Staff first, then switch to fire for damage.

The biggest challenge is obtaining Azuran — hidden at the deepest point of the Abyssal Rift. Many players won't unlock her until NG+. Until then, Solomon makes a passable caster (high Resolve for fast skill unlocks).

Boss effectiveness: Exceptional against Tarsus (ice → fire) and Imrod (fire → ice). Struggles against Corvid (fast-moving) — slow her with Frostweaver Staff first.

Build 4: Bone Crusher — Power

Power★★★ IntermediateSurvivability: BDamage: SMobility: A
ComponentChoiceWhy
ShellEredrim+40% physical damage (max skills), high stamina for heavy chains.
Main WeaponSmoldering Mace +10Highest single-hit damage in the game + armor penetration. Pure brutality.
SecondaryMartyr's Blade +10Slightly faster alternative with AoE knockback for crowd control.

Skill Priority

  1. Might branch: Max — +40% physical damage. Every swing is a tactical nuke.
  2. Destruction branch: Stagger boost. More staggers = more damage windows.
  3. Fury branch: Damage boost at low HP. High risk, high reward.
  4. Giant's Strength: Fun skill — throw small enemies. Limited practical use.

Playstyle

Pure number porn. You don't need to attack often — every hit is devastating. Smoldering Mace at 340 base + Eredrim's 40% bonus = 470+ per swing. Standard enemies: one-shot. Elite enemies: 2-3 hits. Boss fights hinge on charged heavies — Eredrim's faster charge speed makes the Smoldering Mace deceptively fluid.

Mind your stamina: Eredrim has 140 stamina, but each Smoldering Mace heavy drains a huge chunk. Never chain 3 hits and bottom out. 2 hits → Harden → retreat to regen → repeat.

Exceptional against The Unchained and The Dark Father — high stagger interrupts their combos frequently.

Build 5: Sage Scholar — Balanced

Balanced★★★★ BeginnerSurvivability: ADamage: AMobility: B
ComponentChoiceWhy
ShellSolomonWell-rounded stats, highest Soul gain, fastest skill unlocks.
Main WeaponHallowed Sword +10Bonus undead damage + heal on kill. Balanced offense and sustain.
SecondaryObsidian Spear +10Safe distance option when you need to play conservatively.

Skill Priority

  1. Erudition branch: Max — +50% Soul gain. The balanced build levels faster than any other.
  2. Adaptation branch: Small all-stat boost + shortened status effects. Overall improvement.
  3. Insight branch: Extended parry window. Adds execution forgiveness.
  4. Legacy branch: Legacy of Knowledge — convert Resolve to damage. Boss burst tool.

Playstyle

The most versatile all-rounder. Not as tanky as Harrow, not as fast as Tiel, not as hard-hitting as Eredrim — but never bad in any scenario. Solomon's boosted Soul gain accelerates progression, and Hallowed Sword's broad stat profile covers most encounters.

If you're the kind of player who hates being locked into a specific playstyle, this is your build. The Hallowed Sword dominates in undead-heavy zones (Crypt, Abyssal Rift), and the Obsidian Spear covers everything else.

Also excellent for speedrunning and platinum collection — the Soul gain bonus speeds up skill unlocks, and the balanced stats mean you won't need to constantly switch Shells.
